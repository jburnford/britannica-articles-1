MESS

article · 448 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MESS, in a military sense, implies a number of soldiers, who, by laying away a certain proportion of their pay towards provisions, mets together: six or eight is generally the number of each mets. Experience proves, that nothing contributes more to the health of a soldier, than a regular and well chosen diet, and his being obliged every day to boil the pot: it corrects drunkenness, and in a great measure prevents gaming, and thereby deflection.
