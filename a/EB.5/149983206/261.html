METAL

Cross-references: METALLURGY

METAL, in natural history, a simple, ponderous, shining, fixed, opaque body, that fuses and becomes fluid by fire, and by cold coagulates and hardens into a solid mass, capable of being distended under the hammer. See METALLURGY, and CHEMISTRY.
