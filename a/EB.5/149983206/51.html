MAGALESIA

article · 455 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MAGALESIA, and MAGALESES LUDI, feasts and games in honour of Cybele or Rhea the mother of the gods, kept on the 12th of April by the Romans, and famous for great rejoicings and diversions of various sorts. The Galli carried the image of the goddess along the city, with found of drums and other music,

Megara music, in imitation of the noise they made to prevent Saturn from hearing the cries of his infant son Jupiter, when he was disposed to devour him.
