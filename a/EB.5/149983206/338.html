MIDDLEHAM

article · 519 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MIDDLELAM, a town in the north-riding of Yorkshire, situated on the river Ure, 255 miles from London. It had once a castle, where was born Edward prince of Wales, only son of Richard III.; and is noted for a woollen manufactory and frequent horse-races. Its market is on Monday; and fairs Nov. 6. and 7. The town stands on a rising ground; and the castle, which was on the south side, was formerly moated round by the help of a spring conveyed in pipes from the high grounds. The church of Middlelam is extra-parochial.
