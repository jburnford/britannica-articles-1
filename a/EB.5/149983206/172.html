MENUTHIAS

article · 487 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MENUTHIAS (anc. geog.), an island adjoining to the north-east of the promontory Pratum of Ethiopia beyond Egypt. Some take it to be Madagascar, or the island St Laurence. Isaac Vossius will have it to be Zanzibar; Madagascar being at a greater distance from the continent than the ancients ever sailed to, whereas Menuthias was nearer: yet though Zanzibar be nearer the continent, it is however nearer the equator than Ptolemy's Menuthias, placed in south latitude 12\frac{1}{2} degrees.
