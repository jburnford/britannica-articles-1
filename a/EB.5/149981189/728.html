FINESS

article · 393 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FINESS, a French term, of late current in English. Literally, it is of no farther import than our English fineness; but among us it is chiefly used to denote that peculiar delicacy or subtlety perceived in works of the mind, and the nicest and most secret and sublime parts of any science or art.

It is sometimes used to express that kind of subtlety made use of for the purposes of deception.
