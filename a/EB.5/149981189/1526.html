GAMES, in antiquity, were public diversions, exhibited on solemn occasions. Such among the Greeks, were the Olympic, Pythian, Isthmian, Nemean, &c. games; and, among the Romans, the Apollinarian, Circensian, Capitoline, &c. games. See OLYMPIC, PYTHIAN, FUNERAL, &c.
