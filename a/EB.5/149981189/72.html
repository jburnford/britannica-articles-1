EUPHONY

article · 401 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EUPHONY, in grammar, an easiness, smoothness, and elegance of pronunciation. The word is formed of eu, bene, "well," and phōn, vox, "voice." Quintilian calls euphonias, "vocalitas;" Scaglier, "facilis pronuntiatio."

Euphonias is properly a kind of figure whereby we suppress a too harsh letter, or convert it into a smoother, contrary to the ordinary rules. There are examples enough in all languages.
