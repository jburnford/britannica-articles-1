GLICAS

article · 366 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GLICAS, or GLYCAS, (Michael), a Greek historian about the middle of the 15th century, lived in Sicily, and wrote Annals of what passed from the creation of the world to the death of Alexis Comnenus, in 1118. Leunclavius added to it a fifth part, which carries it down to the taking of Constantinople. Glicas was also the author of several useful and curious letters.
