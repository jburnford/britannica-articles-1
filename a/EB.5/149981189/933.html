FOESIUS

FOESIUS (Anulus), a very learned and celebrated physician of the faculty of Paris, born at Mentz in 1528. He translated into Latin the whole works of Hippocrates, judiciously correcting the Greek text as he went along; and composed a kind of dictionary to him, intitled, Oeconomia Hippocratis. He translated, beside, the Commentaries of Galen upon the second book of Hippocrates; and was the author of some other works. After practising physic a long time with great success and reputation, at Lorraine and other places, he died in 1596.
