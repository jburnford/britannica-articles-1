EXCUSATI

article · 320 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EXCUSATI, in church history, a term used to denote slaves, who flying to any church for sanctuary, were excused and pardoned by their masters; but these were obliged to take an oath to that purpose before they could have them again; and, if they broke the oath, they were punished and fined as persons guilty of perjury.
