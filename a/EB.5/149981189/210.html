EXODUS, a canonical book of the Old Testament; being the second of the pentateuch, or five books of Moses.

It is so called from the Greek [exodos], the "going out" or departure of the children of Israel from the land of Egypt; the history of which is delivered in this book, together with the many miracles wrought on that occasion.
