GELD

article · 236 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GELD, in the English old customs, a Saxon word signifying money, or tribute. It also denoted a compensation for some crime committed: Hence ewergeld, in their ancient laws, was used for the value of a man slain; and orsgeld, of a beast.
