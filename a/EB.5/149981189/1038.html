FORGE

FORGE, in the train of artillery, is generally called a travelling forge, and may not be improperly called a portable smith's shop: at this forge all manner of smith's work is made, and it can be used upon a march as well as in camp. Formerly they were very ill contrived, with 2 wheels only, and wooden supporters to

prop the forge for working when in the park. Of late years they are made with 4 wheels, which answers their purpose much better.

FOXEN for red-hot Balls, is a place where the balls are made red-hot before they are fired off: it is built about five or six feet below the surface of the ground, of strong brick-work, and an iron grate, upon which the balls are laid, with a very large fire under them.
