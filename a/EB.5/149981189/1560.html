GARGARISM

article · 441 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GARGARISM (from gargare, "to wash the mouth;") a gargle. Its use is for washing the mouth and throat with, when inflammations, ulcerations, &c. are there. A small quantity may be taken into the mouth, and moved briskly about, and then spit out; or if the patient cannot do this to any advantage, the liquor may be injected by a syringe. When gargles are required, their use should be more frequently repeated than is done in common practice.
