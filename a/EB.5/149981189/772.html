FISHGARD

article · 478 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FISHGARD, or FISGARD, a town of Pembrokeshire, situated on a steep cliff on the sea-shore, 254 miles from London, at the influx of the river Gwaine into the sea, which here forms a spacious bay. It is governed by a mayor, a bailiff, and other officers; and here vessels may lie safely in five or six fathoms water. The inhabitants have a good trade in herrings, and annually cure, between Fiskard and Newport, above 1000 barrels of them. The town sends one member to parliament.
