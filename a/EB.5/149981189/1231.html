FRESH-WATER

article · 377 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FRESH-WATER, is that not tinctured or impregnated with salt or saline particles, enough to be discoverable by the sense. Such generally is that of springs, rains, wells, lakes, &c.

The dulcifying or making of salt water fresh is a secret that has been long sought with great attention. For an account of the principal attempts that have been made with this view, see Sea-Water.
