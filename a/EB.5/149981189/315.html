FABII

article · 498 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FABII, a noble and powerful family at Rome, who derived their name from fabia, a bean, because some of their ancestors cultivated this pulse. They were once so numerous that they took upon themselves to wage a war against the Veientes. They came to a general engagement near the Cremera, in which all the family, consisting of 306 men, were totally slain, year of Rome 277. There only remained one whose tender age had detained him at Rome, and from him arose the noble Fabii in the following ages.
