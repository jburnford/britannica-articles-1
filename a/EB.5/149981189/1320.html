FRUTEN, a shrub. Shrubs, according to Linnaeus, make a branch of the seventh family in the vegetable

getable kingdom; and are distinguished from trees, in that they come up without buds. But this distinction is not universal, though it be generally just with regard to those of Europe. Nature hath made no absolute distinction between trees and shrubs. Fruite, in its general acceptance, is a plant whose trunk is perennial, gemmiparous, woody, dividing and subdividing into a great number of branches. In short, it is the epitome of a tree, exemplified in the rose-bush.
