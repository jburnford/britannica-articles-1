GELENIUS

GELENIUS (Sigismund), a learned and excellent man, born of a good family at Prague, about the year 1498. Erasmus conceiving an esteem for him at Basil, recommended him to John Frobenius as a corrector for his printing-house; which laborious charge he accepted, and had a great number of Hebrew, Greek, and Latin books to correct: he also translated many works himself from the Greek into Latin; and published a dictionary in four languages, Greek, Latin, German, and Selavonian. Profitable and honourable employments were offered him in other places, but nothing could tempt him to quit his peaceful situation at Basil. He died in 1555. All his translations are highly esteemed.
