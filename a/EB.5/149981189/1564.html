GARIZIM

article · 428 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GARIZIM, GERIZIM, or Gerizim, (anc. geog.) a mountain of Samaria, at the foot of which stood Sichem; so near, that Jotham could be heard by the Sichemites from its top, (Judges ix. 7.) Famous for the temple built on it by Sanballat, in favour of his son-in-law Manasseh, by the permission of Alexander the Great, and 200 years after destroyed by John Hyrcanus, son of Simon, the fourth in succession of the Asmoneans (Josephus).
