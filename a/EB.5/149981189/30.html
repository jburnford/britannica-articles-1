EUDOXIANS

article · 375 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EUDOXIANS, a party or sect of heretics in the fourth century, so denominated from their leader Eudoxius, patriarch of Antioch and Constantinople, a great defender of the Arian doctrine. The Eudoxians adhered to the errors of the Arians and Eunomians, maintaining, that the Son was created out of nothing; that he had a will distinct and different from that of the Father, &c.
