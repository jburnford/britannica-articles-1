FASHION-PIECES

article · 291 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FASHION-PIECES, in the sea-language, the aismost or hindmost timbers of a ship, which terminate the breadth, and form the shape of the stern. They are united to the stern-post, and to the extremity of the

wing-transom, by a rabbit, and a number of strong nails or spikes driven from without.
