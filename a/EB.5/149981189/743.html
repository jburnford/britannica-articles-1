FIRE-Ball

Cross-references: FIRE-BALLS

FIRE-Ball, in artillery, a composition of meal-powder, sulphur, salt-petre, pitch, &c. about the bigness of a hand-grenade, coated over with flax, and primed with the slow composition of a fuze. This is to be thrown into the enemy's works in the night-time, to discover where they are; or to fire houses, galleries, or blinds of the besiegers; but they are then armed with spikes or hooks of iron, that they may not roll off, but stick or hang where they are desired to have any effect. See FIRE-BALLS, and LIGHT-BALLS.
