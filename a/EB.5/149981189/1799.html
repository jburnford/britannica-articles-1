GIBBET, or GIBET, a machine in manner of a gallows, wherein notorious criminals, after execution, are hung in irons or chains, as spectacles in terrorem. See GALLOWWS.—The word in French, gibet, properly denotes what we call gallows: it is supposed to come originally from the Arabic gibel, "mount or elevation of ground;" by reason gibets are usually placed on hills or eminences.
