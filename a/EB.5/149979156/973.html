CONGE

article · 317 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CONGE, in the French law, a licence, or permission, granted by a superior to an inferior, which gives him a dispensation from some duty to which he was before obliged. A woman cannot obligate herself without the conge or licence of her husband; a monk cannot go out of his convent, without the conge of his superiors.
