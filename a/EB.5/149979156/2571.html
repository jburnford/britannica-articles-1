DERIVATIVE

article · 314 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DERIVATIVE, in grammar, a word which takes its origin from another word, called its primitive.—Such is the word derivative itself, which takes its origin from the primitive rivus, a rivulet or channel, out of which lesser streams are drawn; and thus manhood, deity, lawyer, &c. are derived from man, deus, law, &c.
