CITIUM

article · 406 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CITIUM, CERIUM, or Cittium (anc. geog.), a town of Cyprus, situated in the south of the island; famous for the birth of Zeno, author of the sect called Stoics; distant two hundred stadia to the west of Salamis (Diodorus Siculus). A colony of Phœnicians, called Chetim: And hence it is that not only Cyprus, but the other islands and many maritime places, are called Chetim by the Hebrews; now called Chiti.
