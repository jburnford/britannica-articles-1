CORVUS || Corybantēs.
for the martins, as soon as the persons who set them turn their backs. They lay up stores for winter; at which time they are seldom seen unless near habitations. They do not bear confinement well. What natural note they have, we are not told; but they are said to act the mocking bird, in imitating that of others.—There are near 30 other species.
