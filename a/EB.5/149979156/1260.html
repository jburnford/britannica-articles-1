CORCYRA (anc. geog.), an island in the Ionian Sea, opposite to Thessprotia, a district of Epirus, called Scheria and Phæcia by Homer. In Callimachus it is called Drepane; its most ancient name, according to the Scholiast, from the curvity of its figure. Famous for the shipwreck of Ulysses and the gardens of Alcinoos. Now Corfu.
