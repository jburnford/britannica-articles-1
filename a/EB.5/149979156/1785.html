CRONIUS

article · 313 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CRONIUS, in chronology, the ancient name of the Athenian month Hecatombæon; which was the first of their year, and answered to the latter part of our June and beginning of July.—There were feasts called Cronienses celebrated at Athens in this month, in honour of Saturn, answering to the Saturnalia of the Romans.
