CONFUTATION

article · 396 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CONFUTATION, in rhetoric, &c. a part of an oration, wherein the orator seconds his own arguments and strengthens his cause, by refuting and destroying the opposite arguments of the antagonist. This is done by denying what is apparently false, by detecting some flaw in the reasoning of the adverse party, by granting their argument, and showing its invalidity, or retorting it upon the adversary.
