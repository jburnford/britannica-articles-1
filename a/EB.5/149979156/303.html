CLESIDES

article · 358 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CLESIDES, a Greek painter, about 276 years before Christ, in the reign of Antiochus I. He revenged the injuries he had received from queen Stratonice by representing her in the arms of a fisherman. However indecent the painter might represent the queen, she was drawn with such personal beauty, that she preferred the piece and liberally rewarded the artist.
