CRAMOND, OVER and NETHER

article · 515 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CRAMOND, OVER and NETHER, two villages about four miles west of Edinburgh; of which only the last deserves notice, as having been once a famous naval station of the Romans. It is situated at the influx of the river Almon into the Forth. Three Roman roads meet at this place, which was called by them Alateros, and whither they brought their grain for the support of their troops. The village contains about 300 inhabitants. Here are the remains of a bath and sudatory; and many altars, medals, &c. have been dug up.
