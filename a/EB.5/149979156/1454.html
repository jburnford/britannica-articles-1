COSENAGE

article · 325 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COSENAGE, in law, a writ that lies where the tressail, that is, the tritavus, the father of the befall, or great grandfather, being seized in fee at his death of

certain lands or tenements, dies; a stranger enters, and abates; then shall his heir have this writ of cosenage; the form of which see in Fitzh. Nat. Br. fol. 221.
