CONTINUED Bass

sub_entry · 369 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CONTINUED Bass, in music, thus called, says Rouffeau, because it is continued through the whole piece. Its principal use, besides that of regulating the harmony, is to support the voice and preserve the tone. They pretend that it was one Ludovico Viana, of whom a treatise still remains, who towards the end of the last century first put the continued bass in practice.
