CRISPIN and CRISTIANUS

article · 566 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CRISPIN and CRISTIANUS, two legendary saints, whose festival, as marked in the calendar, is on the 25th of October. According to the legend, they were brethren, born at Rome; from whence they travelled to Soissons in France, about the year 303, to propagate the Christian religion; and because they would not be chargeable to others for their maintenance, they exercised the trade of shoemakers; but the governor of the town discovering them to be Christians, ordered them to be beheaded. From which time the shoemakers made choice of them for their tutelary saints.
