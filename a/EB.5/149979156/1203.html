COPE

article · 248 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COPE, an ecclesiastical ornament, usually worn by chanters and subchanters, when they officiate in solemnity. It reaches from the shoulders to the feet. The ancients called it pluviale.—The word is also used for the roof or covering of a house, &c.
