DAN

article · 734 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DAN, or JOR-DAN, which last literally denotes "the river Dan;" so named from the people where it has its source, which is a lake called Phiala, from its round figure, to the north of its apparent rising from the mountain Panum or Paneum, as was discovered by Philip, Tetrarch of Trachonites; for on throwing light bodies into the Phiala, he found them to emerge again at Paneum (Josephus). From Paneum it runs in a direct course to a lake called Samachonites; as far as which it is called Jordan the Left; and thence to the lake Genesareth, or of Tiberias, where it comes increased by the lake Samachonitis and its springs, and is called the Greater Jordan; continuing its direct course southwards, till it falls into the Asphaltites.
