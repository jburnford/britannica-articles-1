CUSHION

article · 353 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CUSHION, in gilding, is made of leather, fastened to a square board, from 14 inches square to 10, with a handle. The vacancy between the leather and board is stuffed with fine tow or wool, so that the outer surface may be flat and even. It is used for receiving the leaves of gold from the paper, in order to its being cut into proper sizes and figures.
