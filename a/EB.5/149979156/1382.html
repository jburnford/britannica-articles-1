CORPOREITY

article · 468 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CORPOREITY, the quality of that which is corporeal, or his body; or that which constitutes or denominates it such.—The corporeity of God was the capital error of the Anthropomorphites. Some authors reproach Tertullian with admitting a corporeity in the Deity; but it is manifest, by body he means no more than substance.—The Mahometans reproach the Samaritans at this day, with a belief of the corporeity of God. Many of the ancients believed the corporeity of angels.
