CREODIBA

article · 264 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CREODIBA, in the customs of the middle age, a robbery and murder committed in a wood, where the body of the person killed was burnt in order to prevent any discovery of the crime. The word, says Wendelinus, is compounded of cray and disen, that is, "wood-robbers."
