COCINTUM (anc. geog.), a promontory of the Brutii, reckoned the longest in Italy: and which

Hollenius and Vossius have restored to Ovid, reading Cocintia for Ceurania, Metam. XV. v. 704.—Cocintum, also a town, 22 miles to the south of Scylaceum, almost on the spot where now Stilo stands; from which the opposite promontory Cocintum is commonly called Gapo de Stilo.
