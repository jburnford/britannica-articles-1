COMMONTY, in Scots law, sometimes signifies lands belonging to two or more common proprietors; sometimes a heath or muir though it should be long

long in property to one, if there has been a promiscuous possession upon it by pasturage; and the act 1695, mentions commonties belonging in property to the king and to royal boroughs. See LAW, Part III. No clxxv. 16.
