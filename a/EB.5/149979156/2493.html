DEMOCRACY, from Democ people, and crati to command or govern; the same with a popular government, wherein the supreme power is lodged in the hands of the people: such were Rome and Athens of old; but as to our modern republics, Basil only excepted, their government comes nearer to aristocracy than democracy. See LAW, n° 14.
