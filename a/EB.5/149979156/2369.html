DECUMATES AGRII

article · 384 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DECUMATES AGRII, tithed fields, or granted on a tithe, as appears from Tacitus, to that rabble of Gauls who succeeded the Marcomanni, that had till then proved a check to the Roman conquests up the Rhine; and hence probably their name, people living on the marches or limits of the empire. In Cicero we have Ager Decumans, which is of the same import with the Ager Decumus of Tacitus.
