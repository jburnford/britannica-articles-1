Fossil-CORK

sub_entry · 327 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Fossil-CORK, a name given to a kind of stone. It seems to be a species of amianthus, consisting of flexible

ble fibres loosely interwoven, and somewhat resembling vegetable cork. It is the lightest of all stones; by fire it is fusible, and forms a black glass. It possesses the general qualities of amianthus. See that article.
