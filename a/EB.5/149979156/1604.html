COUTHUTLAUGH

article · 239 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COUTHULLAUGH, from the Saxon couth, "knowing" and ulaugh, "outlaw;" he that wittingly receives a man outlawed, and cherishes or conceals him: for which offence he was in ancient times subject to the same punishment with the outlaw himself.
