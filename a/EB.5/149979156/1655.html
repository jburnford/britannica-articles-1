CRASIS

article · 261 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CRASIS, in grammar, is a figure whereby two different letters are either contracted into one long letter or a diphthong. Such, e. g. is αβ for αββ; αββ for αβββ, &c. ταυτε for ταυτετε, &c. where α and α are contracted into α; ε and α into ε; and ε and α into ε.
