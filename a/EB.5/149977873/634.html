BEMA

article · 246 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BEMA, in antiquity, denotes a step or pace. The bema made a kind of itinerary measure among the Greeks, the length of which was equivalent to one cubit and two thirds, or to ten palms. Whence also the term bematizein, βημαζειν, to measure a road.
