BOUTÉ

article · 206 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BOUTÉ, in the manège. A horse is called bouté, when his legs are in a straight line from the knee to the coronet: short-jointed horses are apt to be bouté, and on the other hand long-jointed horses are not.
