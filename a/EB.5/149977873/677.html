BENEVENTUM

article · 425 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BENEVENTUM, (anc. geog.), a town of the Samnites, formerly called Maleventum from the unwholesomeness of the wind, and under that appellation it is mentioned by Livy; but after a Roman colony was led thither in the 485th year of the city, it came to have the name of Beneventum, as a more auspicious title. It is mentioned by Horace as an ancient city said to have been built by Diomedes before the Trojan war. Now BENEVENTO.
