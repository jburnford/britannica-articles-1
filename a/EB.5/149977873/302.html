BATTEN

BATTEN, a name that workmen give to a scantling of wooden stuff, from two to four inches broad, and about one inch thick; the length is pretty considerable, but undetermined.—This term is chiefly used in speaking of doors and windows of shops, &c. which are not framed of whole deal, &c. with stiles, rails, and panes like wainscot; but are made to appear as if they were

tack.

Battenburg were by means of these battens bradded on the plain Battering-board round the edges, and sometimes cross them, and up and down.
