BROADCAST

article · 434 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: AGRICULTURE

BROADCAST, as opposed to the drill husbandry, denotes the method of cultivating corn, turnips, pulse, clover, the foreign grasses, and most other field-plants, that are not transplanted by sowing them with the hand; in which method they are scattered over the ground at large, and thence said to be sown in broadcast. This is called the old husbandry, to distinguish it from the drill, horse-hoeing, or new husbandry. See AGRICULTURE.
