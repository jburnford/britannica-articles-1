BERME

article · 378 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BERME, in fortification, a space of ground left at the foot of the rampart, on the side next the country, designed to receive the ruins of the rampart, and prevent their falling up the fosse. It is sometimes palisaded, for the more security; and in Holland it is generally planted with a quick-set hedge. It is also called lisiere, relais, foreland, retrait, pais de fouris, &c.
