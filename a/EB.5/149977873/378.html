BEADS

article · 367 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BEADS are also used in speaking of those glais globules vended to the savages on the coast of Africa; thus denominated, because they are strung together for the convenience of traffic.

The common black glais of which beads are made for necklaces, &c. is coloured with manganese only: one part of manganese is sufficient to give a black colour to near twenty of glais.
