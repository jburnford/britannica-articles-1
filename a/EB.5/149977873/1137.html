BLACK-WHYTOS

article · 291 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BLACK-WHYTOS, in our old writers, bread of a middle fineness betwixt white and brown, called in some parts ravel-bread. In religious houses, it was the bread made for ordinary guests, and distinguished from their household loaf, or panis conventualis, which was pure manchet, or white bread.
