Gold-BEATERS

sub_entry · 661 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: FOLIATING

Gold-BEATERS, are artisans, who, by beating gold and silver with a hammer on a marble in moulds of velum and bullocks guts, reduce them to thin leaves fit for gilding, or silvering of copper, iron, steel, wood, &c.

&c. Gold-beaters differ from flatters of gold or silver; as the former bring their metal into leaves by the hammer, whereas the latter only flatten it by pressing it through a mill preparatory to beating.

There are also Tin-Beaters employed in the looking-glass trade, whose business is to beat tin on large blocks of marble till it be reduced to thin leaves fit to be applied with quicksilver behind looking-glasses. See FOLIATING, GOLD-Beating.
