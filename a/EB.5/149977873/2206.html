BRYUM, in botany: A genus of the 56th natural order, viz. Musci, belonging to the cryptogamia class of plants. The anthera is operculated or covered with a lid, the calyptra polished; and there is a filament arising from

from the terminal tubercle. There are 41 species, most of them natives of Britain.
