BONONIA

BONONIA, (anc. geog.), a town of Gallia Belgica, supposed to be the Portus Iecius of Caesar, and the Gessoriacum of Mela, and to have had three different names, (Cluverius). Peutingers' map expressly calls Gessoriacum Bononia. Now Bologne. E. Long. 1. 30. Lat. 50. 40.
