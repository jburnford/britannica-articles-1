BURGUNDY

BURGUNDY, a province or government of France. It contains, besides the government of Burgundy, La Breffe, La Bugy, and the district of Gex; having Champagne on the north, Lyonnais on the south, Franche Comte on the east, and Nivernois and Bourbonnois on the west. Its length from north to south is about 45 leagues, and its breadth from east to west about 30. It is very fertile in corn, wine, fruit, and tobacco; being watered by the Seine, the Delune which falls into the Soane, the Brebince or Bourbonne, the Armançon, the Ouche, and the Tille. There are some noted mineral springs in it, with subterranean lakes, and plenty of ochre. For a long time it had dukes of its own, subordinate to the crown of France; but at last, Louis XI. upon the failure of the heirs male, seized upon it, and annexed it to his crown. The whole government lies within the jurisdiction of the parliament of Burgundy, except a small part that is subject to that of Paris. The states meet regularly every three years, to raise the money required of them by the court. The principal places are Dijon, Auxerre, Autun, Bourbon, L'Aney, &c.
