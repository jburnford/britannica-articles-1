BUCELLARI

article · 498 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BUCELLARI, an order of soldiery under the Greek emperors, appointed to guard and distribute the ammunition bread; though authors are somewhat divided as to their office and quality. Among the Visigoth, bucellarius was a general name for a client or vassal who lived at the expense of his lord. Some give the denomination to parasites in the courts of princes, some make them the body-guards of emperors, and some fancy they were only such as emperors employed in putting persons to death privately.
