BOTALLUS

BOTALLUS (Leonard), physician to the duke of Alençon, and to Henry III. was born at Asli in Piedmont. He introduced at Paris the practice of frequent letting of blood; which was condemned by the faculty; but soon after his death it came into practice with all the physicians. He published several books in physic and surgery; and the best edition of his works is that of Leyden in 1660, octavo.
