BOIOBI

article · 531 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BOIOBI, in zoology, the name of a species of serpent found in America, and called by the Portuguese cobra de verh. It is about an ell in length, of the thickness of a man's thumb, and is all over of a very beautiful and shining green. Its mouth is very large, and its tongue black. It loves to be about houses, and never injures any creature unless provoked or hurt; but it will then bite, and its poison is very fatal. The natives take as a remedy against its poison, the root caa apia bruised, and mixed with water. See Caa Apia.
