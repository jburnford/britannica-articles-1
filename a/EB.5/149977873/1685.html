BOURDALOUE

article · 557 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BOURDALOUE (Lewis), a celebrated preacher among the Jesuits, and one of the greatest orators France has produced, was born at Bourges, on the 20th of August 1632. After having preached at Provence, he, in 1699, went to Paris; and there met with such applause, that the king resolved to hear him: on which he was sent for to court, and frequently preached before Louis XIV. He afflicted the sick, visited the prisoners and hospitals, and was very liberal in giving alms. He died at Paris on the 13th of May 1704. The best edition of his sermons is in octavo.
