ANTITYPE

article · 382 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANTITYPE, among the ancient Greek fathers, and in the Greek liturgy, is also applied to the symbols of bread and wine in the sacrament. Hence it hath been argued, by many Protestants, that the Greeks do not really believe the doctrine of transubstantiation; because they call the bread and wine antitypes, αντιτύποι, q. d. figures, similitudes; and this even after the consecration.
