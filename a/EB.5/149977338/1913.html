BADIGEON

article · 364 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BADIGEON, a mixture of plaster and free-stone, well ground together, and sifted; used by statuary to fill up the little holes, and repair the defects in stones, whereof they make their statues and other work.

The same term is also used by joiners for saw-dust mixed with strong glue, wherewith they fill up the chaps and other defects in wood, after it is wrought.
