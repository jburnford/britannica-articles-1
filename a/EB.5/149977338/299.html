ANTIOPÉ

article · 294 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANTIOPÉ, in fabulous history, the wife of Lycus, king of Thebes, who, being deflowered by Jupiter in the form of a satyr, brought forth Amphion and Zethus.—Another Antiope was queen of the Amazons; and, with the assistance of the Scythians, invaded the Athenians; but was vanquished by Theseus.
