BACCHI, in mechanics, a kind of ancient machines, in form of goats, used by Jupiter, in his wars against the giants. Rudbeck describes two kinds of bacchi, one made like the battering-ram, wherewith Jupiter demolished the enemy's fortifications; the other contrived to cast fire out of, from whence the Greeks are conjectured to have framed their idea of chimera.
