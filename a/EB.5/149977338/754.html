ARCHELAUS

article · 519 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ARCHELAUS, the son of Apollonius, one of the greatest sculptors of antiquity, was a native of Ionia, and is thought to have lived in the time of the emperor Claudius. He executed, in marble, the apotheosis of Homer. This masterpiece in sculpture was found in 1568, in a place named Frateschia, belonging to the princes of Colonna, where, it is said, the emperor Claudius had a pleasure-house. Father Kircher, Cuper, Spanheim, and several other learned antiquaries, have given a description and explication of this work.
