BARBA

article · 440 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BARBA, in botany, a species of pubes, or down, with which the surface of some plants is covered. The term was invented by Linnaeus; and by its application in the Species Plantarum, seems to signify a tuft or bunch of strong hairs terminating the leaves. Mesembryanthemum barbatum, furnishes an example.

The word is also often used in composition with some other, to form the trivial names of several plants, as barba jovis, barba capra, &c.
