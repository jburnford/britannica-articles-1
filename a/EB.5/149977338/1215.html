ASEKI

article · 533 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ASEKI, or ASEKAI, the name which the Turks give to the favourite sultanesse who have brought forth sons. These are greatly distinguished above others in their apartments, attendants, pensions, and honours. They have sometimes shared the government. The sultana who first presents the emperor with a male child, is reckoned the chief favourite, is called buyuk aseki, and ranks as a legitimate wife: though, from the time of Bajazet I. the sultans are forbid to marry by a public law, which Solyman II. violated in favour of Roxalana.
