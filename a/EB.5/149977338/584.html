APPRISING

article · 709 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

APPRISING, in Scots law, the name of that action by which a creditor formerly carried off the estate of his debtor for payment. It is now abolished, and adjudications are appointed in place of it.—Adjudications, charter, resignation, clare constat, cognition of heirs, heritable right, confirmation, novodamus, principal and original instrument of surrender, retour, seisin, and service, in Scotland, pay by different acts 4s. 9 d. duty.

Approach APPROACH, or APPROACHING, in a general sense, the acceding or coming together of two or more things.

Appropration. APPROACHES, in fortification, the works thrown up by the besiegers, in order to get nearer a fortress, without being exposed to the enemy's cannon.
