ANTIQUARE

article · 339 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANTIQUARE, among Roman lawyers, properly denotes the rejecting of a new law, or refusing to pass it. In which sense, antiquating differs from abrogating; as the latter imports the annulling an old law, the former the rejecting a new one.

ANTIQUARE is also used for a law's growing obsolete, or into disuse, either by age or non-observance.
