AVALANCHES, a name given to prodigious snow-balls that frequently roll down the mountains in Savoy.

Savoy, particularly Mount Blanc, to the extreme danger of such adventurous travellers as attempt to ascend those stupendous heights. Some of the avalanches are about 200 feet diameter; being fragments of the ice-rocks which break by their own weight from the tops of the precipices. See Mount Blanc.
