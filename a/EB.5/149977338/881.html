ARGOS Hippium

ARGOS Hippium, the ancient name of Arpi; but Lampe is a ſtill more ancient; afterwards called Argyrippa, and Argippa; built by, and the reſidence of, Diomedes, on the Cerbalus, (Virgil); afterwards a large and populous city, (Livy): A town of Apulia; now in ruins, and the place called Arpi.

ARGOS Pelaſgium, (Homer); an appellation denoting Theſſaly; ſo called from the Pelaſgi.
