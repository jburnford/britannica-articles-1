BANISTER

BANISTER, (John), a physician and surgeon in the reign of Queen Elizabeth, was educated at Oxford, where, says Anthony Wood, he studied logics for a time; but afterwards applied himself solely to physic and surgery. In 1573, he took the degree of bachelor of physic; and, obtaining a licence from the university to practise, settled at Nottingham, where he lived many years in great repute, and wrote several medical treatises. His works were collected and published in 1633, 4to.
