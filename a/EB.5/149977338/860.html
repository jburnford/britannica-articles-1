ARGENTORA

ARGENTORA, Argentina, (Notitiae); Argentoratum, (Ptolemy); Argentoratus, (Ammian); a city of the Tribocci; one of the fifty forts built by Drusus on the Rhine, (Florus): an appellation formed by the Romans from the German, Argen Straffen, or Straten, "unsafe roads for travellers," from the maroding parties of the garriſons that infested the roads. Now See Straßburg*, in the lower Alsace, on the rivulet Ill, near the Rhine. E. Long. 7. 35. Lat. 48. 38.
