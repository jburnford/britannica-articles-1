BAG

article · 324 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BAG, among farriers, is when, in order to retrieve a horse's lost appetite, they put in an ounce of asafoetida, and as much powder of savin, into a bag, to be tied to the bit, keeping him bridled for two hours several times a day: as soon as the bag is taken off, he will fall to eating. The same bag will serve a long time.
