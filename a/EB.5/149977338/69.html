ANIO

article · 745 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANIO, (Cicero, Horace, Priscian); ANION, (Statius); now il Tevere; a river of Italy, which falls into the Tiber, three miles to the north of Rome, not far from Antennæ. It rises in a mountain near Treba, (Pliny); and, running through the country of the Æquicoli, or Æqui, it afterwards separated the Latins from the Sabines; but nearer its mouth, or confluence, it had the Sabines on each side. It forms three beautiful lakes in its course, (Pliny). In the territories of Tibur it falls from a great height, and there forms a very rapid cataract: hence the epithet præcips, and hence the steam caused by its fall, (Horace). Anienus is the epithet formed from it, (Virgil, Propertius): Anienus is also the god of the river, (Propertius, Statius).
