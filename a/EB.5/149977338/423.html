APHÆRESIS

article · 250 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

APHÆRESIS, in grammar, a figure by which a letter or syllable is cut off from the beginning of a word. Thus eiconis by aphæresis, is written conia; contemnere, temnere; emitere, mittere, &c.

A like retrenchment at the end of a word is called APOCOPÆ.
