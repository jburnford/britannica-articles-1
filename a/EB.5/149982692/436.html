LORDOSIS

Cross-references: SURGERY

LORDOSIS, (of lodos, bent inwards), in the medical writings, a name given to a distempered state of the spine, in which it is bent inwards, or toward the anterior parts. It is used in opposition to gibbous, or bump-backed. See SURGERY.
