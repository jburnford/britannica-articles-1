LUBIN

LUBIN (Eilhard), was professor of poetry in the university of Rosstock in 1595; and ten years after, was promoted to the professorship of divinity. He wrote notes on Anacreon, Juvenal, Persius, &c. and several other works; but that which made the most noise is a Treatise on the nature and origin of evil, intitled, Phosphorus de causa prima et natura mali, printed at Rosstock in 1596; in which we have a curious hypothesis to account for the origin of moral evil.
