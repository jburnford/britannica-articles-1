MARKHAM

article · 490 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MARKHAM (Gervase), an English author, was the son of Robert Markham of Gotham, Esq; in Nottinghamshire, and bore a captain's commission under Charles I. in the civil wars. He was esteemed both a good soldier and a good scholar. He was particularly master of the French, Italian, and Spanish. He wrote, 1. The tragedy of Herod and Antipater, which was printed in 1622. 2. Many volumes upon husbandry and horsemanship. 3. A piece on the art of fowling. 4. The soldiers' accidence and grammar.
