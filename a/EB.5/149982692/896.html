MALT-TAX

article · 543 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MALT-TAX, is the sum of 750,000l. raised every year by parliament since 1697, by a duty of 6d. on the bushel of malt, and a proportionable sum on certain liquors, such as cyder and perry, which might

otherwise prevent the consumption of malt. This is under the management of the commissioners of the excise; and is indeed itself no other than the annual excise. In 1760, an additional perpetual excise of 3d. per bushel was laid upon malt; and in 1763, a proportional excise was laid upon cyder and perry, but new-modelled in 1766. See Excise.
