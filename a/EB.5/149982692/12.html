LETTERS Patent or Overt

Cross-references: PATENT

LETTERS Patent or Overt, are writings sealed with the great seal of England, whereby a man is authorized to do, or enjoy any thing, which, of himself, he could not do. See PATENT.—They are so called, by reason of their form; as being open, with the seal affixed, ready to be shown for the confirmation of the authority given by them.
