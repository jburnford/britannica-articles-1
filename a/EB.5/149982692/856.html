MALACA (anc. geog.), furnished Federatorum by Pliny; a maritime town of Bætica: A Carthaginian colony according to Strabo; so called from Malach, signifying "salt;" a place noted for pickled or salted meat. Now Malaga, a port town of Granada in Spain. W. Long. 4. 45. N. Lat. 36. 40.
