LYMPHÆA

article · 288 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LYMPHÆA, were artificial caves or grottos a-
mongst the Romans, furnished with a great many
tubes, canals, and various hydraulic apparatus, thro'
which the water gushed out upon the spectators un-
expectedly whilst they were admiring the beautiful
arrangement of the shell-work in the grotto.
