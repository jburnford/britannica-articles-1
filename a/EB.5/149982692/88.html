LIBERA

article · 289 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LIBERA, in mythology, the name of a goddess, which Cicero, in his book Of the Gods, represents as the daughter of Jupiter and Ceres. Ovid in his Faustus says that the name was given by Bacchus to Ariadne.

Libera is exhibited on medals as a kind of female Bacchus, crowned with vine leaves.
