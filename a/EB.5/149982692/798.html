MAGNUS CAMPUS

article · 406 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MAGNUS CAMPUS, (anc. geog.), a tract lying towards Scythopolis, or Bethsan in Galilee, beyond which it extends into Samaria; Josephus placing the common boundary between these two districts, in the Campus Magnus. Called also Ejdrelon, (Judith); 30 miles long, and 18 broad; having Samaria with mount Ephraim to the south, the lake Genesareth to the east; mount Carmel to the west, and Lebanon to the north.
