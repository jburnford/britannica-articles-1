MANUDUCTOR

article · 456 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MANUDUCTOR, a name given to an ancient officer in the church; who, from the middle of the choir, where he was placed, gave the signal for the choiristers to sing, and marked the measure, beat time, and regulated the music. The Greeks called him mesachoros, because seated in the middle of the choir: but in the Latin church he was called manuductor; from manus and duco, "I lead;" because he led and guided the choir by the motions and gesture of the hand.
