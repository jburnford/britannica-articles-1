Fossile LINEN

sub_entry · 297 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: AMIANTHUS AND ASBESTOS

Fossile LINEN, is a kind of amianthus, which consists of flexible, parallel, soft fibres, and which has been celebrated for the uses to which it has been applied, of being woven, and forming an incombustible cloth. Paper also, and wicks for lamps, have been made of it. See AMIANTHUS AND ASBESTOS.
