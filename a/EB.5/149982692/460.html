LOUGH-DERG

article · 468 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LOUGH-DERG, anciently Dergabhan, i. e. "the river of the woody moras," from a river which issues out of this lake. This lough is situated in the county of Donegal and province of Ulster in Ireland, and is famous for having in it the island that contains St Patrick's purgatory, which is a narrow little cell, hewn out of the solid rock, in which a man could scarce stand upright.—There is also a lake of this name situated between the counties of Galway and Tipperary.
