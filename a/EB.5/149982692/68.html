LEXINGTON

article · 338 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LEXINGTON, a town of North America, and capital of Kentucky. It stands on the head waters of Elkhorn river, is reckoned the capital of Kentucky. Here the courts are held, and business regularly conducted. In 1786, it contained about 100 houses and several stores, with a good assortment of dry goods. It must have greatly increased since.
