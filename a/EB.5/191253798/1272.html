PARIETES

article · 415 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PARIETES, in anatomy, a term used for the inclosures or membranes that stop up or close the hollow parts of the body; especially those of the heart, the thorax, &c. The parietes of the two ventricles of the heart are of unequal strength and thickness; the left exceeding the right, because of its office, which is to force the blood through all parts of the body; whereas the right only drives it through the lungs.
