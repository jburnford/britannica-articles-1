PARIUM

article · 363 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PARIUM (anc. geog.), a noble city of Myfia Minor, with a port on the Propontis; called Adrosta by Homer, according to Pliny; but Strabo distinguishes them: according to others, the Paeolos of Homer. Pariani, the people (Strabo). The birthplace of Neoptolemus surnamed Glossographus (Strabo). Here stood a Cupid equal in exquisite workmanship to the Cnidian Venus.
