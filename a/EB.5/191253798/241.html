NOLLE PROSEQUI

article · 483 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

NOLLE PROSEQUI, is where a plaintiff in an action does not declare in a reasonable time; in which case it is usual for the defendant's attorney to enter a rule for the plaintiff to declare, after which a non prof. may be entered. A nolle prosequi is esteemed a voluntary confession, that the plaintiff has no cause of action; and therefore if a plaintiff enters his nolle prosequi, he shall be amerced; and if an informer cause the same to be entered, the defendant shall have costs.
