OCTANDRIA (ἑπτὰ "eight," and ἄνδρ "a man, or husband," the 8th class in Linnæus's sexual system; consisting of plants with hermaphrodite flowers, which are furnished with eight stamens, or male organs of generation. See BOTANY, p. 430.
