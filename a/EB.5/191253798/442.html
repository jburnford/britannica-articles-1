OBLATI

article · 344 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

OBLATI, in church-history, were secular persons, who devoted themselves and their estates to some monastery, into which they were admitted as a kind of lay-brothers. The form of their admission was putting the bell-ropes of the church round their necks, as a mark of servitude. They wore a religious habit, but different from that of the monks.
