OPPIANUS

OPPIANUS, a poet and grammarian of Anazarba in Cilicia, in the second century. He composed a poem of hunting, and another of fishing, for which Antoninus Caracalla gave him as many golden crowns as there were verses in his poems; they were hence called Oppian's golden verses. He died in the 30th year of his age.
