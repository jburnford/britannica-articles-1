REPLEVY

article · 338 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

REPLEVY, in law, is a tenant's bringing a writ of replevin, or replegiari facere, where his goods are taken by distress for rent: which must be done within five days after the distress, otherwise at the five days end they are to be appraised and sold.

This word is also used for bailing a person, as in the case of a homicine replegiando.
