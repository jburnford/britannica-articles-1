REGIAM MAJESTATEM. See LAW, no civ. 3.
REGICIDE, KING-KILLER, a word chiefly used with us in speaking of the persons concerned in the trial, condemnation, and execution, of king Charles I.

REGIFUGIUM was a feast celebrated at Rome on the 24th of February, in commemoration of the expulsion of Turquias Superbi, and the abolition of regal power. It was also performed on the 26th of May, when the king of the sacrifices, or Rex Sacrorum, offered bean flour and bacon, in the place where the assemblies were held. The sacrifice being over, the people hastened away with all speed, to denote the precipitate flight of King Tarquin.
