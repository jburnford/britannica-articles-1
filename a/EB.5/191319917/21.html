RAPHANIDOSIS

RAPHANIDOSIS, a punishment inflicted at Athens upon adulterers. The manner of it was this: The hair was plucked off from the privities of the offender, hot ashes laid upon the place, and a radish or mullet thrust up his fundament, as has been mentioned under ADULTERY. To this Juvenal alludes, Sat. x. ver. 317. Quosdam mechos et magis intrat. Persons who had been thus punished were called raphanidosi. The word raphanidosi is derived from raphane, a radish.
