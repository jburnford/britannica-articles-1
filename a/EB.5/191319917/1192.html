SCHWEIDNITZ

article · 629 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SCHWEIDNITZ, a strong town of Germany, in Silesia, and capital of a province of the same name, with a castle. It is the handsomest town of Silesia, next to Breslaw. The streets are large, the church fine, and the houses well built. The fortifications are not very considerable, and the royal palace is turned into a convent. All the magistrates are Roman Catholics; but most of the inhabitants are Protestants, who have a church without the town, as also a public school and bells. It is seated on an eminence on the river Weifstritz, 27 miles south-east of Lignitz, and 22 south-west of Breslaw. E. Long. 16. 48. N. Lat. 50. 46.
