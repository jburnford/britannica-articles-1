REVEREND

REVEREND, a title of respect given to ecclesiastics.—The religious abroad are called reverend fathers, and abbesses, prioresses, &c. reverend mothers. In England, bishops are right reverend, and archbishops most reverend. In France, before the Revolution, their bishops, archbishops, and abbots, were all alike most reverend. In Scotland the clergy individually are reverend, a synod is very reverend, and the general assembly is venerable.
