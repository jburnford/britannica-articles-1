SCIPIO

article · 522 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SCIPIO (Publius Emilianus), was the son of Paulus Emilius; but being adopted by Scio. Africanus, he was called Scio. Africanus junior. He showed himself worthy of adoption, following the footsteps of Scio. Africanus, whom he equalled in military fame and public virtues. His chief victories were the conquest of Carthage and Numantia; Yet these signal services to his country could not protect him from an untimely fate. He was strangled in his bed by order of the Decemviri, who dreaded his popularity, 129 B. C. aged 56.
