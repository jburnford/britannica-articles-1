REINS of a Bridle

sub_entry · 308 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

REINS of a Bridle, are two long slips of leather, fastened on each side of a curb or snaffle, which the rider holds in his hand, to keep the horse in subjection.

There is also what is called falsse reins; which is a lath of leather, passed sometimes through the arch of the banquet, to bend the horse's neck.
