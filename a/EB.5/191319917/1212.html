SCIRRHUS

article · 384 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SCIRRHUS, in surgery and medicine, a hard tumor of any part of the body, void of pain, arising, as is supposed, from the inspissation and induration of the fluids contained in a gland, though it may also appear in any other part of the body, especially in the fat; being one of the ways in which an inflammation terminates. These tumors are exceedingly apt to degenerate into cancers.
