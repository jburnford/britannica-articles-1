RENFREW

article · 473 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

RENFREW, the county-town of Renfrewshire, standing on the small river Catheart, which flows into the Clyde at the distance of five miles from Glasgow, is a small but ancient royal borough, the seat of the sheriff's court and of a presbytery. The town is neatly built, and the inhabitants enjoy a tolerable share of commerce.—Renfrew was originally joined to Lanark, but was made an independent sheriffdom by Robert II. who had a palace here. W. Long. 4. 26. N. Lat. 55. 51.
