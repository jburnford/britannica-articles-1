SARMENTOSÆ

article · 318 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: BOTANY

SARMENTOSÆ (from sarmenum, a long shoot like that of a vine); the name of the 11th class in Linnaeus's Fragments of a Natural Method, consisting of plants which have climbing stems and branches, that, like the vine, attach themselves to the bodies in their neighbourhood for the purpose of support. See BOTANY, p. 459.
