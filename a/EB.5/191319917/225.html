REGIUM, REGIUM Lepidi, Regium Lepidum, (anc. geog.); a town of Cisalpine Gaul, on the Via Aemilia, so called from Aemilius Lepidus, who was consul with C. Flaminius; but whence it was named Regium is altogether uncertain. Tacitus relates, that at the battle of Bedriacum, a bird of an unusual size was seen perching in a famous grove near Regium Lepidum. Now called Reggio, a city of Modena. E. Long. 11. o. N. Lat. 44. 45. See REGGIO.
