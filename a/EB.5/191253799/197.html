POLL-Money

sub_entry · 495 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

POLL-Money, or Capitation, a tax imposed by authority of parliament on the person or head; either on all indifferently, or according to some known mark or distinction, as quality, calling, &c.

Thus, by the statute 18 Car. II. every subject in the kingdom was assessed by the head, or poll, according to his degree; every duke L. 100, marquis L. 80, baronet L. 30, knight L. 20, esquire L. 10, &c. and every single private person 12d.

This was no new tax, as appears by former acts of parliament.
