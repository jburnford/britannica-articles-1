RAMESES

article · 256 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

RAMESES, (anc. geog.); a town built by the Israelites during their bondage in Egypt, and from which the Exodus took place, and which must have been towards and not far from the Arabian Gulph, seeing in the third station the Israelites arrived on its shore.
