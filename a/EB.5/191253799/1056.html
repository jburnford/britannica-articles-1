QUEI

article · 537 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

QUEI, in natural history, is a name given by the Chinese to a peculiar earth found in many parts of the east. It is of the nature of an indurated clay, and in some degree approaches to the talcs, as our steatites and the galactites do. It is very white and absterfive, used by the women of China to take off spots from the skin, and render it soft and smooth, as the Italian ladies use talk of Venice. They sometimes use the fine powder of this stone dry, rubbing it on the hands and face after washing; sometimes they mix it in pomatum.
