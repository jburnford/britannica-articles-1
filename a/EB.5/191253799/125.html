PLYERS

article · 429 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PLYERS, in fortification, denote a kind of balance used in raising or letting down a draw-bridge. They consist of two timber levers, twice as long as the bridge they lift, joined together by other timbers framed in the form of a St Andrew's cross to counterpoise them. They are supported by two upright jacks, on which they swing; and the bridge is raised or let down by means of chains joining the ends of the plyers and bridge.
