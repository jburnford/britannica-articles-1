PRINCE also denotes the issue of princes, or those of the royal family. In France, before the revolution, they were called princes of the blood, and during the short continuance of the constitution of 1791, French princes. In England the king's children are called sons and daughters of England; the eldest son is created prince of Wales; the cadets are created dukes or earls as the king pleases; and the title of all the children is royal highness; all subjects are to kneel when admitted to kiss their hand, and at table out of the king's presence they are served on the knee.
