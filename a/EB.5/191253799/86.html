PLOEN

article · 512 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PLOEN is a town of Germany, in the circle of Lower Saxony, and capital of Holstein. It stands on the banks of a lake of the same name, and gave title to a duke, till by the death of the last duke Charles without male issue it escheated to the king of Denmark in 1761. The ducal palace, rising in the midst of the town, on an elevated spot of ground, and overlooking the lake, is a very picturesque object. The town stands 22 miles north-west of Lubeck, and 10 south-east of Kiel. E. Long. 12. 30. N. Lat. 54. 11.
