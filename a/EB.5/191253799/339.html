PORPITES

article · 386 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PORPITES, the HAIR-BUTTON STONE, in natural history, a name given by authors to a small species of fossil coral; which is usually of a rounded figure considerably flattened, and striated from the centre every way to the circumference. These are of different sizes and of different colours, as greyish, whitish, brownish, or bluish, and are usually found immersed in stone. See Plate CC.
