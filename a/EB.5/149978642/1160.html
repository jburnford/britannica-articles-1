CATAGRAPHIA

article · 332 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CATAGRAPHIA, in antiquity, denote oblique figures or views of mens faces; answering to what the moderns call profiles.

Catagraphia are said to be the invention of Simon Cleonus, who first taught painters to vary the looks of their figures, and sometimes direct them upwards, sometimes downwards, and sometimes sideways or backwards.
