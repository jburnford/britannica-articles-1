CEVENNES

article · 334 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CEVENNES, mountains of Languedoc in France, remarkable for the frequent meetings of the Protestants there as a place of security against the tyranny of their governors. In queen Anne's reign there was an attempt made to assist them by an English fleet in the Mediterranean; but to no purpose, for the French had occupied the passages.
