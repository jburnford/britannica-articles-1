CATERVA

article · 356 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CATERVA, in ancient military writers, a term used in speaking of the Gaulish or Celtiberian armies, denoting a body of 6000 armed men. The word caterva, or cateruarius, is also frequently used by ancient writers to denote a party or corps of soldiers in disorder or disarray: by which it stands distinguished from cohort or turma, which were in good order.
