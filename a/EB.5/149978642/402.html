CAMES, a name given to the small slender rods of cast-lead, of which the glaziers make their turned lead.

Their lead being cast into slender rods of twelve or fourteen inches long each, is called the came; sometimes also they call each of these rods a came, which being afterwards drawn through their vice, makes their turned lead.
