CHESTERFIELD, a market town of Derbyshire in England, pleasantly situated on a hill between two small rivers. It has the title of an earldom; and a considerable market for corn, lead, and other country commodities. The houses are, for the most part, built of rough stone, and covered with slate. W. Long. 1. 25. N. Lat. 53. 20.
