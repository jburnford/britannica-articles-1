CARTS of War

sub_entry · 563 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CARTS of War, a peculiar kind of artillery anciently in use among the Scots. They are thus described in an act of parliament, A. D. 1456: "It is thought speidfull, that the King mak request to certain of the great burrows of the land that are of ony myght, to mak carts of weir, and in ilk cart twa guanis, and ilk ane to have twa chalmers, with the remenant of the graith that effeirs thereto, and an cunnand man to shut thame." By another act, A. D. 1471, the prelates and barons are commanded to provide such carts of war against their old enemies the English.
