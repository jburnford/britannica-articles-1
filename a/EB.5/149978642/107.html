CAERFILLY, a town of Glamorganshire in South Wales, seated between the rivers Taff and Rumney, in a moorish ground, among the hills. It is thought the walls, now in ruins, were built by the Romans; there being often Roman coins dug up there. W. Long. 3. 12. N. Lat. 51. 25.
