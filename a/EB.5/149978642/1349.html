CELL

CELL is also used for a lesser or subordinate sort of minister dependent on a great one, by which it was erected, and continues still to be governed. The great abbeys in England had most of them cells in places distant from the mother abbey, to which they were accountable, and from which they received their superiors. The alien priories in England were cells to abbeys in Normandy, France, Italy, &c. The name cell was also given to rich and considerable monasteries not dependent on any other.
