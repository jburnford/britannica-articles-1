CAMBAYES

article · 249 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CAMBAYES, in commerce, cotton cloths made at Bengal, Madras, and some other places on the coast of Coromandel. They are proper for the trade of Martelles, whither the English at Madras send great numbers of them. Many are also imported into Holland.
