CHLAMYS

article · 418 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CHLAMYS, in antiquity, a military habit worn by the ancients over the tunica. It belonged to the patricians, and was the same in the time of war that the toga was in the time of peace. This sort of gown was called pilla, from the rich embroidery with figures in Phrygian work; and purpurea, because the groundwork was purple. The chlamydes of the emperors were all purple, adorned with a golden and embroidered border.
