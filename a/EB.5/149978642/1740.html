CHAUVIN

article · 586 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CHAUVIN (Stephen), a celebrated minister of the reformed religion, born at Nismes, left France at the revocation of the edict of Nantz, and retired to Rotterdam, where he began a new Journal des Savans 3 and afterwards removing to Berlin, continued it there three years. At this last place, he was made professor of philosophy, and discharged that office with much honour and reputation. His principal work is a philosophical dictionary, in Latin, which he published at Rotterdam in 1692; and gave a new edition of it much augmented, at Leiden, in 1713, folio. He died in 1725, aged 85.
