CARPENTUM

article · 384 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CARPENTUM, in antiquity, a name common to divers sorts of vehicles, answering to coaches as well as waggons, or even carts, among us. The carpentum was originally a kind of car or vehicle in which the Roman ladies were carried; though in after times it was also used in war. Some derive the word from carro; others from Carmenta the mother of Evander, by a conversion of the m into p.
