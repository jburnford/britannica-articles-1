CELL, CELLA, in ancient writers, denotes a place or apartment usually under ground, and vaulted, in which were stored up some sort of necessaries, as wine, honey, and the like; and according to which it was

called Cella Vinaria, Olearia, Mellaria, &c. The word is formed from the Latin celare, to conceal.
