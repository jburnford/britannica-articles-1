CASTALIUS FONS

article · 262 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CASTALIUS FONS, (Strabo, Pausanias); Castalia, (Pindar, Virgil): A fountain at the foot of mount Parnassus, in Phocis, near the temple of Apollo, or near Delphi; sacred to the Muses, thence called Castalides. Its murmurs were thought prophetic, (Nonnus, Lucian.)
