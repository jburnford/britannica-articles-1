CATANA, or CATINA (anc. geog.), a town of Sicily, situated opposite to Ætna, to the south-east; one of the five Roman colonies: anciently built by the people of Naxus seven years after the building of Syracuse, 728 years before Christ. It was the country of Charondas, the famous lawgiver. The town is still called Catana. See CATANA.
