CÆTOBRIX

article · 262 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CÆTOBRIX (anc. geog.), a town of Lusitania, near the mouth of the Tagus on the east side; now extinct. It had its name from its fishery; and there are still extant fish-ponds on the shore, done with plaster of Paris, which illustrate the name of the ruined city.
