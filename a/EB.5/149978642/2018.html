CHURCHING OF WOMEN AFTER CHILD-BIRTH

article · 276 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CHURCHING OF WOMEN AFTER CHILD-BIRTH, took its rise from the Jewish rite of purification. In the Greek church it was limited to the fortieth day after delivery; but in the western parts of Europe no certain time is observed. There is an office in the liturgy for this purpose.
