To lie with a ship's fore-foot in a CHASE

sub_entry · 383 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

To lie with a ship's fore-foot in a CHASE, is to sail and meet with her by the nearest distance; and so to cross her in her way, or to come across her fore-foot.

A ship is said to have a good chase, when she is so built foreward on, or a-stern, that she can carry many guns to shoot forewards or backwards; according to which she is said to have a good foreward, or good stern chase.
