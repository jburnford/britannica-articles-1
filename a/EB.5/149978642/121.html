CÆSIANS

article · 238 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CÆSIANS, Cæsarienses, in Roman antiquity, were officers of ministers of the Roman emperors: They kept the account of the revenues of the emperors; and took possession, in their name, of such things as devolved or were confiscated to them.
