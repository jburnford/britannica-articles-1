CHURLE, CORKLE, or CARL

Cross-references: CORKLE

CHURLE, CORKLE, or CARL, in the Saxon times, signified a tenant at will, who held of the thanes on condition of rent and service. They were of two sorts: one rented the estate like our farmers: the other tilled and manured the demesnes, and were called ploughmen. See CORKLE.
