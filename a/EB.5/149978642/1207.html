CATHARINE, Fraternity of St Catharine at Siena, a sort of religious society instituted in that city, in honour of St Catharine, a saint famous for her revelations, and for her marriage with Jesus Christ, whose wedding ring is still preserved as a valuable relic. This fraternity yearly endows a certain number of destitute virgins, and has the privilege of redeeming annually two criminals condemned for murder, and the same number of debtors, by paying their debts.
