CARPUS, the wrist. See ANATOMY, No. 53.
CARR, a kind of rolling throne, used in triumphs, and at the splendid entries of princes. See CHARIOT.

The word is from the ancient Gaulish, or Celtic, Carr; mentioned by Cæsar, in his commentaries, under the name Carrus. Plutarch relates, that Camillus having entered Rome in triumph, mounted on a carr drawn by four white horses, it was looked on as too haughty an innovation.
