CASUS AMMISSIONIS

article · 360 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CASUS AMMISSIONIS, in Scots law, in actions proving the tenor of obligations inextinguishable by the debtors retiring or cancelling them, it is necessary for the pursuer, before he is allowed a proof of the tenor, to condescend upon such a casus amissionis, or accident by which the writing was destroyed, as shows it was lost while in the writer's possession.
