CHORAL

article · 282 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CHORAL, signifies any person that, by virtue of any of the orders of the clergy, was in ancient times admitted to sit and serve God in the choir.

Dordale, in his history of St. Paul's Church, says, that there were with the chorus formerly six vicars choral belonging to that church.
