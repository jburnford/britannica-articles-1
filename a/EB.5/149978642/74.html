CADENCE

article · 319 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CADENCE, in reading, is a falling of the voice below the key-note at the close of every period. In reading, whether prose or verse, a certain tone is assumed which is called the key-note; and in this tone the bulk of the

Cadence words are founded; but this note is generally lowered towards the close of every sentence.
