CARTOUCHE

CARTOUCHE, in the military art, a case of wood, about three inches thick at the bottom, girt with marlin, holding about four hundred musket-balls, besides

fix or eight balls of iron, of a pound weight, to be fired out of a hobit, for the defence of a palsy, &c.

A cartouche is sometimes made of a globular form, and filled with a ball of a pound weight; and sometimes it is made for the guns, being of a ball of half or quarter a pound weight, according to the nature of the gun, tied in form of a bunch of grapes, on a tom-pion of wood, and coated over. These were made in the room of partridge-shot.
