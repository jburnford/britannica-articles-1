CAGANUS

article · 251 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CAGANUS, or CACANUS, an appellation anciently given by the Huns to their kings. The word appears also to have been formerly applied to the princes of Muscovy, now called czar. From the same also, probably, the Tartar title cham or can, had its origin.
