CANICULUM

article · 462 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CANICULUM, or CANICULUS, in the Byzantine antiquities, a golden standish or ink-vessel, decorated with precious stones, wherein was kept the sacred encaustum, or red ink, wherewith the emperors signed their decrees, letters, &c. The word is by some derived from canis, or caniculus; alluding to the figure of a dog which it represented, or rather because it was supported by the figures of dogs. The caniculum was under the care of a particular officer of state.
