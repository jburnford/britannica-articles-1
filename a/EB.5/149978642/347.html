CAMEO

article · 617 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CAMÆIEU, or CAMAYEU, a word used to express a peculiar sort of onyx: also by some to express a

stone, whereon are found various figures, and representations of landscapes, &c. formed by a kind of lusus naturæ; so as to exhibit pictures without painting. The word comes from camebua, a name the Orientals give to the onyx, when they find, in preparing it, another colour; as who should say, a second stone. It is of these camæius Pliny is to be understood when he speaks of the manifold picture of gems, and the party-coloured spots of precious stones: Gemmarum pictura tam multiplex, lapidumque tam discolores macula.
