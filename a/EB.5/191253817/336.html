SYNODALS

article · 402 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SYNODALS, or SYNODIES, were pecuniary rents (commonly of two shillings), paid to the bishop, or archdeacon, at the time of their Easter visitation, by every parish priest. They were thus called, because usually paid in synods; because anciently bishops used to visit and hold their diocesan synods once.—For the same reason, they are sometimes also denominated synodalia; but more usually, pro-visions.
