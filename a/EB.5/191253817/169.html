SUOVETAURILIA

article · 318 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SUOVETAURILIA, an ancient Roman sacrifice, so called because it consisted of a pig (suus), a sheep or rather ram (ovis), and a bull (taurus). They were all males, to denote the masculine courage of the Roman people. It was likewise called solitaurlia, because the animals offered up were always solida, whole or uncut.
