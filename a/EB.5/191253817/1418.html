UMBELLATE

UMBELLATE, the name of a class in Ray's and Tournefort's methods, consisting of plants whose flowers grow in umbels, with five petals that are often unequal, and two naked seeds that are joined at top and separated below.

The same plants constitute the 45th order of Linnaeus's Fragmenta of a Natural Method. See BOTANY.
