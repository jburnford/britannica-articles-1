TESTUDO, in antiquity, was particularly used among the poets, &c. for the ancient lyre; because it was originally made by its inventor Mercury, of the black or hollow of the testudo aquatica, or sea-tortoise, which he accidentally found on the banks of the river Nile. See LYRE.
