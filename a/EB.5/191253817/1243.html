VELOCITY

Cross-references: QUANTITY

VELOCITY, in mechanics, swiftness; that affection of motion whereby a moveable is disposed to run over a certain space in a certain time. It is also called celerity, and is always proportional to the space moved. Huyghens, Leibnitz, Bernoulli, Wolfius, and the foreign mathematicians, hold, that the momenta or forces of falling bodies, at the end of their falls, are as the squares of their velocities into the quantity of matter; the English mathematicians, on the contrary, maintain them to be as the velocities themselves into the quantity of matter. See QUANTITY, n° 11 and 14, &c.
