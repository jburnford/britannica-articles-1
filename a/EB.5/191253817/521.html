TELLINA

article · 399 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TELLINA, in natural history, a genus of animals belonging to the class of serpent, and order of testaceae. The animal is a tethys; the shell is bivalve, generally sloping to one side, with three teeth at the hinge. Gmelin reckons about 90 species.

The tellina bury themselves in the mud or sand at the bottom of the sea, keeping a communication with the water above by means of short tubes or pipes.
