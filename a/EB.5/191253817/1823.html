WORM

Cross-references: ANGUIS · LUMERICUS · LAMPYRIS · SILK

WORM, in gunnery, a screw of iron, to be fixed on the end of a rammer, to pull out the wad of a firelock, carbine, or pistol, being the same with the wad-hook, only the

one is more proper for small arms, and the other for cannon.

Worm. In chemistry, is a long, winding pipe, placed in a tub of water, to cool and condense the vapours in the distillation of spirits.

Blind-Worm, or Slow-Worm. See ANGUIS, n° 2.

Earth-Worm. See LUMERICUS.

Glow-Worm. See LAMPYRIS.

Silk-Worm. See SILK, n° 5.
