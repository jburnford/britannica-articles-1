THYRSUS

article · 301 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

THYRSUS, in botany, a mode of flowering resembling the cone of a pine. It is, says Linnæus, a panicle contracted into an oval or egg-shaped form. The lower footstalks, which are longer, extend horizontally, whilst the upper ones are shorter and mount vertically. Lilac and butter-bur furnish examples.
