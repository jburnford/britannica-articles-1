VOLGA

article · 262 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

VOLGA, the largest river in Europe, rises in the forest of Volkonki, about 80 miles from Tver, a town in Russia. This noble river waters some of the finest provinces in the Russian empire, and at last falls into the Caspian Sea by several mouths, below Astracan.
