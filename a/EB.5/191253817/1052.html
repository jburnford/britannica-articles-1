TROLLIUS, GLOBE-FLOWER, or Lucken Gowan, in botany: A genus of plants belonging to the class of polyandria and order of polycymia; and in the natural system ranging under the 26th order, Multiflora. The calyx is wanting; there are about 14 petals; the capsules are very numerous, ovate, and monospermous. There are two species, the asiaticus and europæus; the latter of which is a British plant.

Europæus, or European globe-flower, has its corolllets convenient, and from 9 to 16 nectaria, of the length of the stamens, linear, plane, incurvated, and perforated at the inside of the base. The leaves are divided first into five segments down

down to the base; the segments are again divided, each about half way, into two or three lobes, which are sharply indented on the edges. The stalk is a foot high, and scarcely branched; the flower is yellow, globose, and spacious. It grows at the foot of mountains, and by the sides of rivulets. The country people in Sweden strew their floors and pavements on holidays with the flowers, which have a pleasant smell, and are ornamental in gardens.
