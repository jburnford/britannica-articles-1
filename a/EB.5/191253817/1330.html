VICAR

article · 309 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

VICAR, in the canon-law, denotes a priest of a parish, the predial tithes whereof are impropriated or appropriated; that is, belong either to a chapter, religious house, &c. or to a layman who receives them, and only allows the vicar the small tithes, or a convenient salary. See the article PARSON and Vicar.
