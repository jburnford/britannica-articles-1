SYDEROPCECILUS

article · 402 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SYDEROPCECILUS, in natural history, the name of a stone mentioned by the ancients. It was found in Arabia, and seems to have obtained this name from its being spotted with a ferruginous colour. The descriptions of the ancients are, however, in this, as in many other instances, too short to suffer us to guess what stone they meant.— This might possibly be a granite with spots of this peculiar colour.
