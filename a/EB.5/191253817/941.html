TRANSUBSTANTIATION

article · 266 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TRANSUBSTANTIATION, in theology, the conversion or change of the substance of the bread and wine in the eucharist, into the body and blood of Jesus Christ; which the Romish church suppose to be wrought by the consecration of the priest. See Supper of the Lord, n. 5.
