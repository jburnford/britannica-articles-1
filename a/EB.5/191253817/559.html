TANT

article · 396 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TANT, in surgery, a roll of lint made into the shape of a nail with a broad flat head, chiefly used in deep wounds and ulcers. They are of service, not only in conveying medicines to the most intimate recesses and sinuses of the wound, but to prevent the lips of the wound from uniting before it

is healed from the bottom; and by their assistance grumous blood, fordes, &c. are readily evacuated.
