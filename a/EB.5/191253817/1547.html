WAGGON, a wheel carriage, of which there are various forms, accommodated to the different uses they are intended for. The common waggon consists of the shafts or

VOL. XVIII. Part II.

rods, being the two pieces which the hind horse bears up; the welds; the stotes, or cross pieces, which hold the shafts together; the bolster, being that part on which the forewheels and the axle-tree turn in wheeling the waggon across the road; the chest or body of the waggon, having the flaves or rails fixed thereon; the bales, or hoops which compose the top; the tilt, the place covered with cloth, at the end of the waggon. See MECHANICS, Sect. iv.
