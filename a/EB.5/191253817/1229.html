VEERING

VEERING, or WEARING, the operation by which a ship, in changing her course from one board to the other, turns her stern to windward. Hence it is used in opposition to TACKING, wherein the head is turned to the wind

and the stern to leeward. See SEAMANSHIP, Vol. XVII. p. 219.
