WACHENDORFIA, in botany: A genus of plants of the class of triandria, and order of monogynia; and arranged in Linnaeus's Natural Method of Classification under the 6th order, Infata. The corolla is hexapetalous, unequal, and situated below the germen; the capsule trilocular and superior. There are four species; none of which are natives of Britain.
