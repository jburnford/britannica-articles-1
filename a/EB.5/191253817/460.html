TARPEIAN

article · 287 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TARPEIAN, in Roman antiquity, an appellation given to a steep rock in Rome; whence, by the law of the twelve tables, those guilty of certain crimes were precipitated. It took its name from Tarpeia, a vestal virgin, who was killed by the Sabines, as related under the article Rome, n° 24.
