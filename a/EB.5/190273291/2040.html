ANAGNIA

article · 447 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANAGNIA, (anc. geog.), a town of Latium, capital of the Hernici; which, after a faint resistance, submitting to the Romans, was admitted to the freedom of the city, yet without the right of suffrage, (Livy.) It was afterwards a colony of Drusus Cæsar, and walled round, and its territory assigned to the vetrans, (Frontinus.) Here Antony married Cleopatra, and divorced Octavia. Now Anagni, 36 miles to the east of Rome. Long. 13. 45. Lat. 42. 48.
