ALLIA

article · 383 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ALLIA, a river of Italy, which running down a very steep channel from the mountains of Crustumnum, mixes with the Tiber at 40 miles from Rome; famous for the great slaughter of the Romans by the Gauls, under Brennus; hence Alliensis dies, an unlucky day, (Virgil, Ovid, Lucan.) Our ancestors, says Cicero, deemed the day of the fight of Allia more fatal than that of taking the city.
