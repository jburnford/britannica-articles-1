AGORANOMUS

article · 410 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AGORANOMUS, in Grecian antiquity, a magistrate of Athens, who had the regulation of weights and measures, the prices of provisions, &c.—The ago-

ranomi, at Athens, were ten in number, five belonging to the city, and as many to the Piræus; though others make them fifteen in all, of whom they assign ten to the city. To these a certain toll or tribute was paid, by all who brought any thing to sell in the market.
