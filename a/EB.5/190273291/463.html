ACHILLES TATIUS. See TATIUS.

Tends Achilles, in anatomy, is a strong tendinous cord formed by the tendons of several muscles, and inserted into the os calcis. It has its name from the fatal wound Achilles is said to have received in that part from Paris the son of Priam.
