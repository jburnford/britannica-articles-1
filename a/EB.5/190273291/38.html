ABANA

article · 222 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABANA, or AMANA (anc. geog.), a river of Phœnicia, which, rising from Mount Hermon, washed the south and west sides of Damascus, and falls into the Phœnician sea to the north of Tripolis, called Chrysorrheas by the Greeks.
