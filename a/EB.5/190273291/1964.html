AMPHORARIUM VINUM

article · 361 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMPHORARIUM VINUM, in antiquity, denotes that which is drawn or poured into amphora or pitchers; by way of distinction from vinum doliare, or cask wine.—The Romans had a method of keeping wine in amphora for many years to ripen, by fastening the lids tight down with pitch or gypsum, and placing them either in a situation where the smoke came, or under ground.
