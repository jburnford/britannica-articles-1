ABRAHAM USQUE

article · 397 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABRAHAM USQUE, a Portuguese Jew, who translated the Bible out of Hebrew into Spanish. It was printed at Ferrara in 1553, and reprinted in Holland in 1630. This Bible, especially the first edition, which is most valuable, is marked with stars at certain words, which are designed to show that these words are difficult to be understood in the Hebrew, and that they may be used in a different sense.
