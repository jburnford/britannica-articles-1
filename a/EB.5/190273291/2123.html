ANCHOR

article · 350 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANCHOR, in architecture, is a sort of carving, somewhat resembling an anchor. It is commonly placed as part of the enrichments of the boudins of capitals of the Tuscan, Doric, and Ionic orders, and also of the boudins of bed-mouldings of the Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian cornices, anchors and eggs being carved alternately through the whole building.
