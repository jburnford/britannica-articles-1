AMBUBAJE

article · 399 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMBUBAJE, in Roman antiquity, were immodest women, who came from Syria to Rome, where they lived by prostitution, and by playing on the flute: the word is derived from the Syriac abub, which signifies a flute; altho' others make it to come from am and Baie, because these prostitutes often retired to Baie. According to Cruquius, these women used likewise to sell paint for ornamenting the face, &c.
