AMICABLE Benches

sub_entry · 249 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMICABLE Benches, in Roman antiquity, were, according to Pitiscus, lower and less honourable seats allotted for the judices pedanei, or inferior judges, who, upon being admitted of the emperor's council, were dignified by him with the title amicici.
