AMBULATORY

article · 286 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMBULATORY, a term anciently applied to such courts, &c. as were not fixed to any certain place; but held sometimes in one place, and sometimes in another. In opposition to stationary courts.—The court of parliament was anciently ambulatory; so also were the courts of king's bench, &c.
