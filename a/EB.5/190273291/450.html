ACHELOUS, a river of Acarnania; which rises in mount Pindus, and, dividing Aetolia from Acarnania, falls from north to south into the Sinus Corinthiacus. It was formerly called Thoas, from its impetuosity, and king of rivers, (Homer.) The epithet Achelous is used for Aqueus, (Virgil); the ancients calling all water Achelous, especially in oaths, vows, and sacrifices, according to Ephorus: Now called Aspro Potamo. Rivers are by the ancient poets called Tauriformes, either from
