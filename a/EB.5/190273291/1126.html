AGRIOPHAGI

article · 384 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AGRIOPHAGI, in antiquity, a name given to those who fed on wild beasts. The word is Greek, compounded of αγριον, "wild," "savage," and φαγιν, "I eat." The name is given, by ancient writers, to certain people, real or fabulous, said to have fed altogether on lions and panthers. Pliny and Solinus speak of Agriophagi in Ethiopia, and Ptolemy of others in India on this side the Ganges.
