ACOUSTIC Vessels

sub_entry · 325 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACOUSTIC Vessels, in the ancient theatres, were a kind of vessels, made of brass, shaped in the bell fashion, which being of all tones within the pitch of the voice or even of instruments, rendered the sounds more audible, so that the actors could be heard through all parts of theatres, which were even 400 feet in diameter.
