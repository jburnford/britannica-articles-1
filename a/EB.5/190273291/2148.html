ANCUS MARTIUS

ANCUS MARTIUS, the fourth king of the Romans, succeeded by Tullius Hostilius, 639 years before Christ. He defeated the Latins, subdued the Fidenates, conquered the Sabines, Volscii, and Veientes, enlarged Rome by joining to it mount Janicula, and made the harbour of Ostia. He died about 615 years before the Christian era.
