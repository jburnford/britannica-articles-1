AMALTHEUS

AMALTHEUS (Jerome, John Baptista, and Cornielle), three celebrated Latin poets of Italy, who flourished in the 16th century. Their compositions were printed at Amsterdam in 1685. One of the prettiest pieces in that collection is an epigram on two children, whose beauty was very extraordinary, though each of them was deprived of an eye:

‘ Lumine Acon dextro, capta est Leonilla sinistro :
‘ Et poterat forma vincere uterque deos.
‘ Parve puer, lumen quod habes concede forori;
‘ Sic tu cæcus Amor, sic erit illa Venus.’
