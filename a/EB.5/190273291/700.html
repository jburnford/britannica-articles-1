ADELSCALC

article · 344 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ADELSCALC, in ancient customs, denotes a servant of the king. The word is also written adelfcaleche, and adelfcaleur. It is compounded of the German adel, or edel, "noble," and scale, "servant." Among the Bavarians, adelfcales appear to have been the same with royal thanes among the Saxons, and those called ministri regis in ancient charters.
