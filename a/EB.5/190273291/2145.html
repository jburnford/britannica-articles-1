ANCORARUM

article · 288 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANCORARUM uans, Ἀνκράτων Πόλις, a city in the Nomos Aphroditopolites, towards the Red Sea; so called because there was in the neighbourhood a stone quarry, in which they hewed stone anchors (Ptolemy) before iron anchors came to be used. The gentilicious name is Ancropolites, (Stephanus).
