ACOLUTHI

article · 372 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACOLUTHI, or ACOLUTHISTS, in antiquity, was an appellation given to those persons who were steady and immovable in their resolutions: and hence the stoics, because they would not forsake their principles, nor alter their resolutions, acquired the title of Acoluthi. The word is Greek, and compounded of α, priv. and κλινειν, way; as never turning from the original course.
