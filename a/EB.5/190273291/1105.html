AGONALIS circus

AGONALIS circus, now La Piazza Navona, a long, large, beautiful street in the heart of Rome, adorned with fountains, and the obelisk of Caracalla, still retaining the form of that circus. The reason of the name Agonalis is either unknown or doubtful. Ovid seems to derive it from the agones, or solemn games,

there celebrated; supposed to have been the Ludi Apollinares, or Attici, instituted by Augustus: whence the circus was called Apollinaris; also Alexandrinus, from the emperor Alexander Severus, who either enclosed or repaired the circus.
