ABERYSWITH

ABERYSWITH, a market-town of Cardigan-shire, in Wales, seated on the Ridal, near its confluence with the Isthwith, where it falls into the sea. It is a populous, rich town, and has a great trade in lead, and a considerable fishery of whiting, cod, and herrings. It was formerly surrounded with walls, and fortified with a castle; but both are now in ruins. Its distance from London is 199 miles west-south-west. W. Long. 4. 15. Lat. 52. 30.
