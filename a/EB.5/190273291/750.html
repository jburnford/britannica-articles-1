ADMANUENSES

article · 313 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ADMANUENSES, in ancient law books, denote persons who swore by laying their hands on the book.—In which sense, admanuenses amount to the same with laymen; and stand opposed to clerks, who were forbid to swear on the book, their word being to be reputed as their oath; whence they were also denominated sine digni.
