ALEXANDRIAN, in a particular sense, is applied to all those who professed or taught the sciences in the school of Alexandria. In this sense, Clemens is denominated Alexandrinus, though born at Athens. The same may be said of Apion, who was born at Oasis; and Arostarchus, by birth a Samothracian. ALEXANDRIAN is more particularly understood of a college of priests, consecrated to the service of Alexander Severus after his deification. Lampridius relates, that, notwithstanding Severus was killed by Maximin, the senate prosecuted his apotheosis; and, for regularity of worship, founded an order of priests, or fofales, under the denomination of Alexandrini.
