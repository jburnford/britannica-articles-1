AGUILLONIUS

article · 347 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AGUILLONIUS (Francis), a Jesuit, born at Brussels: he was rector of the Jesuits college at Antwerp, and eminent for his skill in mathematics. He was the first who introduced that science among the Jesuits in the low countries: he wrote a book of Optics, and was employed in finishing his Catoptrics and Dioptrics, when death prevented him in 1617.
