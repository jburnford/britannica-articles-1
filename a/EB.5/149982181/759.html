JONAS (Justus), a Protestant divine, born at North Hausen, in Thuringia, in 1493. He was one of Luther's most zealous disciples. He contracted a strict friendship with Melanchthon; became principal of the college of Wittemberg, and afterwards dean of the university of that city. He wrote a treatise in favour of the marriage of priests, and other works; and died in 1555.
