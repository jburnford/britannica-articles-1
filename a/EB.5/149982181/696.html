INVENTION

Cross-references: ORATORY

INVENTION, in rhetoric, signifies the finding out and choosing of certain arguments which the orator is to use for the proving or illustrating his point, moving their passions, or conciliating the minds of his hearers. Invention, according to Cicero, is the principal part of oratory: he wrote four books De Inventione, whereof we have but two remaining. See ORATORY.
