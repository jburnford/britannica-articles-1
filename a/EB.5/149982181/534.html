INFUSION

article · 339 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

INFUSION, in pharmacy, an operation whereby the virtues of plants, roots, and the like, are drawn out, by letting them steep in some convenient fluid menstruum, without boiling them therein; since boiling is found to dissipate the finer parts of many bitter and aromatic substances, without carefully extracting their medicinal principles.
