KETTLEWELL

article · 540 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

KETTLEWELL (John), a learned divine, born in 1653, was descended from an ancient family in the North-riding of Yorkshire, bred in Edmund-Hall Oxford, and elected fellow of Lincoln-college. In 1675, he went into orders; but after the revolution was deprived of his living, on account of his refusal to take the oaths to King William and Queen Mary. He died of a consumption in 1695. He published several works, which were collected and reprinted together in 1718, in

2 vols folio. He was a man of great candour, meekness, piety, and charity.
