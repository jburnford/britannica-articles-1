LAVER

article · 253 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LAVER, in scripture history, a sacred utensil placed in the court of the Jewish tabernacle, consisting of a basin, whence they drew water by cocks, for washing the hands and feet of the officiating priests, and also the entrails and legs of the victims.
