ICONIUM

article · 705 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ICONIUM, at present COGNI, formerly the capital city of Lycania in Asia Minor. St Paul coming to Iconium (Acts xiii. 51. xiv. 1, &c.) in the year of Christ 45, converted many Jews and Gentiles there. It is believed, that in his first journey to this city, he converted St Thecla, so celebrated in the writings of the ancient fathers. But some incredulous Jews excited the Gentiles to rise against Paul and Barnabas, so that they were upon the point of offering violence to them, which obliged St Paul and St Barnabas to fly for security to the neighbouring cities. St Paul undertook a second journey to Iconium in the year 51; but we know no particulars of his journey, which relate peculiarly to Iconium.
