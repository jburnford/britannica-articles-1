INSOLATION

article · 319 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

INSOLATION, in pharmacy, a method of preparing certain fruits, drugs, &c. by exposing them to the heat of the sun's rays; either to dry, to mature, or to sharpen them; as is done in vinegar, figs, &c.—The word comes from the Latin verb insolare, which is used by Pliny and Columella, and signifies to expose to the sun.
