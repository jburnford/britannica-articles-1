LATH-Bricks

sub_entry · 440 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LATH-Bricks, a particular sort of bricks made in some parts of England, of 22 inches in length and 6 in breadth, which are used in the place of laths or spars, supported by pillars in cases, for the drying of malt. This is an excellent contrivance: for besides that they are not liable to fire, as the wooden laths are, they retain the heat vastly better; so that being once heated, a very small quantity of fire will serve to keep them so.
