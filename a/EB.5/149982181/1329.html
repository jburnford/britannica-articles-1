LABOUR

article · 261 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LABOUR, in general, denotes a close application to work or business.—Among seamen a ship is said to be in labour when she rolls and tumbles very much, either a hull, under sail, or at anchor.—It is also spoken of a woman in travail or childbirth ; see MIDWIFERY.
