INGENUOUS

article · 324 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

INGENUOUS, (ingenuus), in Roman antiquity, an appellation given to persons born of free parents, who had never been slaves: for the children of the liberti, or persons who had obtained their liberty, were called libertini, not ingenui; this appellation of ingenuus being reserved for their children, or the third generation.
