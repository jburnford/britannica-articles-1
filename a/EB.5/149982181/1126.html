KESWICK

article · 560 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

KESWICK, a town of Cumberland, situated on the side of a lake in a fruitful plain, almost encompassed with mountains, called the Terwent Fells. It was formerly a town of good note, but now is much decayed. However, it is still noted for its mines and miners, who have a convenient smelting-house on the side of the river Derwent, the stream of which is so managed as to make it work the bellows, hammers, and forge, as also to saw boards. There is a work-house here for employing the poor of this parish and that of Crossthwaite. W. Long. 3. o. N. Lat. 54. 30.
