HYPNOTICUS SERPENS, the Sleep-snake, in zoology, the name of an East-Indian species of serpent, called by the Ceylonese nimipolong, a word importing the same sense. It is of a deep blackish brown, varie-

gated with spots of white, and is a very fatal kind in its poison; its bite always bringing on a sleep which ends in death.
