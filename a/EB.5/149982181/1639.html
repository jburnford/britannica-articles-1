LAWINGEN, a town of Germany, in the circle of Suabia; formerly imperial, but now subject to the duke of Neuburg. Here the duke of Bavaria, in 1704, fortified his camp to defend his country against the British forces and their allies commanded by the duke of Marlborough, who forced their intrenchments. It is seated on the Danube, in E. Long. 10. 29. N. Lat. 38. 32.
