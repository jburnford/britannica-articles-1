HYPBOLE

HYPBOLE, or SUBJECTION, (from υπό, and βάλλω, I cast), in rhetoric, a figure; so called, when several things are mentioned, that seem to make for the contrary side, and each of them refuted in order. This figure, when complete, consists of three parts; a proposition, an enumeration of particulars with their answer, and a conclusion. Thus Cicero, upon his return from banishment, vindicates his conduct in withdrawing so quietly, and not opposing the faction that ejected him. See ORATORY, n° 81.
