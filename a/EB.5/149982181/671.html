INTERROGATORIES

article · 558 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

INTERROGATORIES, in law, are particular questions demanded of witnesses brought in to be examined in a cause, especially in the court of chancery. And these interrogatories must be exhibited by the parties in suit on each side; which are either direct for the party that produces them, or counter, on behalf of the adverse party; and generally both plaintiff and defendant may exhibit, direct, and counter, or cross interrogatories. They are to be pertinent, and only to the points necessary; and either drawn or perused by counsel, and to be signed by them.
