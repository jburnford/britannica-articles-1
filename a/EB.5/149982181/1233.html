KIRTON, or KIRKTON

KIRTON, or KIRKTON, a town of Lincolnshire, 151 miles from London. It had its name from its kirk or church, which is truly magnificent. It has a market and two fairs. This place is famous for the pippin, which, when grafted on its stock, is called the rennet. It gives names to its hundreds, in which are four villages of the same name.
