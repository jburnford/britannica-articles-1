ICKENILD-STREET

article · 496 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ICKENILD-STREET, is that old Roman highway, denominated from the Iceni, which extended from Yarmouth in Norfolk, the east part of the kingdom of the Iceni, to Barley in Hertfordshire, giving name in the way to several villages, as Ickworth, Icklingham, and Ickleton in that kingdom. From Barley to Royston it divides the counties of Cambridge and Hertford.
