ENNA

ENNA, (anc. geog.), a town of Sicily, situated on an eminence to the south of the Chrysis; called the centre of Sicily. It was famous for a sacred grove, in which the rape of Proserpina happened; for a temple of Ceres, thence furnished Ennea, and Ennefis; and for fine springs, whence the name (Bochart.)
