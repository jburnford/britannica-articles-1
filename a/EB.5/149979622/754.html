EBORACUM

EBORACUM (anc. geog.), a famous city of the Brigantes in Britain, the residence of Septimius Severus and Constantius Chlorus, and where they both died; a Roman colony; and the station of the Legio Sexta Viatrix. Now York. W. Long. 50. Lat. 54. Cair-freeck, or Cair-effree, in British (Camden).
