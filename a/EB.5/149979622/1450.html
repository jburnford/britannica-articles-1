ESSEDARII

Cross-references: GLADIATOR

ESSEDARI, a sort of gladiators, mentioned by Seneca, Suetonius, and Tully, who on some occasions engaged one another out of chariots called esseda. The esseda was a sort of heavy chariot from which the Gauls and Britons engaged the Romans. See GLADIATOR.
