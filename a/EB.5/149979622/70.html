DICTYMNIA

article · 296 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DICTYMNIA, or DICTYNMIA, in mythology, were feasts celebrated at Lacedæmon and in Crete, in honour of Diana Dictymnia or Dictynnia, or of a nymph taken for her, who, having plunged herself into the sea, to escape the passion of Minos, was caught in a fisherman's net or dictynna, whence the name.
