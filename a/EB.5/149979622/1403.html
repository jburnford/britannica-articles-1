ERYTHEA, or ERYTHIA, an island adjoining, according to the ancients, either to or a part of Gades; no where now to be found by the description given of it.

it by ancient authors. The poets feign this to be the habitation of the fabulous Geryon, disarmed by Hercules, who drove away his cattle.
