EPISCOPAL, something belonging to BISHOPS. EPISCOPALIAN, in church-history, an appellation given to those who prefer the episcopal government and discipline to all others. See EPISCOPACY.

By the test act, none but Episcopalian, or members of the church of England, are qualified to enjoy any office civil or military.
