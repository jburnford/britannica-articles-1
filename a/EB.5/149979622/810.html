ECTHLIPSIS

article · 283 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ECTHLIPSIS, among Latin grammarians, a figure of profody whereby the m at the end of a word, when the following word begins with a vowel, is elided, or cut off, together with the vowel preceding it, for the sake of the measure of the verse: thus they read multi ille, or multum ille.
