ESTATES

article · 333 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ESTATES, in a political sense, is used either to denote the dominions of some prince, or the general classes into which the people are divided.

In Britain, the estates are the king, lords, and commons;

mons; or rather the lords and commons, who meet the king in parliament, for reforming abuses, and enacting good and wholesome laws.
