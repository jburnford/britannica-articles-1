ENORMOUS

article · 371 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ENORMOUS, something excessive or monstrous, especially in bulk.—The word is formed of the privative e, and norma, "rule;" q. d. "void of, or contrary to, rule or measure;" contra normam. In the corrupt ages of Latinity, they used innormis, and inormis.

In the French jurisprudence, lesso enormis, "enormous damage," is that which exceeds half the value of the thing sold.
