DIVERSIFYING

article · 544 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIVERSIFYING, in rhetoric, is of infinite service to the orator; it is an accomplishment essential to his character, and may fitly be called the subject of all his tropes and figures. Vossius lays down six ways of diversifying a subject. 1. By enlarging on what was briefly mentioned before. 2. By a concise enumera-

tion of what had been insisted on at length. 3. By Diversion adding something new to what is repeated. 4. By repeating only the principal heads of what had been said. 5. By transposing the words and periods. 6. By imitating them.
