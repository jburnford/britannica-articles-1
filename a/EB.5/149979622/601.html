DUCHAL (James), D. D. a late pious and learned
dissenting minister, was born in Ireland, and finished
his studies at the university of Glasgow; which after-
wards, from a regard to his merit, conferred on him
the degree of doctor of divinity. He resided 10 or 11
years at Cambridge, as the pastor of a small congre-
gation there; where he enjoyed his beloved retirement,
the

the advantage of books and of learned conversation, which he improved with the greatest diligence. On Mr Abernethy's removal from Antrim, he succeeded him there; and on that gentleman's death, he succeeded him as minister of the dissenting meeting-house in Wood-street, Dublin. In this situation he continued till his death, which happened on the 4th of May 1761, when he had completed his 64th year. He published a volume of excellent discourses on the presumptive arguments in favour of the Christian religion, and many occasional tracts; and after his death were published a number of his sermons, in three volumes 8vo.
