ERETRIA

article · 352 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ERETRIA (anc. geog.), a town of Eubœa, situated on the Euripus, in the south-west of the island. A very ancient city, and the largest of the island, after Chalcis. After being demolished by the Persians, it was restored on an adjoining spot, according to Strabo, who mentions a school of Eretrian philosophers there. The Abantes of Homer were of Eubœa.
