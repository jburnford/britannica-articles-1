DRAGON-Shell

sub_entry · 465 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DRAGON-Shell, in natural history, a name given by people curious in shells to a species of conchiferous patella or limpet. This has a top very much bent; and is of an ash-colour on the outside, but of an elegant and bright flesh-colour within. This has been found sticking on the back of a tortoise, as the common limpets do on the sides of rocks; and some have been

found affixed to large shells of the pinna marina brought from the East Indies at different times.
