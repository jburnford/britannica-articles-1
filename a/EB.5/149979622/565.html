DRIVING

article · 354 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DRIVING, in the sea-language, is said of a ship, when an anchor being let fall will not hold her fast, nor prevent her sailing away with the wind or tide. The best help in this case is to let fall more anchors, or to veer out more cable; for the more cable she has out, the safer she rides. When a ship is a-hull or a-try, they say she drives to leeward.
