PICHFORD, in the county of Salop in England; on the south-east side of Shrewsbury, near Conduover. It is noted for a spring of pitchy water (from whence some derive its name), on the top of which there always flows a sort of liquid bitumen. Over most of the coal-pits hereabouts there lies a stratum of blackish rock; of which, by boiling and grinding, they make pitch and tar, and also distil an oil from it.
