PHUT, or PHUTH, the third son of Ham (Gen. x. 6.) Calmet is of opinion, that Phut peopled either the canton of Ptemphut, Ptemphuti, or Ptembuti, set down in Pliny and Ptolemy, whose capital was Thara in Lower Egypt, inclining towards Libya; or the canton called Pthenotes, of which Butus was the capital. The prophets often speak of Phut. In the time of Jeremiah, Phut was under the obedience of Necho king of Egypt. Nahum (iii. 9.) reckons up his people in the number of those who ought to have come to the assistance of Noammon or Diospolis.
