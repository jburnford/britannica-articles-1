PHARÆ

article · 510 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PHARÆ (anc. geog.), a town of Achaia in Peloponnesus, on the river Pierus, 70 stadia from the sea, and to the south of Patre 150 stadia. Another, of Crete (Pliny); a colony from the Phara of Messenia (Stephanus.) A third Phara, or Phera (Strabo, Ptolemy); Phara, &c. (Polybius); a town of Messenia, on the river Nedo (Strabo); on the north side of the Sinus Messenius, and to the north-west of Abea. Anciently read Pharis in Homer (Paufanias, Statius), though now read Phare. Pharisa is the name of the people.
