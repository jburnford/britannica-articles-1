PIL

Cross-references: FUNERAL

PIL, among the Greeks and Romans, was a pyramid built of wood, wherein were laid the bodies of the deceased to be burnt. It was partly in the form of an altar, and differed in height according to the quality of the person to be consumed. Probably it might originally be considered as an altar, on which the dead were consumed as a burnt-offering to the in-

fernal deities. The trees made use of in the erection of a funeral pile were such as abounded in pitch or rosin, as being most combustible; if they used any other wood, it was split that it might the more easily catch fire. Round the pile were placed cypress boughs to hinder the noisome smell. See FUNERAL.
