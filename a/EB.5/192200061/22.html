PASTOPHORI

article · 469 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PASTOPHORI, amongst the ancients, were priests whose office it was to carry the images, along with the farines of the gods, at solemn festivals, when they were to pray to them for rain, fair weather, or the like. The Greeks had a college of this order of priests in Sylla's time. The cells or apartments near the temples, where the pastophori lived, were called pastophoria. There were several lodging-rooms for the priests of a similar kind in the temple of Jerusalem.
