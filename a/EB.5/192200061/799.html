PIGUS

article · 1,078 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PIGUS, in ichthyology, is the name of a species of leather mouthed fish, very much resembling the nature of the common carp; being of the same shape and size, and its eyes, fins, and fleshy palate, exactly the same; from the gills to the tail there is a crooked dotted line; the

Pihahiroth the brook and fides are bluish, and the belly reddish. It is covered with large scales; from the middle of each of which there rises a fine, pellucid, prickle, which is very sharp. It is an excellent fish for the table, being perhaps preferable to the carp: and it is in season in the months of March and April. It is caught in lakes in some parts of Italy, and is mentioned by Pliny, tho' without a name. Arteri says it is a species of cyprinus, and he calls it the cyprinus, called pico and pigus.

PI-HAMIROTH, (Moses); understood to be a mouth or narrow pass between two mountains, called Chiroth, or Eiroth, and lying not far from the bottom of the western coast of the Arabian gulf; before which mouth the children of Israel encamped, just before their entering the Red Sea, (Wells).
