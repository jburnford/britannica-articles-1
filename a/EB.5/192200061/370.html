PERIOD

article · 532 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PERIOD, in medicine, is applied to certain diseases which have intervals, and returns, to denote an entire course or circle of such disease; or its progress from any state through all the rest till it return to the same again.

Galen describes period as a time composed of an intension and remission; whence it is usually divided into two parts, the paroxysm or exacerbation, and remission.

In intermitting fevers, the periods are usually stated and regular; in other diseases, as the epilepsy, gout, &c. they are vague or irregular.
