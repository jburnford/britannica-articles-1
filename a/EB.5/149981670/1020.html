HENED-PENNY

article · 330 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HENED-PENNY, in our old writers, a customary payment of money instead of hens at Christmas. It is mentioned in a charter of king Edward III. Mon. Angl. tom. ii. p. 327. Du-Cange is of opinion it may be hen-penny, gallinagium, or a composition for eggs; but Cowel thinks it is misprinted hened-penny for heved-penny, or heav-penny.
