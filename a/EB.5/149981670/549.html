HAILING

article · 261 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HAILING, the salutation or accosting of a ship at a distance, either at sea, or in a harbour. The usual expression is, "Hoo, the ship ahoay!" To which she answers, "Holloa! Whence came ye? Where are ye bound? Good voyage! What cheer? All well! How fare ye?" &c.
