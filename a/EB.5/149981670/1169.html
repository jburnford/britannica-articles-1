HETERODOX

article · 312 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HETERODOX, in polemical theology, something that is contrary to the faith or doctrine established in the true church. The word is formed of the Greek ἑτεροδόξος; a compound of ἑτερος "alter," and δόξα "opinion." Thus, we say a heterodox opinion, a heterodox divine, &c. The word stands in opposition to orthodox.
