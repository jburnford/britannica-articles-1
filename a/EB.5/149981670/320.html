GROSS

article · 450 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GROSS, or Grossus, in our ancient law writers, denote a thing absolute, and not depending on another. Thus, villain in gross, villanus in grosso, was a servant, who did not belong to the land, but immediately to the person of the lord; or a servile person not appendant or annexed to the land or manor, and to go along with the tenures as appurtenant to it; but like other personal goods and chattels of his lord, at his lord's pleasure and disposal.
