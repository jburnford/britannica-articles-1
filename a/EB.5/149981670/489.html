GYMNOSPERMIA

Cross-references: BOTANY

GYMNOSPERMIA, in botany, (from γυμνός "naked," and σπέρμα "seed;") the first order in Linnaeus's class of didynamia. It comprehends those plants of that class which have naked seeds. The feet are constantly four in number, except in one genus, viz. phryma, which is monospermous. See BOTANY, p. 431.
