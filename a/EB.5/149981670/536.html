HÆMUS

article · 233 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HÆMUS, (anc. geog.), a vast ridge, running from Illyricum towards the Euxine, (Pliny); so high as to afford a prospect both of the Euxine and Adriatic. Here, in after-ages, was constituted a province called Hæmumontus, or Hæmumontus.
