HOOD

HOOD (Robin), a famous outlaw and deer-stealer, who chiefly harboured in Sherwood forest in Nottinghamshire. He was a man of family, which by his pedigree appears to have had some title to the earldom of Huntingdon; and played his pranks about the latter end of the 12th century. He was famous for archery, and for his treatment of all travellers who came in his way; levying contributions on the rich, and relieving the poor. Falling sick at last, and requiring to be blooded, he is said to have been betrayed and bled to death. He died in 1247; and was buried at Kirklees in Yorkshire, then a Benedictine monastery, where his gravestone is still shown.
