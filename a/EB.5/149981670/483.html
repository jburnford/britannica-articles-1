GYGES

article · 1,006 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GYGES (fab. hist.), a Lydian, to whom Candaulis king of the country showed his wife naked. The queen was so incensed at this instance of imprudence and infirmity in her husband, that she ordered Gyges either to prepare for death himself, or to put Candaulis to death. He chose the latter; and, marrying the queen, ascended the vacant throne about 718 years before the Christian era. He was the first of the Mermnadæ who reigned in Lydia. He reigned 38 years, and distinguished himself by the immense presents which he made to the oracle of Delphi (Hered. 1. c. 8.)—According to Plato, Gyges descended into a chasm of the earth, where he found a brazen horse, whose sides he opened, and saw within the body the carcase of a man of uncommon size, from whose finger he took a brazen ring. This ring, when he put it on his finger, rendered him invisible; and by means of its virtue he introduced himself to the queen, murdered her husband, and married her and usurped the crown of Lydia. (Cic. Off. iii. c. 9.)
