HERACLEONITES. HEPTANDRIA, in botany (from hepta septem, and andria a man); the seventh class in Linnaeus's sexual method, consisting of plants with hermaphrodite flowers, which have seven stamens or male-organs. The orders are four, derived from the number of styles or female-organs.
