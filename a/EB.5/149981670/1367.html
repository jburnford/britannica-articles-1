HOLOGRAPHUM

article · 406 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HOLOGRAPHUM (composed of ὁλος "all," and γράψω "I write"), in the civil law, something written wholly in the hand-writing of the person who signs it. The word is chiefly used in speaking of a testament written wholly in the testator's own hand.

The Romans did not approve of holographic testaments; and, though Valentinian authorized them by a novel, they are not used where the civil law is in full force.
