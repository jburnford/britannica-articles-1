HEBRUS

HEBRUS (anc. geog.), the largest river of Thrace, rising from mount Scombrus; running in two channels till it comes to Philippopolis, where they unite. It empties itself at two mouths into the Ægean Sea, to the north of Samothrace. It was supposed to roll its waters upon golden saads. The head of Orpheus was thrown into it after it had been cut off by the Ciconian women.
