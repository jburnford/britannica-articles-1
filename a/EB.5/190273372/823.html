NAARDA, NEARDA, Neerda, or Nebarda, (anc. geog.), a town situated on the confines of Mesopotamia and Babylonia; populous, and with a rich and extensive territory, not easily to be attacked by an enemy, being surrounded on all sides by the Euphrates and strong walls (Josephus). In the lower age the Jews had a celebrated school there.
