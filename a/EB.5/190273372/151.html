MITCHELSTOWN, a post-town of Ireland, in the county of Cork and province of Munster in Ire-

land, 102 miles from Dublin. Here is a college for the support of 12 decayed gentlemen and 12 decayed gentlewomen, who have L. 40 yearly, and handsome apartments, and a chaplain at L. 100 a-year, with a house: divine service is daily performed in a neat chapel belonging to the college: the whole was founded by the late earl of Kingston. Here is also a most magnificent seat of Lord Kingsborough.—Fairs are held at this town 30th July and 12th November.
