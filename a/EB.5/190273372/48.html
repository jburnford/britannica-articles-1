MILLIARIUM AUREUM

article · 356 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: FULLING

MILLIARIUM AUREUM, was a gilded pillar in the forum of Rome, at which all the highways of Italy met, as one common centre. From this pillar the miles were counted, and at the end of every mile a stone was put down. The milliary column was erected by Augustus Cæsar, and, as we are informed by travellers, is still to be seen.
