MODREVIUS

article · 458 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MODREVIUS (Andreas Frichius), secretary to Sigismund Augustus king of Poland, acquired considerable reputation by his learning and works. He broke off from the Romish church, favoured the Lutherans and Anti-trinitarians, and took great pains in order to unite all Christian societies under the same communion. Grotius has placed him in the class of the reconcilers of the different schemes of religion. His principal work is intitled, De republica emendanda.
