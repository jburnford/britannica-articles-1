SIPUNCULUS

article · 334 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SIPUNCULUS, in natural history, a genus of the intestina class of worms in the Linnæan system. Its characters are these: the body is round and elongated; the mouth attenuated and cylindrical; and the lateral aperture of the body rugged. There are two species; one found under stones in the European, and the other in the Indian ocean.
