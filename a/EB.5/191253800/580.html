SIRIUM, in botany; a genus of plants belonging to the class of tetrandria and order of monogynia. The calyx is quadrifid; there is no corolla; the nectarium is quadrifollious and crowning the throat of the calyx; the germen is below the corolla; the stigma is trifid, and the berry trilocular. There is only one species, the myrtifolium.
