SEPTIZON

SEPTIZON, or SEPTIZONIUM, in Roman antiquity, a celebrated mausoleum, built by Septimus Severus, in the tenth region of the city of Rome: it was so called

Septuagesima, called from septem and zona, by reason it consisted of seven stories, each of which was surrounded by a row of columns.
