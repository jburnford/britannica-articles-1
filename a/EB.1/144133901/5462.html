BUNT of a sail

BUNT of a sail, the middle part of it, formed designedly into a bag or cavity, that the sail may gather more

more wind. It is used mostly in top-fails, because courses are generally cut square, or with but small allowance for bunt or compass. The bunt holds much leeward wind, that is, it hangs much to leeward.
