AMYGDALUS

article · 335 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMYGDALUS, or ALMOND-TREE, in botany, a genus of the icosandria monogynia class. The calix is divided into five segments; and the corolla consists of five petals. The species are three, viz. the persica, or peach-tree; the communis, a native of Mauritania; and the nana, a native of Asia. Almonds are used in medicine as emollient, &c.
