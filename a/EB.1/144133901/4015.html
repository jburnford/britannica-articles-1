BATTEN, a name that workmen give to a scantling of

wooden stuff, from two to four inches broad, and about one inch thick; the length is pretty considerable, but undetermined.

This term is chiefly used in speaking of doors and windows of shops, &c. which are not framed of whole deal, &c. with styles, rails, and panells like wainscot, but are made to appear as if they were, by means of these battens, bradded on the plain board round the edges, and sometimes cross them, and up and down.
