Cross-references: LAW

ACCESSORY, in Scots law, is the subject acquired by accession; or, in crimes, it signifies the person by whose assistance, advice, or command, the crime was committed: In this latter sense, it is the same with accomplice, art and part, &c. See LAW, title, Crimes.
