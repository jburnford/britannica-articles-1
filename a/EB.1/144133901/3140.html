ASPHURELATA

article · 421 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: CHEMISTRY

ASPHURELATA, in natural history, are semi-metallic fossils, fusible by fire, and not malleable in their purest state, being in their native state intimately mixed with sulphur and other adventitious matter, and reduced to what are called ores.

Of this series of fossils, there are only five bodies, each of which makes a distinct genus; and these bodies are antimony, bismuth, cobalt, zinc, or quicksilver. See CHEMISTRY.
