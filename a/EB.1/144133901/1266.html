ALA

article · 318 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ALA, in botany, is used in different senses; sometimes it denotes the hollow between the stalk of a plant and the leaves; sometimes it is applied to the two side-petals of the papilionaceous flowers; others use it for the slender membranaceous expansions found in the stems of plants, thence denominated alated stalks.
