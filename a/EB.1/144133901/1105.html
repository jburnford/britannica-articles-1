AGNOETÆ

article · 265 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AGNOETÆ, in church-history, a sect of heretics, so called on account of their maintaining, that Christ, with respect to his human nature, was ignorant of many things, and particularly of the day of judgment, an opinion which they built upon the text, Mark xiii. 32.
