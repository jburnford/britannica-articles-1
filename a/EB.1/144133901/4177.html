BEECH-OIL

article · 307 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BEECH-OIL, an oil drawn by expression from the mast of the beech-tree, after it has been shelled and pounded. This oil is very common in Picardy, and used there, and in other parts of France, instead of butter; but most of those who take a great deal of it, complain of pains and a heaviness in the stomach.
