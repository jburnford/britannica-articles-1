BOUTÉ

article · 208 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BOUTÉ, in the menage. A horse is called bouté, when his legs are in a straight line from the knee to the coronet: Short-jointed horses are apt to be bouté; and, on the other hand, long-jointed horses are not.
