ACQUIETANDIS plegiis

sub_entry · 157 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACQUIETANDIS plegiis, in the English law, is a writ that lies for a surety, against a creditor, who refuses to acquit the complainant after the debt is paid.
