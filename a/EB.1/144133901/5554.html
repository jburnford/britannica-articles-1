BUSTUM

article · 385 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BUSTUM, in antiquity, a pyramid or pile of wood upon which were anciently placed the bodies of the deceased, in order to be burnt. Some authors say, that it was properly called bustum after the burning,

quasi bene usum; that before the burning it was called pyra, and during the burning, regus.

The bustum in the Campus Martius was encompassed round with white stone, and an iron rail.
