BACCHIUS

article · 282 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BACCHIUS, in ancient poetry, a kind of foot composed of a short syllable, and two long ones, as the word [avari]. It takes its name from the god Bacchus, because it frequently entered into the hymns composed in his honour. The Romans called it likewise enotrius, tripodius, faltans.
