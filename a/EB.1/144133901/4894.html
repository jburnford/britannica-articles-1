BOROUGH-HEAD, or HEADBOROUGH, called also borough-holder, or burgholder, the chief man of the decenna, or hundred, chosen to speak and act in behalf of the rest.
Headborough also signifies a kind of head constable, where there are several chosen as his assistants, to serve warrants, &c. See CONSTABLE.
