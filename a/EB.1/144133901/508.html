ACLIDES

article · 249 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACLIDES, in Roman antiquity, a kind of missive weapon, with a thong affixed to it, whereby to draw it back. Most authors describe it as a sort of dart or javelin; but Scaliger makes it roundish or globular, with a slender wooden stem to poise it by.
