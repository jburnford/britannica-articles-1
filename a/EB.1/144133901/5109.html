BREACH

article · 482 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BREACH, in fortification, a gape made in any part of the works of a town by the cannon or mines of the besiegers, in order to make an attack upon the place. To make the attack more difficult, the besieged sow the breach with crow-feet, or stop it with chevaux de frize.

A practicable breach, is that where the men may mount and make a lodgement, and ought to be fifteen or twenty fathoms wide. The besiegers make their way to it, by covering themselves with gabions, earth-bags, &c.
