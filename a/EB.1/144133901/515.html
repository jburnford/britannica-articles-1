ACOEMETÆ

ACOEMETÆ, or ACOEMETI, in church history, or men who lived without sleep; a set of monks who chaunted the divine service night and day in their places of worship. They divided themselves into three bodies, who alternately succeeded one another, so that their churches were never silent. This practice they founded upon the precept, Pray without ceasing.
