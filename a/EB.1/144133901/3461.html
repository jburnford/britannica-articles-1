AWL

article · 230 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AWL, among shoe-makers, an instrument wherewith holes are bored through the leather, to facilitate the stitching or sewing the same. The blade of the awl is usually a little flat and bended, and the point ground to an acute angle.
