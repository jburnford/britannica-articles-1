ANCHOR

ANCHOR, in architecture, a sort of carving, something resembling an anchor. It is commonly placed as part of the enrichments of the boudins of capitals of the Tuscan, Doric, and Ionic orders; and also of the the boudins of bed-mouldings of the Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian cornices; anchors and eggs being carved alternately through the whole building.
