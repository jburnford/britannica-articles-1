ACHIOTTE

article · 330 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACHIOTTE, an American drug, used in dyeing and in chocolate. It is produced from the mitella, a tree which grows in North America. Between the small filaments or leaves of this tree, little grains of a vermilion colour are found, which the Indians make into cakes, and send in this form to Europe; it is supposed to promote urine.
