ATOMICAL philosophy

sub_entry · 376 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: EPICUREAN

ATOMICAL philosophy, or the doctrine of atoms, a system which, from the hypothesis that atoms are endowed with gravity and motion, accounted for the origin and formation of things. This philosophy was first broached by Moschus, some time before the Trojan war; but was much cultivated and improved by Epicurus, whence it is denominated the Epicurean philosophy. See EPICUREAN.
