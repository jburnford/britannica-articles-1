BIDENTAL

article · 345 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BIDENTAL, in Roman antiquity, a place blasted with lightning, which was immediately consecrated by an haruspex, with the sacrifice of a bidens. This place was afterwards accounted sacred, and it was unlawful to enter it, or to tread upon it; for which reason it was commonly surrounded with a ditch, wall, hedge, ropes, &c.
