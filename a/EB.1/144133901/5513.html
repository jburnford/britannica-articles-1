BURMANNIA

article · 308 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BURMANNIA, in botany, a genus of the hexandria monogynia class. The calix is shaped like a prism, coloured and divided into three segments, with membranaceous angles; the petals are three; the capsule is three-celled; and the seeds are very small. There are only two species, none of them natives of Britain.
