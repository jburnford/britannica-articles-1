BOROUGH, in Scots law, is a body corporate made up of the inhabitants of a certain tract of ground erected by the sovereign, and endowed with a limited jurisdiction, and certain privileges. They are divided into boroughs royal, of regality, and of barony. See LAW, tit. Inferior Judges and Courts of Scotland.
