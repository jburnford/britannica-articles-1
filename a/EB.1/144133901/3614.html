BALE

BALE, in commerce: Any goods packed up in cloth, and corded round very tight, in order to keep them from breaking, or preserve them from the weather, is called a bale.

A bale of cotton yarn is from three to four hundred weight; of raw silk, is from one to four hundred; of lockram or dowlafs, either three, three and a half, or four pieces.
