ARBITRARY punishment

sub_entry · 347 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ARBITRARY punishment, in Scots law, denotes such punishments as are by statute left to the discretion of the judge. It is a general rule in arbitrary punishments, that the judge cannot inflict death. Hence all punishments that are not capital have acquired the name of arbitrary punishments, even although they be expressly pointed out by statute.
