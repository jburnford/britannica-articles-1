APPRYSING

Cross-references: SCOTS LAW

APPRYSING, in Scots law, the name of that action by which a creditor formerly carried off the estate of his debtor for payment. It is now abolished, and adjudications are appointed in place of it. See SCOTS LAW, title, Apprysings and Adjudications.
