AXUNGIA vitri

sub_entry · 346 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AXUNGIA vitri, SANDIVER, or SALT of glass, a kind of salt which separates from the glass while it is in fusion. It is of an acrimonious and biting taste. The farriers use it for cleansing the eyes of horses. It is also made use of for cleansing the teeth; and is sometimes applied to running ulcers, the herpes, or the itch, by way of defecative.
