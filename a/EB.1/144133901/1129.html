AGONOTHEA

article · 254 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AGONOTHEA, or AGONOTHETES, in Grecian antiquity, was the president or superintendent of the sacred games; who not only defrayed the expenses attending them, but inspected the manners and discipline of the athletes, and adjudged the prizes to the victors.
