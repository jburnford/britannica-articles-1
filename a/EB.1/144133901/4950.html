BOULETTE

article · 257 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BOULETTE, in the menage. A horse is called boulette, when the fetlock, or postern-joint, bends forward, and out of its natural situation; whether through violent riding, or by reason of being too short jointed, in which case the least fatigue will bring it.
