BREAD-ROOM, in a ship, that destined to hold the bread, or biscuit.

The boards of the bread-room should be well joined and caulked, and even lined with tin plates, or mats. It is also proper to warm it well with charcoal, for several days before the biscuit is put into it; since nothing is more injurious to the bread than moisture.
