AMPHICTYONS

AMPHICTYONS, in Grecian antiquity, an assembly composed of deputies from the different states of Greece, and resembling, in some measure, the diet of the German empire.

The amphictyons met regularly at Delphi twice a year, viz. in spring and autumn, and decided all differences between any of the Grecian states, their determinations being held sacred and inviolable.
