The value or price of the goods received and delivered in barter being always equal, it is obvious, that the product of the quantities received and delivered, multiplied into their respective rates, will be equal.

Hence arises a rule which may be used with advantage in working several questions; namely, Multiply the given quantity and rate of the one commodity, and the product divided by the rate of the other commodity quotes the quantity sought; or divided by the quantity quotes the rate.
