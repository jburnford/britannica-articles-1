AMMANNIA

article · 309 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMMANNIA, in botany, a genus of the tetrandria monogynia class. The corolla consists of four petals inserted into the calix, which has eight teeth. The capsule has four cells. There are three species of ammannia, viz. the latifolia, and ramiflor, both natives of America; and the baccifera, a native of China.
