CARTHAMUS, or BASTARD-SAFFRON

CARTHAMUS, or BASTARD-SAFFRON, in botany, a genus of the syngenesia polygamia æqualis class. The calix is ovated, and imbricated with foliaceous scales of an oval shape. There are nine species, none of them natives of Britain. The feeds are purgative; but operate slowly, and disorder the bowels.
