CLOISTER, an habitation surrounded with walls, and inhabited by religious.

In a more general sense, it is used for a monastery of religious of either sex. In the first sense, it is the principal part of a regular monastery, being a square surrounded with walls or buildings. It is commonly placed between the church, the chapter-house, and refectory, underneath the dormitory.
