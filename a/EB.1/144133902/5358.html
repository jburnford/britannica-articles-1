GRANADO

article · 362 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GRANADO, a hollow ball or shell, of iron or other metal, about two inches and a half in diameter; which, being filled with fine powder, is set on fire by means of a small fusee, fastened to the touch-hole, made of the same composition as that of a bomb: as soon as the fire enters the shell, it bursts into many pieces, much to the damage of all that stand near.
