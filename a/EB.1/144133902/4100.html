ESCHEAT

article · 311 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: SCOTS LAW

ESCHEAT, in Scots law, is that forfeiture which is incurred upon a person's being denounced rebel. It is either single or liferent: single escheat is the forfeiture of the rebel's moveable estate; liferent escheat is the forfeiture of the rents of his heritable estate, during his life. See SCOTS LAW, title 12.
