ELATINE

article · 280 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ELATINE, in botany, a genus of the octandria-tetragynia class. The calix consists of four leaves, and the corolla of four petals; the capsule has four cells and

four depressed valves. There are two species, one of which, viz. the alsinastrum or water-wort, is a native of Britain.
