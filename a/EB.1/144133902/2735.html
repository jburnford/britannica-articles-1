DAMIANISTS

DAMIANISTS, in church-history, a branch of the ancient accephali-feveritæ. They agreed with the catholics in admitting the IVth council, but disowned any distinctions of persons in the Godhead; and professed one single nature, incapable of any difference; and yet they called God, the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost.
