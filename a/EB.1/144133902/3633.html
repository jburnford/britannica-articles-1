DYTISCUS

article · 282 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DYTISCUS, WATER-BEETLE, in zoology, a genus of insects of the order of the coleoptera; the antennæ of which are slender and setaceous, and the hind feet are hairy, and formed for swimming. There are twenty-three species, distinguished by their antennæ, the colour of the elytra, &c.
