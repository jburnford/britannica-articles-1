LYCOPUS

article · 279 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LYCOPUS, in botany, a genus of the diandria monogynia class. The corolla consists of four segments, one of them emarginated; the stamens are distant; and there are four seeds. There are two species; only one of them, viz. the europeus, or water-horehound, is a native of Britain.
