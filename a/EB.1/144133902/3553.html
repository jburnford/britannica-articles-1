DUCKING

article · 324 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DUCKING, plunging in water, a diversion anciently practised among the Goths, by way of exercise; but among the Celts, Franks, and ancient Germans, it was a sort of punishment for persons of scandalous lives.

They were shut up, naked to the shift, in an iron cage, fastened to the yard of a shallop, and ducked several times.
