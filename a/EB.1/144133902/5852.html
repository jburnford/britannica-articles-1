HERETAGE

Cross-references: LAW

HERETAGE, in Scots law, lands, houses, or any immoveable subject, in contradistinction to moveables or moveable subjects. See LAW, tit. 9. It also sometimes signifies such immoveable property as a person succeeds to as heir to another, in contradistinction to that which he himself purchases or acquires in any other manner, called conquest. See LAW, tit. 27.
