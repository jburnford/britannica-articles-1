DATIVE

article · 322 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DATIVE, among grammarians, the third case in the declension of nouns, expressing the relation of a thing to whose profit or loss some other thing is referred. It is called dative, because usually governed by a verb, implying something to be given to some person. In English, the dative is expressed by the signs to or for.
