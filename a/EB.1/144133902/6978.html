LILIUM

LILIUM, in botany, a genus of the hexandria monogynia class. The corolla is bell-shaped, and consists of six petals, with a longitudinal nectariferous line; and the valves of the capsule are connected with a lattice-work of hair. There are nine species, none of them natives of Britain. The root of the white lily is reckoned emollient and suppurative.
