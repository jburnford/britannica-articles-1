GAMELIA

article · 290 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GAMELIA, in Grecian antiquity, a nuptial feast, or rather sacrifice, held in the ancient Greek families on the day before a marriage; thus called, from a custom they had of shaving themselves on this occasion, and presenting their hair to some deity to whom they had particular obligations.
