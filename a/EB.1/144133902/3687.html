ELECTICS, ancient philosophers, who, without attaching themselves to any particular sect, selected whatever appeared to them the best and most rational, from each.

Potamon of Alexandria was the first of the electics: he lived in the reigns of Augustus and Tiberius; and being tired with the scepticism of the Pyrrhonians, he resolved upon a scheme that would allow him to believe something, but without being so implicit as to swallow any entire hypothesis.
