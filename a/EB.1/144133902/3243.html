DIOSMA, in botany, a genus of the pentandria monogynia class. The corolla has five petals; and the nectarium is shaped like a crown, is divided into five segments, and situate above the germen; it has five united capsules; and the seeds are furnished with calyptra. There are nine species, none of them natives of Britain.
