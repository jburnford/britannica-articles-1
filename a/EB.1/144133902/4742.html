FORE-MAST OF A SHIP

article · 269 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FORE-MAST OF A SHIP, a large round piece of timber, placed in her fore-part, or fore-castle, and carrying the fore-sail and fore top-sail yards. Its length is usually \frac{2}{3} of the main-mast, and the fore top gallant mast is \frac{1}{2} the length of the fore-top.
