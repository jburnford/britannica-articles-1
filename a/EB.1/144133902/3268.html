DISCOUNT

DISCOUNT, in commerce, a term among traders, merchants, and bankers. It is used by the two former on occasion of their buying commodities on the usual time of credit, with a condition that the seller shall allow the buyer a certain discount at the rate of so much per cent. per annum, for the time for which the credit is

generally given, upon condition that the buyer pays ready money for such commodities, instead of taking the time of credit.
