CAMPHOR

article · 1,101 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CAMPHOR, or CAMPHIRE, a solid concrete juice extracted from the wood and roots of the laurus camphora, which grows in Japan. The camphor is extracted in the same way by which we extract essential oils. As it first sublimes from the wood, it appears brownish, composed of semipellucid grains mixed with dirt. In this state it is exported by the Dutch, and purified by a second sublimation; after which it is reduced to leaves, probably by fusion in close vessels, and in this form it is sold to us. Pure camphor is very white, pellucid, somewhat unctuous to the touch; of a bitterish, aromatic, acrid taste, yet accompanied with a sense of coolness. It has a fragrant smell, somewhat like that of rosemary, but much stronger. It is totally volatile and inflammable; soluble in vinous spirits, oils, and mineral acids; but not in water, alkaline liquors, or the vegetable acids. Camphor is esteemed one of the most efficacious diaphoretics, and has long been celebrated in fevers, malignant and epidemical distempers. In deliria, where opiates fail of procuring sleep, this medicine frequently succeeds.
