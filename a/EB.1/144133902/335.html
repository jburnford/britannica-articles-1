CANNEL-COAL

article · 431 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CANNEL-COAL, a substance which has a long time, though with very little reason, been confounded, both by authors and druggists, with jet. It is dug up in many parts of England in great abundance, particularly in Lancashire, where it is burnt as common fuel. It is worked into toys and utensils of various kinds, under the name of jet. In medicine, it is said to be good in the colic; but the present practice takes no notice of it.
