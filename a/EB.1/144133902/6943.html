LIBELLULA

article · 351 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LIBELLULA, in the history of insects, a genus of four-winged flies, called in English dragon-flies, or adder-flies; the characters of which are these: The mouth is furnished with jaws; the feelers are shorter than the

breast; and the tail of the male terminates in a kind of hooked forceps. There are 21 species, chiefly distinguished by their colour.
