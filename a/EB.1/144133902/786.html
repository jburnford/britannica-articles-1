CELERES

article · 277 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CELERES, in Roman antiquity, a regiment of bodyguards, belonging to the Roman kings, established by Romulus, and composed of 300 young men, chosen out of the most illustrious Roman families, and approved by the suffrages of the curie of the people, each of which furnished ten.
