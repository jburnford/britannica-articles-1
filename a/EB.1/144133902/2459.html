CRITHMUM

article · 249 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CRITHMUM, in botany, a genus of the pentandria digynia class. The fruit is oval and compressed. There are two species, one of which, viz. the maritimum, or sampire, is a native of Britain; the leaves are said to be stomachic, aperient, and diuretic.
