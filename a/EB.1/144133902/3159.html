DIAUGOPHRAGMIA

article · 349 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIAUGOPHRAGMIA, in natural history, a genus of fossils of the order of septaria, whose partitions, or septa, consist of spar with an admixture of crystal. Of this genus there are three species. 1. A red kind, with brownish-yellow partitions. 2. A brownish yellow kind, with whitish partitions. 3. A bluish-white kind, with straw-coloured partitions.
