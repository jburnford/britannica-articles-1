GEMARA, in Jewish antiquity, a collection of decisions and determinations on the law, written after the Mishna was completed.

It was called gemara, or perfection, because it was considered as so perfect an explication of the law, that after it no further additions could be made, or any thing more desired. It is otherwise called the talmud. See TALMUD.
