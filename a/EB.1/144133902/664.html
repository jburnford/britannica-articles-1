CASTLE, a fortress or place rendered defenceable, either by nature or art.

A castle is a fort, or little citadel. See CITADEL. It frequently signifies with us the principal mansion of noblemen.

In the time of Henry II. there were no less than 1115 castles in England, each of which contained a manor.
