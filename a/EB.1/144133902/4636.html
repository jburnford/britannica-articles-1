FLAME

article · 363 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: FIRE

FLAME, the small parts of an inflammable body, that are set on fire, or briskly agitated and thrown off, with a certain vibrative motion at the surface of that body into the open air: or, in Sir Isaac Newton's words, the flame of a body is only the smoke thereof heated red hot; and the smoke is only the volatile part of the body separated by the fire. See FIRE.
