CATARRH, in medicine, a distillation or defluxion from the head upon the mouth and aspera arteria, and

and through them upon the lungs. See MEDICINE. CATASTASIS, in poetry, the third part of the ancient drama, being that wherein the intrigue, or action, set forth in the epitasis, is supported and carried on, and heightened, till it be ripe for the unravelling in the catastrophe. Scaliger defines it, the full growth of the fable, while things are at a stand in that confusion to which the poet has brought them.
