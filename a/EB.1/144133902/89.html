CÆSTUS

article · 254 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CÆSTUS, or CÆSTUM, was also a kind of girdle, made of wool, which the husband untied for his spouse the first day of marriage, before they went to bed.

This relates to Venus's girdle, which Juno borrowed of her, to entice Jupiter to love her. See CÆSTUS.
