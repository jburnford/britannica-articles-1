GHENT, or GAUNT, a city and capital of Flanders, thirty miles north west of Brussels: E. long. 3^{\circ} 36', N. lat. 51^{\circ}.
It is a large fortified town, twelve miles in circumference, and defended by a citadel; and yet is a place of no great strength, by reason of the vast extent of ground it takes in.
