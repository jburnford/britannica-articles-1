DEPOSITION

Cross-references: SCOTS LAW

DEPOSITION, in Scots law, is a contract by which one commits the custody or possession of any thing to another, to be kept for behoof of the owner, and returned on demand, or at any period specified in the contract. The owner is called the depositor, and the person to whose custody the thing is committed the depositary. See SCOTS LAW, title 20.
