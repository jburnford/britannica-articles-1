DOWNHAM

article · 277 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DOWNHAM, a market-town of Norfolk, ten miles south of Lynn, famous for its good butter; there being a thousand, and sometimes two thousand firkins bought here every Monday, and sent up the river Ouse to Cambridge, from whence it is conveyed to London, in the Cambridge waggons.
