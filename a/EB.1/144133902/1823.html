COMPLEMENT

article · 332 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COMPLEMENT, in geometry, is what remains of a quadrant of a circle, or of 90^{\circ}, after any certain arch has been taken away from it. Thus, if the arch taken away be 40^{\circ}, its complement is 50^{\circ}: because 50+40=90. The sine of the complement of an arch is called the cosine, and that of the tangent the cotangent, &c.
