DECLARATORY action

sub_entry · 241 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: SCOTS LAW

DECLARATORY action, in Scots law, is that by which a pursuer only craves, that some right or privilege shall be declared to belong to him, without demanding the payment or performance of any thing from the defender. See SCOTS LAW, title, 30.
