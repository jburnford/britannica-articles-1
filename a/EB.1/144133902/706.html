CATASTROPHE, in dramatic poetry, the fourth and last part of the ancient drama, or that immediately succeeding the catastasis: Or, according to others, the third only; the whole drama being divided into protasis, epitasis, and catastrophe; or, in the terms of Aristotle, prologue, epilogue, and exode. See EPIC and DRAMATIC compositions.
