ELAPHEBOLIUM

article · 343 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ELAPHEBOLIUM, in Grecian antiquity, the ninth month of the Athenian year, answering to the latter part of February and beginning of March. It consisted of thirty days, and took its name from the festival elaphebolia, kept in this month, in honour of Diana the huntress; on which occasion, a cake made in the form of a deer, was offered to her.
