CARROUSAL

article · 381 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CARROUSAL, a course of horfes and chariots, or a magnificent entertainment exhibited by princes on some public rejoicing. It consists in a cavalcade of several gentlemen richly dressed and equipped, after the manner of ancient cavaliers divided into squadrons, meeting in some public place, and practising julls, tournaments, &c. The last carrousals were in the reign of Lewis XIV.
