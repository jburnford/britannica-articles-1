HARDNESS

article · 295 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HARDNESS, in physiology, that quality in bodies whereby their parts cohere firmly together, so as not to give way to any external impulse, nor yield inwards, without breaking.

In this sense hardness coincides with what on other occasions we call firmness, in opposition to softness and fluidity.
