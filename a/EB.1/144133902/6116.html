HYPOCAUSTUM

article · 276 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HYPOCAUSTUM, among the Greeks and Romans, a subterraneous place, wherein was a furnace to heat the baths.

Another sort of hypocaustum was a kind of kiln, to heat their winter parlours.

Among the moderns, it is that place where the fire is kept that warms a stove or hot-house.
