CANTRED

article · 239 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CANTRED, or CANTREP, signifies an hundred villages, being a British word, compounded of the adjective cant, i. e. hundred, and tref, a town or village. In Wales, some of the countries are divided into cantreds, as in England into hundreds.
