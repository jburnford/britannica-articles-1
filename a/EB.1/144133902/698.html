CATAPHRACTA

article · 255 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CATAPHRACTA, in antiquity, a kind of coat of mail, which covered the soldier from head to foot.

Hence, cataphracti were horsemen armed with the cataphracta, whose horses, as Sallust says, were covered with linen full of iron plates disposed like feathers.
