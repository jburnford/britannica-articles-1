IDYLLION

article · 353 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

IDYLLION, in ancient poetry, is only a diminutive of the word [eidos], and properly signifies any poem of moderate extent, without considering the subject. But as the collection of Theocritus's poems were called idyllia, and the pastoral pieces being by far the best in that collection, the term idyllion seems to be now appropriated to pastoral pieces.
