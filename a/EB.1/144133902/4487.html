NUMA

article · 625 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: SACRIFICE · FASTI

NUMA, when he distributed the year into twelve months, divided the same into the dies festi, dies profesti, and dies intercali.

The festi were again divided into days of sacrifices, banquets, games, and feriae. See SACRIFICE, EPULÆ, LUDI, and FERIÆ.

The profesti were those days allowed to men for the administration of their affairs, whether of a public or private nature: these were divided into fasti, comitiales, comperendini, stati, and praeliares. See FASTI, COMITIALES, &c.

The intercali were days common both to gods and men, some parts of which were allotted to the service of the one, and some to that of the other.
