HELIOCARPUS

article · 263 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HELIOCARPUS, in botany, a genus of the dodecandria digynia class. The calix consists of four leaves; the petals are four; the styli are simple; and the capsule consists of two compressed cells, radiated on each side. There is but one species, a native of America.
