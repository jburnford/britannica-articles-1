CARDIGAN, the capital of Cardiganshire, near the mouth of the river Tivy and the Irish channel, about thirty miles north of Pembroke: W. long. 4^{\circ} 40', N. lat. 52^{\circ} 15'. It gives the title of earl to the noble family of Brudenel, and sends only one member to parliament.
