CALATHUS

article · 253 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CALATHUS, in antiquity, a basket, hamper, or panier of oisters, reeds, or twigs, for women to put their work in, or to gather flowers in.

CALATHUS was also a vessel, or pan, for cheese-curds and milk; also the name of a cup for wine, used in sacrifices.
