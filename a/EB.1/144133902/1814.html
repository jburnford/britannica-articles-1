COMPASS dials

sub_entry · 396 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: DIALING

COMPASS dials, are small horizontal dials, fitted in brass or silver boxes, for the pocket, to shew the hour of the day, by the direction of a needle, that indicates how to place them right, by turning the dial about, till the cock or style stand directly over the needle, and point to the northward: but these can never be very exact, because of the variations of the needle itself. See DIALING.
