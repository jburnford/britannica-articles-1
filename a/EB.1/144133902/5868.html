HERNANDIA

article · 337 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HERNANDIA, in botany, a genus of the monococcia triandria class. The calix of the male has three segments, and the corolla three petals. The calix of the female is entire and truncated; the corolla consists of six petals; and the drupa is hollow, with an open mouth, and a loose kernel. There are two species, both natives of the Indies.
