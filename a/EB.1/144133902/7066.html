LOAMS

article · 308 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LOAMS, in natural history, are defined to be earths composed of dissimilar particles, hard, stiff, dense, and hard and rough to the touch; not easily ductile while moist, readily diffusible in water, and composed of sand and a tough viscous clay. Of these loams, some are whitish, and others brown or yellow.
