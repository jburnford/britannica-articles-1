DIFFUSE, an epithet applied to such writings as are wrote in a prolix manner. Among historians, Sallust Vol. II. No. 44.

is reckoned sententious, and Livy diffuse. Thus also among the orators, Demosthenes is close and concise; Cicero, on the other hand, is diffuse.
