HYDROCHARIS

article · 426 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HYDROCHARIS, the LITTLE WATER-LILY, in botany, a genus of the dicotia decandria class. The spatha of the male consists of two leaves, the calix of three segments, and the corolla of three petals. The calix of the female consists of three segments, and the corolla of three petals; it has six styli; and the capsule has six cells and many seeds. There is but one species, viz. the morfus ranæ, or frog bit, a native of Britain.
