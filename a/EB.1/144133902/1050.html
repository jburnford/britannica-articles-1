CHELIDONIUS lapis, in natural-history, a stone said by the ancients to be found in the stomachs of young swallows, and greatly cried up for its virtues in the falling-sickness; but from their description, it appears to be only a species of lycodontes, or bufonite. See LYCODONTES, and BUFONITE.
