DECLINATURE of judges

sub_entry · 305 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: SCOTS LAW

DECLINATURE of judges, in Scots law, declining the jurisdiction of a judge, or refusing to acquiesce in his judgment from any legal obligation to the judge himself, the incompetency of his jurisdiction to the nature of the action, or upon the privilege of the objector or decliner. See SCOTS LAW, title 2.
