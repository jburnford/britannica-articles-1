FLEAM

article · 473 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FLEAM, in surgery and farriery, an instrument for letting a man or horse blood. A case of fleams, as it is called by farriers, comprehends six sorts of instruments; two hooked ones, called drawers, and used for cleaning wounds; a pen knife; a sharp pointed lancet, for making incisions; and two fleams, one sharp and the other broad pointed. These last are somewhat like the point of a lancet, fixed in a flat handle, only no longer than is just necessary to open the vein.
