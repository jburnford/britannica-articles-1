PARANYMPH

article · 316 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PARANYMPH, among the ancients, the person who waited on the bridegroom, and directed the nuptial solemnities; called also pronubus, and auspex, because the ceremonies began by taking auspicia. As the paranymph officiated only on the part of the bridegroom, a woman called pronuba officiated on the part of the bride.
