STYLE, in matters of language, a particular manner of expressing one's thoughts agreeably to the rules of syntax; or, as F. Buffier more accurately defines it, the manner wherein the words, constructed according to the laws of syntax, are arranged among themselves, suitably to the genius of the language. See COMPOSITION.
