TAMARIX

article · 324 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TAMARIX, in botany, a genus of the pentandria trigynia class. The calix consists of five segments, and the corolla of five petals: the capsule has one cell, and three valves; and the seeds are pappous. There are two species, none of them natives of Britain.

The bark and leaves of the tamarix-tree are moderately astringent.
