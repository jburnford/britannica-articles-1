PAULICIANS

article · 312 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PAULICIANS, Christian heretics of the VIIth century, disciples of one Constantine, a native of Armenia, and a favourer of the errors of Manes; who, as the name Manichees was become odious to all nations, gave those of his sect the title of Paulicians, on pretence that they followed only the doctrine of St Paul.
