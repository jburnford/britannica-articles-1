THALICTRUM

article · 308 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

THALICTRUM, in botany, a genus of the polyandria polygynia class. The corolla consists of five petals; it has no corolla; and the seeds are naked. There are 14 species, three of them natives of Britain, viz. the flavum, or meadow-rue; the minus, or lesser meadow-rue; and the alpinum, or mountain meadow-rue.
