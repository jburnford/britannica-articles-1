SADDLE

article · 294 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SADDLE, is a seat upon a horse's back, contrived for the convenience of the rider.

A hunting saddle is composed of two bows, two bands, fore-bolsters, pannels, and saddle-traps; and the great saddle has, besides these parts, corks, hind-bolsters, and a trousequin.

The pommel is common to both.
