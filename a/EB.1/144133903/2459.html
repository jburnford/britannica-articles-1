Toga PRETEXTA

sub_entry · 265 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Toga PRETEXTA, among the ancient Romans, a long white gown, with a border of purple round the edges, and worn by children of quality till the age of puberty, viz. by the boys till seventeen, when they changed it for the toga virilis; and by the girls till marriage.
