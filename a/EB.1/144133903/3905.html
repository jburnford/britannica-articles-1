SHEATHING

article · 270 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SHEATHING, in the sea-language, is the casing that part of a ship which is to be under water, with fir board of an inch thick; first laying hair and tar, mixed together, under the boards, and then nailing them on, in order to prevent worms from eating the ship's bottom.
