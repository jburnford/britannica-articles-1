TAMUS

article · 281 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TAMUS, in botany, a genus of the dicœcia hexandria class. The calix of both male and female consists of six segments; neither of them have any corolla; the style is trifid; and the berry has three cells, containing two seeds. There are two species, none of them natives of Britain.
