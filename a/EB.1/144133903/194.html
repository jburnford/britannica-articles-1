MARTYNIA

article · 248 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MARTYNIA, in botany, a genus of the didynamia angiosperma class. The calix consists of five segments; the corolla is ringent; and the capsule is woody, with a hooked beak, two valves, and three cells. There are two species, both natives of America.
