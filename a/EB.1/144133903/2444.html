PRESBYTA

article · 275 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PRESBYTA, in optics, a person whose eyes being flat, can see distant objects distinctly, but those near confusedly: which defect of sight got this appellation, because old people are naturally subject to it.

Spectacles, or convex glasses, are the only remedy for this defect.
