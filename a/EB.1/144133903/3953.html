SIDERIA

article · 634 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SIDERIA, in natural history, the name of a genus of crystals, used to express those altered in their figure by particles of iron. These are of a rhomboidal form, and composed only of six planes. Of this genus there are four known species: 1. A colourless, pellucid, and thin one, found in considerable quantities among the iron ores of the forest of Dean in Gloucestershire, and in other the like places. 2. A dull, thick, and brown one, not uncommon in the same places with the former. And, 3. A black and very glossy kind, a fossil of very great beauty, found in the same place with the others, as also in Leicestershire and Sussex.
