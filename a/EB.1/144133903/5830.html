WALE-REARED

article · 382 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

WALE-REARED, on board a ship, a name the seamen give to a ship, which, after she comes to her bearing, is built strait up: this way of building, though it does not look well, nor is, as the seamen term it, ship-shapen; yet it has this advantage, that a ship is thereby more roomy within board, and becomes thereby a wholesome ship at sea, especially if her bearing be well laid out.
