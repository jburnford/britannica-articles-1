PLANTAGO

PLANTAGO, in botany, a genus of the tetrandria monogynia class. The calix and corolla has each four segments; the stamens are very long; and the capsule is bicellular. There are 21 species, eight of them natives of Britain.

The root, leaves, and seeds of plantain, are reckoned cooling and astringent. It is likewise accounted a great healer of fresh wounds.
