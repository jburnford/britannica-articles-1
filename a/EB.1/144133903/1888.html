PENTECOST, a solemn festival of the Jews, so called because it was celebrated on the fiftieth day after the sixteenth of the month Nisan, which was the second day of the passover. See PASSOVER.

The feast of pentecost was instituted in memory of the Law's being given on the fiftieth day after the Israelites came out of Egypt.
