NOTARY

article · 499 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

NOTARY, signifies a person, usually some scrivener, who takes notes, or frames short draughts, of contracts, obligations, charter-parties, or other writings. At present we call him a notary-public who publicly attests deeds, or writings, in order to make them authentic in another nation: but he is principally employed in business concerning merchants, as making pro-ests of bills of exchange &c. And noting a bill, is where he goes to take notice of a merchant's refusal to accept or pay the same.
