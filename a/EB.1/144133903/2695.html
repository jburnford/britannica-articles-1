PYRUS

article · 301 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PYRUS, in botany, a genus of the icosaandria pentagynia class. The calix consists of 5 segments, and the corolla of five petals; the apple has five cells, containing many seeds. There are four species, two of them natives of Britain, viz. the communis, or wild pear-tree; and the malus, or crab-apple.
