SHERARDIA, in botany, a genus of plants belonging

to the tetrandria monogynia class. The corolla consists of one funnel-shaped petal; and there are two seeds having three teeth. The species are three, only one of them, viz. the arvensis, or little field-madder, a native of Britain.
