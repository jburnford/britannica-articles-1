PRIVILEGE, in law, some peculiar benefit granted to certain persons or places, contrary to the usual course of the law.

Privileges are said to be personal or real. Personal privileges are such as are extended to peers, ambassadors, members of parliament and of the convocation, and their menial servants, &c. See PEER, EMBASSADOR, PARLIAMENT, &c.
