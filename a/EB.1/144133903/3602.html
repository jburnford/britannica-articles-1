SCARBOROUGH, a borough and port town of Yorkshire, thirty-seven miles north-east of York.

It sends two members to parliament, and is famous for a medicinal spring, which has been the subject of great contests and disputes among the physical people; all allowing it considerable virtues, but some attributing them to one ingredient, others to another.
