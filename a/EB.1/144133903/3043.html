REPELLENTS, in medicine, remedies which drive back a morbid humour into the mass of blood from which it was unduly secreted.

The most remarkable in the class of repellents are the white of an egg, the lapis calaminaris, litharge of gum, red-lead, tully, pampolix, house-leek, putty, and cow-web.
