SUPPURATION, the second way wherein an inflammation terminates, being a conversion of the inspissated blood and the soft adjacent parts, as the vessels and fat, into pus, or matter: which disorder, when it has not yet found an opening, is generally called an abscess. See MEDICINE and SURGERY.
