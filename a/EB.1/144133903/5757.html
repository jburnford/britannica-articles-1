URACHUS

article · 365 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

URACHUS, a membranous canal in the foetus of quadrupeds in general, of a pyramidal figure, extended immediately from the fundus of the bladder to the navel; and after passing through this, it is by degrees enlarged, and makes its way into the allantois at right-angles each way, or nearly so, and conveys the urine from the bladder into the cavity of this membrane.
