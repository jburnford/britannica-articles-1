STATUTE, in its general sense, signifies a law, ordinance, decree, &c. See LAW, &c.

Statute, in our laws and customs, more immediately signifies an act of parliament made by the three estates of the realm: and such statutes are either general, of which the courts at Westminster must take notice, without pleading them; or they are special and private, which last must be pleaded.
