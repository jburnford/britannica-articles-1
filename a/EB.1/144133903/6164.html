ZAFFER

ZAFFER, or ZAFER, in chemistry, the name of a blue substance, of the hardness and form of a stone; and generally supposed to be a native fossil.

It is in reality, however, a preparation of cobalt; the calx of that mineral being mixed with powdered flints and wetted with water to bring it into this form.
