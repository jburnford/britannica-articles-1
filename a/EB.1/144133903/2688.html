PYROLA

article · 348 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PYROLA, in botany, a genus of the decandria monogynia class. The calix consists of five segments, and the corolla of five petals; and the capsule has five cells. There are six species, three of them natives of Britain, viz. the rotundifolia, or common winter-green; the minor, or lesser winter-green; and the secunda, or dented leaved winter-green.
