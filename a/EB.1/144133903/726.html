MUSA, the PLAIN-TREE, in botany, a genus of the polygamia monœcia class. The calix is a spatha: the corolla consists of two petals; one of them straight and toothed; and the other is concave, short, and furnished with a nectarium. It has six filaments, and one stylus. There are four species, all natives of the Indies.
