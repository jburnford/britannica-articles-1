URANOSCOPUS

article · 322 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

URANOSCOPUS, in ichthyology, a genus belonging to the order of jugulares. The head is large, rough, and depressed, the upper jaw being shorter than the under one; there are five dentated rays in the membrane of the gills; and the anus is in the middle of the body. There is but one species, found in the Mediterranean sea.
