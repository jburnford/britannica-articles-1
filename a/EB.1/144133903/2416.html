PRECIPITANT

article · 375 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: CHEMISTRY

PRECIPITANT, in chemistry, is applied to any liquor, which, when poured on a solution, separates what is dissolved, and makes it precipitate, or fall to the bottom of the vessel. See CHEMISTRY.

The term precipitant is also used, in medicine, to denote any remedy that moderates the heat of the blood, by separating, as is supposed, any heterogeneous matter contained therein.
