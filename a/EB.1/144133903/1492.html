OXFORD

article · 381 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

OXFORD, the capital of Oxfordshire, and the see of a bishop; it is situated at the confluence of the Isis and Cherwell, fifty-five miles west of London: W. long. 1^{\circ} 15', and N. lat. 51^{\circ} 45'.

Oxford is most remarkable on account of its university, which consists of twenty colleges and five halls; this city sends two members to parliament, and the university as many.
