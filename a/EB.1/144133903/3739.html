SEEDY

article · 345 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SEEDY, in the brandy trade, a term used by the dealers, to denote a fault that is found in several parcels of French brandy, which renders them unsaleable. The French suppose that these brandies obtain the flavour which they express by this name from weeds that grow among the vines from whence the wine of which this brandy is made was pressed.
