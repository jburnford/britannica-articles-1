TUNNAGE

article · 369 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TUNNAGE is used for a custom or impost, payable to the crown, for goods and merchandise imported or exported, and is to be paid after a certain rate for every tun thereof. This duty, as well as that of poundage, was first granted for life to king Charles II and has been continued in the same manner to his royal successors, down to his present majesty king George III.
