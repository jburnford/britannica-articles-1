PORT-GREVE

article · 266 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PORT-GREVE, or PORT-GRAVE, was formerly the principal magistrate of maritime towns. The chief magistrate of London was anciently called by this name, till Richard I. caused the city to be governed by two bailiffs; soon after which king John granted the city a mayor.
