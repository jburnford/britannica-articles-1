ORYZA, RICE, in botany, a genus of the hexandria digynia class. The calix is a double-valved glume, with one flower; and the corolla consists of two equal valves. There is but one species.

This plant is cultivated in vast abundance in the East, as also in Carolina, for food. It is said to be good in dysenteries, diarrhoeas, &c.
