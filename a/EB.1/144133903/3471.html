SAMBUCUS

article · 323 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SAMBUCUS, in botany, a genus of the pentandria trigynia class. The calix and corolla consist of five segments; and the berry contains three seeds. There are four species, two of them natives of Britain, viz. the nigra, or common elder; and the ebulus, or dwarf elder.

The inner green bark of this shrub is gently cathartic.
