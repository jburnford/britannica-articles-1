MODE

article · 398 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MODE, in music, is defined to be a particular manner of constituting the octave; or, it is the melodious constitution of the octave, as it consists of seven essential sounds, besides the key or fundamental.

A mode, then, is the particular order of the continuous degrees of an octave; the fundamental note whereof may be called the key, as it signifies that principal note which regulates the rest.
