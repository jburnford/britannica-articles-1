RECHABITES

article · 392 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

RECHARITIES, a kind of religious order among the ancient Jews, instituted by Joshua the son of Rechab, comprehending only his own family and posterity. Their founder prescribed them three things: first, not to drink any wine; secondly, not to build any houses, but to dwell in tents; and thirdly, not to sow any corn, or plant vines. These rules the Rechabites observed with great strictness.
