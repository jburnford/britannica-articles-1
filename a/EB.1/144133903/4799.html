TAGETES

article · 291 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TAGETES, in botany, a genus of the syngenesia polygamia superflua class. The receptacle is naked; the pappus has five straight awns; the calix consists of one tubulous leaf with five teeth; and there are five persistent floccules in the radius. The species are three, all natives of America.
