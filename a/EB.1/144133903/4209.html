SPAW

SPAW, a town of Germany, in the circle of Westphalia, and bishopric of Liege, situated seventeen miles south east of Liege, famous for its mineral waters ever since the time of the Romans, of which there are still great quantities sent abroad to all parts of Europe.

Spaw waters are the lightest and most subtle of all the mineral waters. One very remarkable virtue of this water is, that it greatly relieves in all disorders of the kidneys, ureters, and bladder, whether occasioned by stone, gravel, or ulcerations. It possesses, beside, all the vir-

tues of the other mineral waters, and is of the greatest service in edulcorating sharp, and dividing viscous humours, and removing all diseases arising from these causes, by disposing them to pass off by proper emunctories.
