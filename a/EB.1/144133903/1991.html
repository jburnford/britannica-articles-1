PETROBRUSSIANS

article · 397 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PETROBRUSSIANS, in church-history, a religious sect which arose in France and the Netherlands, about the year 1126, so called from their leader Peter Bruys. They denied that children, before the use of reason, can be justified by baptism. They also condemned all places of public worship, crosses, crucifixes; and are said to have rejected the sacrament of the eucharist, and prayers for the dead.
