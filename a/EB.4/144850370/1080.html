AGUIRRA

article · 418 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AGUIRRA (Joseph Sænz de), a Benedictine, and one of the most learned men of the 17th century, was born March 24, 1630. He was censor and secretary of the supreme council of the inquisition in Spain, and interpreter of the scriptures in the university of Salamanca. He printed three volumes in folio upon Philosophy, a Commentary upon Aristotle's ten books of Ethics, and other pieces. He died at Rome, August 19, 1699.
