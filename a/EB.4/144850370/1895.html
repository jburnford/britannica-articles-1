ANAPHE

ANAPHE, (anc. geogr.) an island spontaneously emerging out of the Cretan sea, near Thera, (Pliny, Strabo.) Now called Nausis. Its name is from the sudden appearance of the new moon to the Argonauts in a storm, (Apollonius.) Anaphous, an epithet of Apollo, who was worshipped there. Anaphai, the people.
