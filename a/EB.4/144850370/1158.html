AKISSAT

article · 655 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AKISSAT, the ancient Thyatira, a city in Nattolia, in Asia, situated in a plain 18 miles broad, which produces plenty of cotton and grain. The inhabitants, who are reckoned to be about 5000, are said to be all Mahometans, and not one Christian among them, except a few slaves. The houses are built of nothing but earth or turf dried in the sun, and are very low and ill contrived: but there are six or seven mosques, which are all of marble. There are remarkable inscriptions on marble in several parts of the town, which are part of the ruins of ancient Thyatira. It is seated on the river Hermus, 50 miles from Pergamos. E. Long. 28. 30. N. Lat. 38. 50.
