ARÆOMETER, an instrument to measure the gravity

vity of liquors; which is usually made of a thin glass ball, with a taper neck, sealed at the top, there being first as much mercury put into it as will keep it swimming in an exact posture. The neck is divided into two parts, which are numbered, that so, by the depths of its descent into any liquor, its lightness may be known by these divisions.
