AGAPETE

article · 264 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AGAPETE, in church-history, a name given to those young maidens who frequented the company of ecclesiastics out of a motive of piety and charity. This practice afterwards degenerated into an occasion of libertinism, inasmuch that agapeta became a term of reproach.
