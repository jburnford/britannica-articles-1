ADELIA

article · 418 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ADELIA, a genus of the monadelphia order, belonging to the dicotyledon class of plants. Of this genus there are three species; the bernardia, the ricinella, and acidoton, for which we have no proper names in English. They are natives of Jamaica, and are akin to the ricinus or croton, and may be propagated in hot-beds from seeds procured from Jamaica; but they have little beauty, and are therefore seldom cultivated.
