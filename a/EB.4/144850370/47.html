ABAS

ABAS (Schah) the Great, was third son of Codabendi, 7th king of Persia, of the race of the Sophis. Succeeding to his father at 18, in 1585, he found the affairs of Persia at a low ebb, occasioned by the conquests of the Turks and Tartars. He regained several of the provinces they had seized; but death put a stop to his victories in 1629, after a reign of 44 years. He was the greatest prince that had reigned in Persia for many ages; and it was he who made Isfahan the metropolis of Persia: his memory is held in the highest veneration among the Persians.
