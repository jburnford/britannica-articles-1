ANACREONTIC VERSE

article · 251 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANACREONTIC VERSE, in ancient poetry, a kind of verse, so called from its being much used by the poet Anacreon. It consists of three feet and an half, usually spondees and iambuses, and sometimes anapests: Such is that of Horace, Lydia, dic per omnes.
