ALERIA, ALALIA, or ALARIA

sub_entry · 320 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ALERIA, ALALIA, or ALARIA, (anc. geog.) a town of Corsica, situated near the middle of the east side of the island, on an eminence, near the mouth of the river Rotanus mentioned by Ptolemy; built by the Phœcians, (Diodorus Siculus.) Afterwards Sylla led a colony thither. It is now in ruins, and called Aleria Distrutta.
