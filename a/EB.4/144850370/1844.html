ANACLINTERIA

article · 370 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANACLINTERIA, in antiquity, a kind of pillows on the dining-bed, wherein the guests used to lean. The ancient tricliniary beds had four tracina, one at the head, another at the feet, a third at the back, and a fourth at the breast. That on which the head lay, was properly called by the Greeks anacliniterion, or anacliniterion; by the Romans falerum, sometimes plateus.
