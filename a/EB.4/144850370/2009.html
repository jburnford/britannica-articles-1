ANDRONA

article · 287 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANDRONA, in ancient writers, denotes a street, or public place, where people met and conversed together. In some writers, androna is more expressly used for the space between two houses; in which sense, the Greeks also use the term androna, for the way or passage between two apartments.
