ABELLINUM

article · 299 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABELLINUM, anciently a town of the Hirpini, a people of Apulia; distant about a mile from the rivulet Sabbato, between Beneventum and Salernum. Pliny calls the inhabitants Abellinates, with the epithet Protopi, to distinguish them from the Abellinates Marisi. Now Avellino. E. Long. 15. 20. Lat. 21.
