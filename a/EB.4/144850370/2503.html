APOSTROPHE

article · 296 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

APOSTROPHE, in rhetoric, a figure by which the orator, in a vehement commotion, turns himself on all sides, and applies to the living and dead, to angels and to men, to rocks, groves, &c. Thus Adam, in Milton's Paradise Lost:

O Woods, O fountains, hillocks, dales, and bowers,
With other echo, &c.
