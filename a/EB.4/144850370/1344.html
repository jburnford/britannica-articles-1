ALEMBROTH

article · 400 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: CHEMISTRY

ALEMBROT, in the writings of the alchemists, a word used for a sort of fixed alkaline salt, which had the power of the famous alkahest, in dissolving bodies, opening the pores of most or all known substances, and thence, as well as by destroying sulphurs, promoting the separation of metals from their ores.—It is also used for a compound of corrosive mercury and sal ammoniac. See CHEMISTRY, p. 337.
