ASTEISM

article · 285 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ASTEISM, in rhetoric, a genteel irony, or handsome way of deriding another. Such, e. gr. is that of Virgil: Qui Bætiæ non est, aut tua carmina, Mari, &c.

Diomed places the characteristic of this figure, or species of irony, in that it is not gross and rustic, but ingenious and polite.
