ABUCARAS

article · 552 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABUCARAS (Theodorus), metropolitan of Caria in the ninth century, was remarkable for his zeal in defending what he believed to be the truth, and was the author of above forty controversial treatises against the Saracens, Jews, and reputed heretics. This metropolitan at first embraced the doctrines of Photius; for which, begging pardon of the council of Constantinople in 869, he was restored to the communion of the church, and obtained a seat in the council. His works are inserted in the Supplement of the Bibliothèque des Pères, the Paris edition.
