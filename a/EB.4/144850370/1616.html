AMANUS, a mountain of Syria, separating it from Cilicia; a branch of mount Taurus, (Cicero, Strabo, Pliny); extending chiefly eastward, from the sea of Cilicia, to the Euphrates: now called Monte Negro, or rather Montagna Neres, by the inhabitants; that is, the watery mountain, as abounding in springs and rivulets.
