APPARITOR

article · 280 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

APPARITOR, among the Romans, a general term to comprehend all attendants of judges and magistrates appointed to receive and execute their orders. Apparitor, in England, is a messenger that serves the process of a spiritual court, or a beadle in an university who carries the mace.
