ACHATES, (Sil. Italicus); a river of Sicily, now the Drillo, (Cluverius); which runs from north to south, almost parallel with, and at no great distance from, the Gela; and rises in the north of the territory of Noto. It gave name to the Achates, or Agate, said to be first found there.
