ANGERONA

article · 484 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANGERONA, in mythology, the name of a pagan deity whom the Romans prayed to for the cure of a distemper called the quinzy; in Latin, angina. Pliny calls her the goddess of silence and calmness of mind; who banishes all uneasiness and melancholy. She is represented with her mouth covered, to denote patience and refraining from complaints. Her statue was set up, and sacrificed to, in the temple of the goddess Volupta, to shew that a patient enduring of affliction leads to pleasure.
