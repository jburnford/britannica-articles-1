TOGA

article · 505 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TOGA, in Roman antiquity, a wide woollen gown or mantle; which seems to have been of a semi-circular form, without sleeves; differing both in richness and largeness, according to the circumstances of the wearer, and used only upon occasion of appearing in public.

Every body knows that the toga was the distinguished mark of a Roman: hence, the jus togæ, or privilege of a Roman citizen; i. e. the right of wearing a Roman habit, and of taking, as they explain it, fire and water through the Roman empire.
