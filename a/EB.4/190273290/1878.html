TYRANT

article · 372 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TYRANT, among the ancients, denoted simply a king or monarch; but the ill use which several persons invented with that sacred character made of it, has altered the import of the word; and tyrant now carries with it the idea of an unjust or cruel prince, who invades the people's liberty, and rules in a more despotic manner than the laws of nature or of the country allow.
