SHELLS

article · 590 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SHELLS, in gunnery, are hollow iron balls to throw out of mortars or howitzers, with a fuse-hole of about an inch diameter, to load them with powder, and to receive the fuze. The bottom, or part opposite to the fuze, is made thicker than the rest, that the fuze may fall uppermost. But in small elevations this does not always happen, nor indeed is it necessary; for, let the shell

shell fall as it will, the fuse sets fire to the powder within, which bursts the shell, and causes great devastation. The shells had much better be of an equal thickness; for then they burst into more pieces.
