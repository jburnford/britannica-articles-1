TANGIER

TANGIER, a port-town of Africa, in the empire of Morocco and kingdom of Fez, situated at the entrance of the Straits of Gibraltar, in W. Long. 7o. N. Lat. 35. 40. In 1662, this place belonged to the Portuguese, and was given to king Charles II. upon his marriage with the Infanta of Portugal: but he, growing weary of the charge of keeping it, caused it to be blown up and destroyed in 1684; ever since which time it has been only a poor fishing town. Anciently it was called Tingis, and gave name to the province of Mauritania Tingitana.
