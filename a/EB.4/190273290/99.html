SEIGNIORY

article · 367 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SEIGNIORY, is borrowed from the French seigneurie, i. e. dominatus, imperium, principatus; and signifies with us a manor or lordship, seignory de fohemans. Seignory in gross, seems to be the title of him who is not lord by means of any manor, but immediately in his own person; as tenure in capite, whereby one holds of the king as of his crown, is seignory in gross.
