STAMINA

article · 314 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

STAMINA, in botany, are those upright filaments which, on opening a flower, we find within the co-

rolla surrounding the pistillum. According to Linnaeus, they are the male organs of generation, whose office it is to prepare the pollen. Each stamen consists of two distinct parts, viz. the FILAMENTUM and the ANTERA.
