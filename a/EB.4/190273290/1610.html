TORIES, in the history of England, a faction or party opposed to the whigs. See WHIGS.—The tories are great sticklers for the prerogatives of the crown, as the whigs are for the liberties and privileges of the people: though, in truth, the principles of the moderate people of both parties do not greatly differ.
