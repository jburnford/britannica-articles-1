ALDER, (Encycl.) The bark of the black berry-bearing alder is said to be the most certain purge for the horned cattle in obstinate constipations of the bowels. Gunner says, that horses do not eat the leaves, but that cows sometimes will, and that it greatly increases the quantity of the milk.
