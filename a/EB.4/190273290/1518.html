TICINUS, (anc. geog.) a river in Infurbia, rising in mount Adula, traversing the Lacus Verbana southwards, and falling into the Po near Ticinum. Between this river and the Po Hannibal gained his first victory over the Romans under P. Scipio. The general himself escaped with the utmost difficulty, and that by the bravery of his son the first Scipio Africanus. Now the Ticino, rising in mount Godard, running south through the Lago Maggiore and Milan, by Pavia, into the Po.
