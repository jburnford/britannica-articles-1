TORRICELLIAN EXPERIMENT

TORRICELLIAN EXPERIMENT, a famous experiment made by Torricelli, a disciple of the great Galileo, by which he demonstrated the pressure of the atmosphere in opposition to the doctrines of suction, &c. finding that pressure able to support only a certain length of mercury, or any other fluid, in an inverted glass tube.
