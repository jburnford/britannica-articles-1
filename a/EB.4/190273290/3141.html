MASQUE, or MASK

MASQUE, or MASK, a cover for the face, contrived with apertures for the eyes and mouth; originally worn chiefly by women of condition, either to preserve their complexion from the weather, or out of modesty, to prevent their being known.

Poppæa, wife of Nero, is said to be the first inventor of the masque; which she did to guard her complexion from the sun and weather, as being the most delicate woman, with regard to her person, that has been known.

Theatrical masques were in common use both among the

Brantome observes, that the common use of modern masques was not introduced till towards the end of the 16th century.
