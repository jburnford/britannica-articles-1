SHROVE-TUESDAY, is the Tuesday after Quinquagesima Sunday, or the day immediately preceding the first Lent; being so called from the Saxon word

shrive,

Shrouds shrive, which signifies to confess, as having been employed by the people in the time of Popery in confessing their sins in order to receive the sacrament, and thereby qualify themselves for a more religious observation of Lent.
