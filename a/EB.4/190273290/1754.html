TRIPOD

article · 308 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TRIPOD, in antiquity, a famed sacred seat or stool, supported by three feet, whereon the priests and sibils were placed to render oracles. It was on the tripod that the gods were said to inspire the Pythias

with that divine fury and enthusiasm wherewith they were seized at the delivery of their predictions.
