VENTRILLOQUOUS

article · 299 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

VENTRILLOQUOUS, a term applied to persons who speak inwardly; having a peculiar art of forming speech, by drawing the air into the lungs, so that the voice, proceeding out of the thorax, to a by-stander seems to come from a distance. The word is compounded of venter, "belly;" and loquor, "I speak."
