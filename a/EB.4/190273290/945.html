STRAND

article · 305 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

STRAND (Saxon), any shore or bank of a sea or great river. Hence the street in the west suburbs of London, which lay next the shore or bank of the Thames, was called the Strand. An immunity from custom, and all impositions upon goods or vessels by

land or water, was usually expressed by strand or stream.
