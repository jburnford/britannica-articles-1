TARQUIN the Proud

sub_entry · 314 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TARQUIN the Proud, a tyrant and usurper, had rendered himself odious to the Romans by his pride and cruelty, when his son, Sextus Tarquinius, having ravished Lucretia, occasioned that well-known revolution which put an end to the monarchy, and established the republic of Rome. See the article ROME, n° 49. 51, &c.
