TERRASSON (Abbe John), a French writer born at Lyons in 1669. He distinguished himself in the

Terrestrial dispute concerning Homer, between La Motte and Madam Dacier, by writing a Dissertation contre l'Iliade; he wrote a political and moral romance called Sethos, full of learning and philosophy; and another capital work of his is a French translation of Diodorus Siculus. He died in 1750.
