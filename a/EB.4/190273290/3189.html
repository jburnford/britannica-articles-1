PHASCUM

article · 249 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PHASCUM, in botany, a genus of the class of cryptogamia, and order of musci or mosses. Its characters are, that the anthera is covered, and guarded at the margin by parallel bristles; and that the calyptra, or veil, is small. There are four species.
