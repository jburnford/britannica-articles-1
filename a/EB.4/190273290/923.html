STONEHIVE

STONEHIVE, or STONEHAVEN, a small town in the county of Merca, in Scotland, 12 computed miles south from Aberdeen. It was built in the time of Charles II. and stands at the foot of some high cliffs, in a small bay, with a rocky bottom, opening a little in one part, so that small vessels may find admittance but only at high water. A pier laps over this harbour from the north side to secure them after their entrance. The town contains about 800 inhabitants. The manufactures are sail-cloths and Osna-burghs, knit worsted and thread stockings.
