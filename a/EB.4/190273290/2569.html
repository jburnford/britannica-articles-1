XENOPHON

article · 442 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

XENOPHON the Younger, a Greek writer, so called to distinguish him from the celebrated Xenophon, was born at Ephesus, and lived, according to some authors, before Heliodorus, that is, about the beginning of the 4th century. He is only known by his Ephesiaca, a Greek romance, in five books, which is esteemed, and contains the amours or adventures of Abracomes and Anthia. This romance was printed at London, in

Greek and Latin, in 1724, 4to.
