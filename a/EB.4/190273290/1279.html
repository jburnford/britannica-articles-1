TARPEIAN

article · 292 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TARPEIAN, in Roman antiquity, an appellation given to a steep rock in Rome; whence, by the law of the twelve tables, those guilty of certain crimes were precipitated.

The rock took its name from Tarpeia, a vestal virgin who was killed by the Sabines, as related under the article ROME, n° 24.
