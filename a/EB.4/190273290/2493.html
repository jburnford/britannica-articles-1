WOLAW

article · 489 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

WOLAW, a town in Germany, in Silesia, and capital of a duchy of the same name. It is surrounded with strong walls and a morass, and one part of the houses are built with stone. The castle is also encompassed with deep ditches, and the greatest part of the inhabitants are employed in a woollen manufactory. In 1709 a Protestant church was allowed to be built here. It is seated on the river Oder, 20 miles northwest of Breslau, and 32 south-east of Glogau. E. Long. 16. 48. N. Lat. 51. 16.
