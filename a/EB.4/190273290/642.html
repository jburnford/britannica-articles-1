SPADIX

article · 414 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SPADIX, in botany, anciently signified the receptacle of the palms. It is now used to express every flower-stalk that is protruded out of a spathe or sheath.

The spadix of the palms is branched; that of all other plants, simple. This last case admits of some variety; in callis, draconium and pothos, the florets cover it on all sides; in arum, they are disposed on the lower part only; and in zofera, on one side.
