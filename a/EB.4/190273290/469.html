SLEUT-HOUNDE

article · 262 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SLEUT-HOUNDE, the ancient Scots name of the blood-hound. The word is from the Saxon slot, the impression that a deer leaves of its foot in the mire, and

Sleswick and bound, a "dog." So they derive their name from following the track. See the article BLOOD-HOUND.
