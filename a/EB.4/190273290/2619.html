ZALUSKI

ZALUSKI (Andrew Chrysoform), bishop of Plock, and afterwards of Varnia, and grand chancellor of Poland, was the son of Alexander Zaluski, waiwode of Rawa. He had a great share in all the important affairs of the state, and died in 1711, aged 61. His principal work is a curious collection of Latin Letters, printed in 1709 and 1711, in 3 vols, folio. These letters contain the history of Poland, and interesting memoirs of Europe, during his own time.
