PENTANDRIA

PENTANDRIA, (from πέντε, "five," and ἄνδρ, "a man or husband"); the name of the fifth class in Linnæus's sexual method, consisting of plants which have hermaphrodite flowers, with five stamens or male organs. See BOTANY, p. 1296.
