PARTISAN

article · 424 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PARTISAN, in the art of war, a person dextrous in commanding a party; who, knowing the country well, is employed in getting intelligence, or surprising the enemy's convoy, &c. The word also means an officer sent out upon a party, with the command of a body of light troops, generally under the appellation of the partisans corps. It is also necessary that this corps should be composed of infantry, light-horse, and hussars.
