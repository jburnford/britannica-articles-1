PANORPA

PANORPA, the SCORPION FLY, in zoology, a genus of insects belonging to the order of neuroptera. The rostrum is horny and cylindrical; there are two pappi, and three stemonata; the feelers are longer than the thorax; and the tail of the male is furnished with a forceps. There are four species, distinguished by the colour and shape of their wings. They skip, and are found in meadows.
