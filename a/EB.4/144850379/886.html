PHILIP II. king of Macedon, and father of Alexander the Great; equally renowned as a legislator, politician, and warrior. After having conquered Greece, he meditated the conquest of Persia; but while he

he was preparing for this expedition, he was assassinated at a banquet by Paufanias, a captain of his guards, in 336 B. C.
