PLATFORM, in the military art, an elevation of earth, on which cannon is placed, to fire on the enemy; such are the mounts in the middle of curtains. On the ramparts there is always a platform, where the cannon are mounted. It is made by the heaping up of earth on the rampart, or by an arrangement of madders, rising insensibly, for the cannon to roll on, either in a casemate, or on attack in the outworks. All prac-
