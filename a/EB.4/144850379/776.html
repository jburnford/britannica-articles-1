PERSEVERANCE, in theology, a Christian virtue, by which we are enabled to persist in the way of salvation to the end.

The final perseverance of the saints is a doctrine much controverted between the Arminians and Calvinists; the latter of whom maintain that it is impossible for grace to be lost, and consequently make perseverance to the end a necessary consequence thereof; while the others imagine, that the most confirmed believers are never out of a possibility of falling.
