ORCHESTRA

article · 334 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ORCHESTRA, in the ancient theatres, a place in the form of a semicircle, where the dancing was performed. In the Greek theatres, the orchestra made part of the stage; but, among the Romans, it answered nearly to our pit; only that in it were disposed the seats for the senators, magistrates, vassals, and other persons of distinction.
