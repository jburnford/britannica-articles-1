PHYLACTERY

article · 368 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PHYLACTERY particularly denoted a slip of parchment, wherein was wrote some text of holy scripture, particularly of the decalogue, which the more devout people among the Jews wore on the forehead, the breast, or the neck, as a mark of their religion.

The primitive Christians also gave the name phylacteries to the cases wherein they inclosed the relics of their dead.
