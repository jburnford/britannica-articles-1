Hydraulic ORGAN

sub_entry · 390 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Hydraulic ORGAN, denotes a musical machine that plays by water instead of wind. Of these there are several in Italy, in the grottos of vineyards. Ctesibes of Alexandria, who lived in the time of Ptolemy Euergetes, is said to have invented organs that played by compressing the air with water, as is still practised. Archimedes and Vitruvius have left us descriptions of the hydraulic organ.
