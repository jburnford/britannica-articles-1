PASSIONS

Cross-references: METAPHYSICS · MORAL PHILOSOPHY

PASSIONS, in moral philosophy, are certain motions of the soul, which make it pursue what appears to be good, and avoid whatever threatens evil. See METAPHYSICS, no 31, 32.

On the just regulation and subordination of the passions depends in a great measure the happiness of mankind. See MORAL PHILOSOPHY, no 6, 17, 31, 32, 68, 212.
