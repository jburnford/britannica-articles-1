PHUL, or PUL, king of Assyria, by some historians said to be Ninus under another name, and the first founder of that monarchy: A renowned warrior. He invaded Israel in the reign of Manahem, who became tributary to him, and paid him 1000 talents of silver for a peace. Flourished 771 B. C.
