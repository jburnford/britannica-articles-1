CELLARIUS

article · 709 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CELLARIUS (Christopher) was born in 1638, at Smalcade in Franconia, of which town his father was minister. He was successively rector of the colleges of Weymar, Zeits, and Meribourg; and the king of Prussia having founded an university at Hall in 1693, he was prevailed on to be professor of eloquence and history there, where he composed the greatest part of his works. His great application to study hastened the infirmities of old age; for it is said, he would spend whole days and nights together at his books, without any attention to his health, or even the calls of nature. His works relate to grammar, geography, history, and the oriental languages, and the number of them is amazing. He died in 1707.
