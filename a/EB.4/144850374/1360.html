CHANDLER

CHANDLER (Mary), distinguished by her talent for poetry, was the daughter of a dissenting minister at Bath; and was born at Malmesbury in Wiltshire, in 1687. She was bred a milliner; but from her childhood had a turn for poetry, and in her riper years applied herself to the study of the poets. Her poems, for which she was complimented by Mr Pope, breathe the spirit of piety and philosophy. She had the misfortune to be deformed, which determined her to live

single; though she had great sweetness of countenance, and was solicited to marry. She died in 1745, aged 58.
