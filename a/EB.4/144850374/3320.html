CROW-FOOT

article · 298 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CROW-FOOT, on ship-board, a complication of small cords spreading out from a long block, like the smaller parts which extend from the back-bone of a herring; (Plate LXXXII. fig. 12). It is used to suspend the ownings; or to keep the top-fails from striking violently, and fretting against the tops.
