CAB, an Hebrew dry measure, being the sixth part of a seah or satum, and the 18th part of an ephah. A cab contained 2½ pints of our corn-measure: a quarter

cab was the measure of dove's dung, or more properly a sort of chick-pease called by this name, which was sold at Samaria, during the siege of that city, for five shekels.
