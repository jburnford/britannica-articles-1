CHRISTOPHERS

CHRISTOPHERS, St. one of the Caribbee islands, in America, lying to the north-west of Nevis, and about 60 miles west of Antigua. It was formerly inhabited by the French and English: but, in 1713, it was ceded to the latter. It is about 20 miles in breadth, and 7 in length; and has high mountains in the middle, whence rivulets run down. Between the mountains are dreadful rocks, horrid precipices, and thick woods; and in the south-west part of the island, hot sulphurous springs at the foot of them. The air is good; the soil light, sandy, and fruitful; but the island is subject to hurricanes. The produce is chiefly sugar, cotton, ginger, indigo, and the tropical fruits. W. Long. 62. 32. N. Lat. 17. 30.
