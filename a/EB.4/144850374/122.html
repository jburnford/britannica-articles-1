CAGE

CAGE, an inclosure made of wire, wicker, or the like, interwoven lattice-wise, for the confinement of birds, or wild beasts.

The cage, in the Roman amphitheatres, was a place wherein savage animals were confined. It was inclosed with iron rails, and open at top, so as to be seen to the bottom by the spectators.
