CHARTA Magna

CHARTA Magna, the great charter, is an ancient instrument, containing several privileges and liberties granted to the church and state by Edward the confessor; together with others relating to the feudal laws of William the conqueror, granted by Henry I.; all confirmed by the succeeding princes above thirty times. See LAW, n° 31. par. penult.
