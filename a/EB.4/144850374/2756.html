CONTINUED Bass

Cross-references: MUSIC

CONTINUED Bass, in music, thus called, says Roussseau, because it is continued through the whole piece. Its principal use, besides that of regulating the harmony, is to support the voice and preserve the tone. They pretend that it was one Ludovico Viana, of whom a treatise still remains, who towards the end of the last century first put the continued bass in practice. See MUSIC, Art. 204.
