GRESSY

GRESSY, a port-town of Picardy in France, about 44 miles south of Calais, and 27 north-west of Abbeville, remarkable on account of the victory obtained there over the French, by Edward III. of England, in the year 1346. E. Long. 2.0. N. Lat. 50. 20.
