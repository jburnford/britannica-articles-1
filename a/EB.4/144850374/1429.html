CHARLES MARTEL

article · 605 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CHARLES MARTEL, a renowned conqueror in the early annals of France. He deposed and restored Chilperic king of France; and had the entire government of the kingdom, once with the title of mayor of the palace, and afterwards as duke of France; but he would not accept the crown. He died, regretted, in 741.

CHARLES le Gros, emperor of the west in 881, king of Italy and Suabia, memorable for his reverse of fortune; being dethroned at a diet held near Mentz, by the French, the Italians, and the Germans, in 887: after which he was obliged to submit to the bounty of the archbishop of Mentz. He died in 888.
