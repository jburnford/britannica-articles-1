CENTUMVIRI

article · 250 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CENTUMVIRI, in Roman antiquity, judges appointed to decide common causes among the people: they were chosen, three out of each tribe; and though five more than an hundred, were nevertheless called centumviri, from the round number centum, an hundred.
