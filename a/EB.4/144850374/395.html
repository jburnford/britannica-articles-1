CAMUS (John Peter), a French prelate born in 1582. He was author of a number of pious romances (the taste of his time), and other theological works, to the amount of 200 vols. His definition of politics is remarkable: Arts non tam regendi, quam fallendi, homines; "the art not so much of governing, as of deceiving, mankind." He died in 1652.
