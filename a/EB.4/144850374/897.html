CASSIUS (Spurius), a renowned Roman general and consul, whose enemies accusing him of aspiring to royalty, he was thrown down from the Tarpeian rock,

485 years before Christ; after having thrice enjoyed the consular dignity, been once general of the horse under the first dictator that was created at Rome, and twice received the honour of a triumph.
