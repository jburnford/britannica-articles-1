CATECHESIS

article · 609 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CATECHESIS, in a general sense, denotes an instruction given any person in the first rudiments of an art or science; but more particularly of the Christian religion.

religion. In the ancient church, catechesis was an instruction given viva voce, either to children, or adult heathens, preparatory to their receiving of baptism. In this sense, catechesis stands contra-distinguished from mythologica, which were a higher part of instruction given to those already initiated, and containing the mysteries of faith. Those who give such instructions are called catechists; and those who receive them, catechumens.
