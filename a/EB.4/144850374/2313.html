COLOPHONY

article · 659 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COLOPHONY, in pharmacy, black resin, or turpentine, boiled in water, and afterwards dried; or, which is still better, the caput mortuum remaining after the distillation of the ethereal oil, being further urged by a more intense and long continued fire.—It receives its name of colophonía, from Colophon, a city of Ionia; because the best was formerly brought from thence. Two sorts are mentioned in ancient writings; the one dry, the other in a liquid form. The latter

seems to have been liquid pitch, which is the crude resin of the pine brought from Colophon; the other was called resina frusta, and consisted only of the former deprived of its humid parts.
