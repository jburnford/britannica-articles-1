CANDLE-WOOD

article · 326 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CANDLE-WOOD, slips of pine about the thickness of a finger, used in New England and other colonies to burn instead of candles, giving a very good light. The French inhabitants of Portuga use slips of yellow santal-wood for the same purpose and under the same denomination, which yields a clear flame, though of a green colour.
