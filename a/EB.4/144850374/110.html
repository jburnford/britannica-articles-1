CÆSTUS

article · 322 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CÆSTUS, in antiquity, a large gantlet made of raw hide, which the wrestlers made use of when they fought at the public games.—This was a kind of leather strap, strengthened with lead, or plates of iron, which encompassed the hand, the wrist, and a part of the arm, as well to defend these parts, as to enforce their blows.
