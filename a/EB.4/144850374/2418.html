COMMINATORY

article · 391 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COMMINATORY, an appellation given to whatever threatens punishment, or some penalty. Thus, in France, when an exile is enjoined not to return under pain of death, it is deemed a comminatory penalty; since if he do return, it is not strictly executed; but a second injunction is laid on him, which is more than comminatory, and, from the day of the date thereof, imports death without remedy.
