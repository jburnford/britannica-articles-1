CARBUNCLE, in heraldry, a charge or bearing, consisting

filing of eight radii, four whereof make a common cross, and the other four a saltier.

Some call these radii buttons, or flaves, because round, and enriched with buttons, or pearled like pilgrims flaves, and frequently tipped or terminated with flower-de-luces; others blazon them, royal sceptres, placed in saltier, pale and fesse.
