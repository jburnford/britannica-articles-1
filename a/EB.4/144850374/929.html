CASTIGATION

article · 377 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CASTIGATION, among the Romans, the punishment of an offender by blows, or beating, with a wand or switch. Castigation was chiefly a military punishment; the power of inflicting which on the soldiery was given to the tribunes. Some make it of two kinds; one with a stick or cane, called fustigatio; the other with rods, called flagellatio: the latter was the most dishonourable.
