CATARACT

article · 299 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CATARACT, in hydrography, a precipice in the channel of a river, caused by rocks, or other obstacles, stopping the course of the stream, from whence the water falls with a greater noise and impetuosity: Such are the cataracts of the Nile, the Danube, Rhine, and the famous one of Niagara in America.
