CLARO-Obscuro, or Chiaro-scuro

sub_entry · 428 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CLARO-Obscuro, or Chiaro-scuro, is also used to signify a design consisting only of two colours, most usually black and white, but sometimes black and yellow; or it is a design washed only with one colour, the shadows being of a dusky brown, and the lights heightened up by white.

The word is also applied to prints of two colours taken off at twice: whereof there are volumes in the cabinets of those who are curious in prints.
