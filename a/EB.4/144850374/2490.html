Beam COMPASSES

sub_entry · 328 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Beam COMPASSES consist of a long branch, or beam, carrying two brass cursors, the one fixed at one end, the other sliding along the beam, with a screw to fasten it on occasion. To the cursors may be screwed points of any kind, whether steel, for pencils, or the like. It is used to draw large circles, to take great extents, &c.
