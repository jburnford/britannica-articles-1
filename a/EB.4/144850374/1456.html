CHARTER

CHARTER, in law, a written instrument, or evidence of things acted between one person and another. The word charter comes from the Latin carta, anciently used for a public and authentic act, a donation, contract, or the like; from the Greek χαρτης, "thick paper" or "passeboard," whereon public acts were wont to be written. Britton divides charters into those of the king, and those of private persons. 1. Charters of the king, are those whereby the king passeth any grant to any person or body politic, as a charter of exemption of privilege, &c.; charter of pardon, whereby a man is forgiven a felony, or other offence committed against the king's crown and dignity; charter of the forest, wherein the laws of the forest are comprised, such as the charter of Canutus, &c. 2. Charters of private persons, are deeds and instruments for the conveyance of lands, &c. And the purchaser of lands shall have all the charters, deeds, and evidences, as incident to the same, and for the maintenance of his title.
