COMPLETUS FLOS, in botany. A flower is said to be complete, which is provided with both the covers, viz. the calix or flower-cup, and the petals. The term was invented by Vaillant, and is synonymous to calyculatus flos in Linnaeus. Berkenhout erroneously confounds it with the autus and calyculatus calix of the same author.
