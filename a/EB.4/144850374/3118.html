COUNTER-POINT

COUNTER-POINT, in music: a term derived from the Latin preposition contra, and the verb pungere; because the musical characters by which the notes in each part are signified are placed in such a manner each with respect to each as to shew how the parts answer one another. See COMPOSITION.
