CARTHAGENA, a sea-port town of Spain in the kingdom of Murcia, and capital of a territory of the same name; built by Afdrubal, a Carthaginian general, and named after Carthage. It has the best harbour in all Spain, but nothing else very considerable; the bishop's fee being transferred to Toledo. In 1706 it was taken by Sir John Leak; but the Duke of Berwick retook it afterwards. W. Long. 0. 58. N. Lat. 37. 36.
