ERINUS

ERINUS, in botany, a genus of the angiospermia order, belonging to the didynamia class of plants. There are six species, none of them natives of Britain. They grow from two inches to four feet in height, and are adorned with flowers of a white or purple colour. They are propagated by seeds, but in this country generally require to be kept in a stove.
