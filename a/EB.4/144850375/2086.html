FESTINO

article · 328 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FESTINO, in logic, the third mood of the second figure of the syllogism, the first proposition whereof is an universal negative, the second a particular affirmative, and the third a particular negative; as in the following example:

FES No bad man can be happy,

TI Some rich men are bad men.

NO Ergo, some rich men are not happy.
