ENTAIL

Cross-references: FEE

ENTAIL, in law, signifies fee tail, or fee entailed; that is, abridged, curtailed, or limited to certain conditions. See FEE, and TAIL.

ENTE', in heraldry, a method of marshalling, more frequent abroad than with us, and signifying grafted or ingrafted.

We have, indeed, one instance of enté in the fourth grand quarter of his majesty's royal ensign, whose blazon is Brunswick and Lunenburg impaled with ancient Saxony, enté en pointé, "grafted in point."
