DOWNHAM

article · 262 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DOWNHAM, a market-town of Norfolk, 10 miles south of Lynn, famous for its good butter; there being 1000, and sometimes 2000, sirkins bought here every Monday, and sent up the river Ouse to Cambridge, from whence it is conveyed to London in the Cambridge-waggons.
