In Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Middleburgh, &c. books and accounts are kept by some in guilders shivers and pennings, and by others in pounds shillings and pence Flemish.

Britain gives 1 l. Sterling for an uncertain number of shillings and pence Flemish. The par is 1 l. Sterling for 36.59 s. Flemish; that is, 1 l. 16 s. 7.08 d. Flemish.

When the Flemish rate rises above par, Britain gains and Holland loses by the exchange, and vice versa.

Sterling money is changed into Flemish, by saying, As 1 l. Sterling to the given rate, So is the given Sterling to the Flemish sought.

Or, the Flemish money may be cast up by practice. Dutch money, whether pounds, shillings, pence Flemish, or guilders, shivers, pennings, may be changed into Sterling, by saying,

As the given rate to 1 l. Sterling, So the given Dutch to the Sterling sought.

Books and accounts are kept at the bank, and by most people in the city, in marks, schilling-lubs, and phennings; but some keep them in pounds, shillings, and groots Flemish.

The agio at Hamburg runs between 20 and 40 per cent. All bills are paid in bank-money.

Hamburg exchanges with Britain by giving an uncertain number of schillings and groots Flemish for the pound Sterling. The groot or penny Flemish here, as also at Antwerp, is worth 1/2 of a penny Sterling; and so something better than in Holland, where it is only 1/4 d. Sterling.
