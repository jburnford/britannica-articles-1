FABRICIUS (John Albert), one of the most learned and laborious men of his age, was born at Leipzig in 1668. He was chosen professor of eloquence at Hamburg in 1699, and was made doctor of divinity at Kiel. His works are numerous; and he died at Hamburg in 1736, after a life spent in the severest literary application to collect and publish valuable remains of ancient learning.
