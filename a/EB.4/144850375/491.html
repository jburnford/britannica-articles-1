DIAMOND-MINE of Raolconda.—In the neighbourhood of this mine the earth is sandy, and full of rocks and cople. In these rocks are found several little veins of half and sometimes a whole inch broad, out of which the miners, with a kind of hooked irons, draw the sand, or earth, wherein the diamonds are; breaking the rocks when the vein terminates, that the track may be found again, and continued. When a sufficient quantity of earth or sand is drawn forth, they wash it two or three times, to separate the stones therefrom. The miners work quite naked, except for a thin linen cloth before them; and besides this precaution, have likewise inspectors, to prevent their concealing of stones: which, however, maugre all this care, they frequently find means to do, by watching opportunities when they are not observed, and swallowing them down.
