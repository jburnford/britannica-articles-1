DIGIT

article · 195 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIGIT, in astronomy, the twelfth part of the diameter of the sun or moon, used to express the quantity of an eclipse. Thus an eclipse is said to be of six digits, when six of these parts are hid.
