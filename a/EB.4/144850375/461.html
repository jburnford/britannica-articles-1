DIETETÆ

article · 476 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIETETÆ, in Grecian antiquity, a kind of judges, of which there were two sorts, the cleroti and diallaeterii. The former were public arbitrators, chosen by lot to determine all causes exceeding ten drachms, within their own tribe, and from their sentence an appeal lay to the superior courts.

The diallaeterii, on the contrary, were private arbitrators from whose sentence there lay no appeal, and accordingly they always took an oath to administer justice without partiality.
