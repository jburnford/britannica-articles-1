DIPSAS

article · 210 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIPSAS, a sort of serpent, the bite of which produces such a thirst as proves mortal; whence its name dispas, which signifies thirsty. In Latin it is called fitula, a pail. Moses speaks of it in Deut. viii. 15.
