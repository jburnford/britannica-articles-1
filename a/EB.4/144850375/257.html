DELIBERATIVE

article · 398 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DELIBERATIVE, an appellation given to a kind or branch of rhetoric, employed in proving a thing, or convincing an assembly thereof, in order to persuade them to put it in execution.

To have a DELIBERATIVE voice in the assembly, is when a person has a right to give his advice and his vote therein. In councils, the bishops have deliberative voices; those beneath them have only consultative voices.
