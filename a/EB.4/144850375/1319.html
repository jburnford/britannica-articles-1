EMPHYSEMA

article · 366 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EMPHYSEMA, in surgery, a windy tumour, generally occasioned by a fracture of the ribs, and formed by the air insinuating itself, by a small wound, between the skin and muscles, into the substance of the cellular or adipose membrane, spreading itself afterwards up to the neck, head, belly, and other parts, much after the manner in which butchers blow up their veal.
