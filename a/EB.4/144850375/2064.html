FERONIA

article · 480 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FERONIA, the pagan goddess of woods and orchards. The deity took her name from the town Feronia, situated at the foot of mount Sarocete in Italy, where was a wood and temple consecrated to her. This town and wood are mentioned by Virgil, in the catalogue of Turnus's forces. Strabo relates, that those, who sacrificed to this goddess, walked barefoot upon burning coals, without being hurt. She was the guardian deity of freed-men, who received their cap of liberty in her temple.
