DARDA

DARDA, a town and fort of Lower Hungary, built by the Turks in 1686, and taken by the Imperialists the next year, in whose hands it remains. It is seated on the river Draw, 10 miles from its confluence with the Danube, and at the end of the bridge of Esfeck. E. Long. 19. 10. N. Lat. 45. 45.
