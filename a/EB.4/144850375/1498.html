EPOPOEIA

article · 295 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EPOPOEIA, in poetry, the history, action, or fable, which makes the subject of an epic poem. The word is derived from the Greek, ἐπος, carmen, "verse;" and ποιέω, facio, "I make."

In the common use of the word, however, epopeia is the same with epos, or epic poem itself. See the article POETRY.
