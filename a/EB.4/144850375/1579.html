ERYTHRONIUM, DOG'S-TOOTH VIOLET

ERYTHRONIUM, DOG'S-TOOTH VIOLET; a genus of the monogynia order, belonging to the hexandria class of plants. There is only one species, which, however, admits of several varieties in its flowers, as white, purple, pale red, dark red, crimson, and yellow. The plants are low and herbaceous, with a purple stalk and hexapetalous flowers. All the varieties are hardy and durable; and may be planted in small patches in borders, where they will make a good appearance. They rarely perfect their seeds in this country, but may be propagated by offsets. In Siberia, according to Gmelin, they dry and mix the root of this plant with their soups. It grows there in great abundance; and is called by the people of the country, bess.
