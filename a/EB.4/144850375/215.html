DEFEND

article · 486 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DEFEND, in our ancient laws and statutes, signifies to prohibit or forbid: as, Usuarius defendit quoque rex Edwardus ne remanerent in regno. L. L. Edw. Conf. c. 37. & 5 Rich. 2. c. 7. In which sense Chaucer also uses it in the following passage:

"Where can you say in any manner age,

"That ever God defended marriage."

In 7 Edw. I. there is a statute, intitled, "Statutum de defensione portandi arma," &c. And "it is defended by law to distrain on the highway;" Coke on Littl. fol. 161.
