DITTO

article · 220 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DITTO, in books of accounts, usually written Do, signifies the aforementioned. The word is corrupted from the Italian detto, "the said:" as in our law-phrase, "the said premises," meaning the same as were aforementioned.
