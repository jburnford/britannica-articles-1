NAXUS (anc. geogr.), a town of Crete, famous for its bones, called Lapis Naxius. Another of Sicily,

built by the Chalcidian; situate on the south side of Mount Taurus, destroyed by Dionysius the tyrant; from whose ruins Tauromenium, built by Timoleon, either arose or was increased, (Plutarch).
