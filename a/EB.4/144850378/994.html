NOMINALS

NOMINALS, or NOMINALISTS, a sect of school-philosophers, the disciples and followers of Occam, or Ockham, an English cordelier, in the 14th century. They were great dealers in words, whence they were vulgarly denominated Word-sellers; but had the denomination of Nominalists, because that, in opposition to

the Realists, they maintained, that words, and not things, were the object of dialectics.
