MISERICORDIA

article · 382 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MISERICORDIA, in law, is an arbitrary fine imposed on any person for an offence: this is called misericordia, because the amercement ought to be but small, and less than that required by magna charta. If a person be outrageously amerced, in a court that is not of record, the writ called moderata misericordia lies for moderating the amercement according to the nature of the fault.
