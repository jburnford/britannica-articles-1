MULTIPLICATUS FLOS, a luxuriant flower, whose petals are multiplied so as to exclude a part of the lamina.

Multiplic-
tos
Mum.

A multiplied luxuriant flower differs from a full one, the highest degree of luxuriance, in that the petals of the latter are so multiplied as to exclude all the stamens: whereas those of the former are only repeated or multiplied, two, three, or four times, as to the exclusion of only a small part of the essential organs.
