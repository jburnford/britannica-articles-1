METATARSUS, in anatomy. See there, no 69.
METATHESIS, in grammar, a species of the metaplasmus; being a figure whereby the letters or syllables of a word are transposed, or shifted out of their usual situations, as pistris for pristis, Lybia for Libya, &c.

This word is, by physicians, used with respect to morbid causes; which, when they cannot be evacuated, are removed to places where they are less injurious.
