MONOSYLLABLE

article · 338 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MONOSYLLABLE, in grammar, a word that consists only of one syllable, and is composed either of one or more letters pronounced at the same time. The too frequent use of monosyllables has a very bad effect in English poetry, as Mr Pope both intimates and exemplifies in the same verse, viz.

"And ten flow words out except in one dull line."
