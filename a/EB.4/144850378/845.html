NEREIS

article · 561 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

NEREIS, in zoology, a genus of animals belonging to the order of vermes mollusca. The body is oblong, linear, and fitted for creeping; it is furnished with lateral pencilled tentacula. There are 11 species; of which the most remarkable is the noctiluca, being one of the causes of the luminousness of the sea. They are inhabitants of almost every sea; and illuminate the water like glow-worms, but with a brighter splendour, so as at night to make the element appear as if on fire all around. Their bodies are so minute as to elude examination by the naked eye.
