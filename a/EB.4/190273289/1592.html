SANCTORIUS

article · 674 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SANCTORIUS, a most ingenious and learned physician, professor in the university of Padua, in the beginning of the 17th century. He contrived a kind of statistical chair, by means of which, after estimating the aliments received, and the sensible discharges, he was enabled to determine with great exactness, the quantity of insensible perspiration, as well as what kind of victuals and drink increased or diminished it. On these experiments he erected a curious system, which he published under the title of De medicina statistica; of which we have an English translation by Dr Quincy. Sanctorius published several other treatises, which shewed great abilities and learning.
