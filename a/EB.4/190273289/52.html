POLLIO

article · 588 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

POLLIO (Caius Aflinius), a celebrated Latin poet and orator, was of consular dignity, and composed some tragedies which were esteemed, but are now lost. He was the first who opened at Rome a library for the use of the public. He was the friend of Mark Antony; which prevented his complying with the solicitations of Augustus, who pressed him to embrace his party. At length Augustus having wrote some verses against Pollio, he was urged to answer them; on which he said, "I shall take care of writing against a man who has the power of proferibing us." He is praised by Virgil and Horace.
