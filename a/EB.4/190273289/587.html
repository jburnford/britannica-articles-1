PUTEOLI

article · 563 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PUTEOLI, (Livy, Strabo:) a town of Campania; so called either from its wells, there being many hot and cold springs thereabouts; or from its stench, putor, caused by sulphureous exhalations, (Varro, Strabo): Anciently called Dicarchia, from its equal and just government.—The port of Cumæ, a place of great trade, probably built by the Cumæans; situated on the braw of a hill. (id.) A Roman colony, (Livy); furnished Augusta, under Nero, (Frontinus). Putolani, the people, (Cicero). Now Pozzuoli, nine miles to the west of Naples. E. Long. 14. 40. N. Lat. 41. 15.
