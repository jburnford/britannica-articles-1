SABINUS

SABINUS (George), a celebrated Latin poet, born in the electorate of Brandenburg in 1508. His poem Res gestæ Casarum Germanorum, spread his reputation all over Germany, and procured him the patronage of all the princes who had any regard for polite literature: he was made professor of the belles lettres at Frankfort on the Oder, rector of the new academy at Königsburg, and counsellor to the elector of Brandenburg. He married two wives, the first of which was the eldest daughter of the famous reformer Melancthon; and died in 1560. His poems are well known, and have been often printed.
