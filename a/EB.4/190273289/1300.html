ROCK-CRYSTAL

article · 502 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ROCK-CRYSTAL, in natural history, otherwise called spig-crystal, a name given to the third order of crystals, from their being affixed to a rock or other solid body. This kind of crystal is the most common of all others, and is what the generality of authors describe under the name of crystal of the stops. The clearest, purest, and most transparent that can be had is to be chosen: and to prove its genuineness, it may be tried with aqua fortis; true crystal making no effervescence with that medium.
