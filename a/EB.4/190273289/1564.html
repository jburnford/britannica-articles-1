SALTIER

article · 609 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SALTIER, one of the honourable ordinaries defined p. 3595, and represented in Plate CXLVII. This says G. Leigh, in his Accedence of Armp. 70. was anciently made of the height of a man, and driven full of pins, the use of which was to scall walls, &c. Upton says it was an instrument to catch wild beasts, whence he derives this word from saltus, i. e. "a forrest." The French call this ordinary sauter, from sauter "to leap;" because it may have been used by soldiers to leap over walls of towns, which in former times were but low: but some modern authors think it is borne in imitation of St Andrew's cross.
