POLYANDRIA, (from πολύς, "many," and γυνή, a

"man, or husband,") many husbands; the name of the Polyandria 13th class in Linnæus's sexual method, consisting of plants with hermaphrodite flowers, which are furnished with several stamens, that are inserted into the common receptacle of the flower.
