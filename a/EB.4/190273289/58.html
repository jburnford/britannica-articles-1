POLTROON

article · 565 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

POLTROON, or POLTRON, a coward, or dastard, wanting courage to perform any thing great or noble. The word is borrowed from the French, who, according to Salmasius, derive it a pollice truncato; because anciently those who would avoid going to the wars, cut off their thumb. But Menage, with more probability, derives it from the Italian poltrone, and that from poltro a "bed;" because timorous, pusillanimous people, take pleasure in lying a-bed. Others choose to derive the word from the Italian, poltro, a "colt;" because of that creature's readiness to run away.
