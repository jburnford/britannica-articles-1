PORTICI

article · 407 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PORTICI, a palace of the king of Naples, four miles from that capital. It has a charming situation, on the sea-side, near mount Vesuvius. It is enriched with a vast number of fine statues, and other remains of antiquity, taken out of the ruins of Herculaneum, which is not far from thence, and was swallowed up by an earthquake which attended an eruption of mount Vesuvius in the reign of the emperor Titus.
