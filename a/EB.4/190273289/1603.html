Gum-SANDARACH

sub_entry · 660 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Gum-SANDARACH, is a dry and hard resin, usually met with in loose granules, of the bigness of a pea, a horse-

horfe-bean, or larger; of a pale whitish yellow colour, transparent, and of a resinous smell, brittle, very inflammable, of an acrid and aromatic taste, and diffusing a very pleasant smell when burning. It is produced from a species of the juniper.

It flows only from these trees in hot countries; but the natives promote its discharge by making incisions in the bark.

Sandarach is esteemed good in diarrhoeas, and in hæmorrhagies.

The varnish-makers make a kind of varnish of it, by dissolving it in oil of turpentine or linseed, or in spirit of wine.
