REGISTERS

article · 304 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: STEAM-ENGINE

REGISTERS, in chemistry, are holes, or chinks with stopples, contrived in the sides of furnaces, to regulate the fire; that is, to make the heat more intense or remiss, by opening them to let in the air, or keeping them close to exclude it. There are also registers in the steam-engine. See STEAM-ENGINE.
