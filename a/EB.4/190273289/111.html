PONTON-BRIDGE

article · 664 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PONTON-BRIDGE, is made of pontoons slipped into the water, and placed about five or six feet alander; each fastened with an anchor, when the river has a strong current; or to a strong rope that goes across the river, running through the rings of the pontoons. Each boat has an anchor, cable, baulks, and chests. The baulks are about five or six inches square, and 21 feet long. The chests are boards joined together by wooden bars, about three feet broad, and 12 feet long. The baulks are laid across the pontoons at some distance from one another, and the chests upon them joined close; which makes a bridge in a very short time, capable of supporting any weight.
