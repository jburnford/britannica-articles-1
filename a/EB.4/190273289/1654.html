SARDIS

article · 638 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SARDIS, now called Sarda, is an ancient town of Nolia in Asia, and is seated forty miles east of Smyrna. This town was formerly the seat of king Cræsus; but is now reduced to a poor village, containing nothing but wretched huts. However, there is a large caravansary, where travellers may commodiously lodge. The inhabitants are generally shepherds, who lead their sheep into the fine pastures of the neighbouring plain. The Turks have a mosque here, which was a Christian church, at the gate of which there are several columns of polished marble. There are a few Christians, who are employed in gardening. E. Long. 28. 5. N. Lat. 37. 51.
