AUGEAS

article · 597 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AUGEAS, in fabulous history, was king of Elis, and particularly famed for his stable, which contained 3000 oxen, and had not been cleaned for 30 years. Hercules was desired to clear away the filth from this stable in one day; and Augeas promised, if he performed it, to give him a tenth part of the cattle. This task Hercules is said to have performed by turning the course of the river Alpheus through the stable; when Augeas refusing to stand by his engagement, Hercules flew him with his arrows, and gave his kingdom to Pityleus his son, who had shewn an abhorrence of his father's insincerity.
