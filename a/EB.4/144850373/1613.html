BIDENTALES

article · 297 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BIDENTALES, in Roman antiquity, priests instituted to perform certain ceremonies and expiations when thunder fell on any place. Their principal office was the sacrificing a sheep of two years old, which in Latin is called bidens; from whence the place struck with thunder got the name of bidental.
