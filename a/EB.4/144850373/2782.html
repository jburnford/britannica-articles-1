BUGEY, a province of France, bounded on the east by

Buggers
Bugia. by Savoy, on the west by Bresse, on the south by Dauphiny, and on the north by the territory of Gex and the Franche Comte. It is about 40 miles long, and 25 broad. Though it is a country full of hills and rivers, yet it is fertile in some places, the rivers abound with trouts, and there are plenty of all sorts of game. The chief places are Belley the capital, Seisel, St Rambert, Fort l'Ecluse, and Chateau-Neuf.
