BARNABITES, a religious order, founded in the 16th century by three Italian gentlemen, who had been advised.

advised by a famous preacher of those days to read carefully the epistles of St Paul. Hence they were called clerks of St Paul; and Barnabites, because they performed their first exercise in a church of St Barnabas at Milan. Their habit is black; and their office is to instruct, catechise, and serve in mission.
