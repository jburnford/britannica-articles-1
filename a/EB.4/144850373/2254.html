BOUGEANT

article · 481 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BOUGEANT (William Hyacinth), a famous Jesuit, first taught humanity at Caen and Nevers, and afterwards settled at the college of Lewis the Great, where he employed himself in writing several works; the principal of which were, 1. A collection of physical observations, extracted from the best authors. 2. An history of the wars and negotiations which preceded the treaty of Weisphalia. 3. The female doctor, a philosophical amusement on the language of beasts, &c. He died in 1743.
