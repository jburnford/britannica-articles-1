Horse-BALLS, among farriers. Horses have a very nice taste; it is therefore proper to give the more disagreeable drugs in the form of balls, and to make drenches of the more palatable. Balls should be of an oval shape, not exceeding the size of a pullet's egg; and should be dipped in sweet oil to make them slip down the easier. Some horses have a strait gullet, which makes them very averse to a ball being thrust down their throats; such horses had better have drenches given them, or their medicines may be mixed with

bran, or in their mashes. See FARRIERY, passim.
