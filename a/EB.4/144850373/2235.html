BOTELESS, (sine remedio). In the charter of Hen. I. to Tho. archbishop of York, it is said, "that no judgment, or sum of money, shall acquit him that commits sacrilege; but he is in English called boteless, viz. without emendation." We retain the word still in common speech: as, It is boteless to attempt such a thing; that is, It is in vain to attempt it.
