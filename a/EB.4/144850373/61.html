ATLANTIDES

ATLANTIDES, in astronomy, a denomination given to the Pleiades, or seven stars, sometimes also called Vergilie. They are thus called, as being supposed by the poets to have been the daughters either of Atlas, or his brother Hesperus, who were translated into heaven.
