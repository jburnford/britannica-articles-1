BADEN

article · 605 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BADEN, a town of Germany, in the arch-duchy of Austria, seated on the Little Suechat, is a neat little walled town, standing in a plain not far from a ridge of hills which run out from the mountain Cetius. It is much frequented by the people of Vienna, and the neighbouring parts, on account of its baths. The springs supply two convenient baths within the town, five without the walls, and one beyond the river. They are good for distempers of the head, the gout, dropsy, and most chronic distempers. It is observable that all these Badens take their names from the baths. E. Long. 17. 10. N. Lat. 48. 0.
