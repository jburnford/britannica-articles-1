AURILLAC

article · 453 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AURILLAC, a town of France in the Lower Auvergne, seated on a small river called Jordane. It is one of the most considerable towns of the province, has six gates, is very populous, and yet has but one parish. The castle is very high, and commands the town. The abbot is lord of Aurillac, and has episcopal jurisdiction; he is also chief justice of the town. This place is remarkable for having produced several great men. E. Long. 2. 33. N. Lat. 44. 55.
