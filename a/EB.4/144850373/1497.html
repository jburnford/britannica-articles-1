BERWICK

article · 463 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BERWICK, a shire in Scotland, bounded by the river Tweed, on the south; by Lothian, on the north; by the German Ocean, on the east; and by Teviotdale on the west. It abounds with corn and grass, and has in it several seats of persons of quality. The principal rivers are the Tweed, the Whiteater, Blackadder, Eye, and Ednel. The principal place is the town and castle of Dunse, which is the best place for trade in the country. It sends two members to parliament.
