BASTILE

article · 318 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BASTILE, denotes a small antique castle, fortified with turrets. Such is the bastile of Paris, which seems the only castle that has retained the name: it was begun to be built in 1369 by order of Charles V. and was finished in 1383 under the reign of his successor.—Its chief use is for the custody of state-prisoners.
