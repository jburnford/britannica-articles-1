AUXILIARY VERBS

article · 336 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AUXILIARY VERBS, in grammar, are such as help to form or conjugate others; that is, are prefixed to them, to form or denote the moods or tenses thereof: as, to have and to be, in the English; être and avoir, in the French; bo and son in the Italian, &c. In the English language, the auxiliary verb am supplies the want of passive verbs.
