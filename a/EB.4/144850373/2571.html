BRIGHTHELMSTONE

BRIGHTHELMSTONE, a sea-port town of Sussex in England. It is a pretty large and populous town; but ill built, inhabited chiefly by fishermen, and has a pretty good harbour. W. Long. o. 10. N. Lat. 50. 50. It was at this place king Charles II. embarked for France, 1651, after the battle of Worcester.
