BEAUMONT

article · 588 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BEAUMONT, a town of the Netherlands, in Hainault, on the confines of the territory of Liege. It was ceded to the French in 1684; and taken in 1691 by the English, who blew up the castle. It is situated between the rivers Maese and Sambre, in E. Long. 4. 1. N. Lat. 50. 12.

BEAUMONT le Roger, a town of Upper Normandy in France. E. Long. 0. 56. N. Lat. 49. 2.

BEAUMONT le Vicomte, a town of Maine in France. E. Long. 0. 10. N. Lat. 48. 12.

BEAUMONT sur Oise, a town in the Isle of France, seated on the declivity of a hill, with a bridge over the river Oise. E. Long. 2. 29. N. Lat. 49. 9.
