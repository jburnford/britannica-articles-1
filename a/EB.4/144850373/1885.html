BLINDS, or BLINDS

sub_entry · 383 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BLINDS, or BLINDS, in the art of war, a sort of defence commonly made of oziers, or branches interwoven, and laid across between two rows of stakes, about the height of a man, and four or five feet asunder, used particularly at the heads of trenches, when they are extended in front towards the glacis; serving to shelter the workmen, and prevent their being overlooked by the enemy.
