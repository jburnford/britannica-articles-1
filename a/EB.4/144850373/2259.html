BOUILLON, in the menage, a lump or excrescence of
flesh that grows either upon or just by the frush, in-
much that the frush shoots out, just like a lump of flesh,
and makes the horse halt; and this is called the flesh
blowing upon the frush. Menage horses, that never wet
their feet, are subject to these excrescences, which make
them very lame. See FRUSH.
