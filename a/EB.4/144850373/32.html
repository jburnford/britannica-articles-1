ATHANATI

article · 265 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ATHANATI, in Persian antiquity, a body of cavalry, consisting of 10,000 men, always complete. They were called athanati, (a word originally Greek, and signifying immortal), because, when one of them happened to die, another was immediately appointed to succeed him.
