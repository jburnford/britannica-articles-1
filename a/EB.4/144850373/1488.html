BERRIMAN

article · 986 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BERRIMAN (Dr William), was the son of Mr John Berriman apothecary in Bishopsgate-street, London, where he was born on the 24th of September 1688. He studied at Oriel-college, Oxford, where he took his several degrees, and became curate and lecturer of All-hallows, in Thames-street, and lecturer of St Michael's, Queenhithe. In 1720, he was appointed domestic chaplain to Dr Robinson bishop of London, who soon after collated him to the living of St Andrew's Undercroft; and in 1727, he was elected fellow of Eton-college. He died on the 5th of February, 1750, in the 62d year of his age. He wrote, 1. A seasonable review of Mr Whiston's account of primitive dogologies. 2. An historical account of the Trinitarian controversy, in 8 sermons, at lady Moyer's lectures. 3. Brief remarks on Mr Chandler's introduction to the history of the inquisition. 4. Sermons at Boyle's lectures, 2 vols 8vo. 5. Christian doctrines and duties explained and recommended, in 2 vols 8vo; and other works.
