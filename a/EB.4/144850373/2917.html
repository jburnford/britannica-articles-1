BURSA

BURSA, Burse, originally signifies a purse. In middle-age writers it is more particularly used for a little college or hall in an university, for the residence of students called barsales or barsarii. In the French universities it still denotes a foundation for the maintenance of poor scholars in their studies. The nomination to bursas is in the hands of the patrons and founders thereof. The bursas of colleges are not benefices, but mere places assigned to certain countries and persons. A burse becomes vacant by the burser's being promoted to a cure.
