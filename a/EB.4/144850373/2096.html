BONDAGE

article · 342 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BONDAGE, properly signifies the same with slavery, but in old law books is used for villenage. — Tenants in bondage paid kenots, and did fealty; they were not to fell trees in their own garden, without licence of the lord. The widow of a tenant in bondage held her husband's estate quam diu vixerit sine marito, "as long as she lived single."
