ATARGATIS FANUM

article · 440 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ATARGATIS FANUM, the temple of a goddess worshipped by the Syrians and Parthians, having the face of a woman, and tail of a fish, and called Derceto by the Greeks. Her temple stood in the city Bambyce, called afterwards Hierapolis. It was extremely rich, insomuch that Crassus, in his march against the Parthians, spent several days in weighing the treasure. Vossius makes the name of this goddess Phœnician, from Addir-dag, the great fish.
