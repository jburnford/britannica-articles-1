ATHLETÆ

article · 483 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ATHLETÆ, in antiquity, persons of strength and agility, disciplined to perform in the public games. The word is originally Greek, ἀθλητής; formed from ἀθλῶν, certamen, combat; whence also ἀθλῶν, the prize or reward adjudged the victor. Under athlete were comprehended wrestlers, boxers, runners, leapers, throwers of the disk, and those practised in other exercises exhibited in the Olympic, Pythian, and other solemn sports; for the conquerors wherein there were established prizes.
