ICOSANDRIA

Cross-references: BOTANY

ICOSANDRIA, (from icos, "twenty," and andria, "a man, or husband;") the name of the 12th class in Linnæus's sexual method, consisting of plants with hermaphrodite flowers, which are furnished with 20 or more stamens, that are inserted into the inner side of the calix or petals. See BOTANY, p. 1292.
