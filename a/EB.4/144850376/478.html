DAYS OF GRACE

Cross-references: LAW

DAYS OF GRACE, three days immediately following the term of payment of a bill, within which the creditor must protest it if payment is not obtained, in order to entitle him to recourse against the drawer. See LAW, Part III. No clxxiii. 16.

GRACE is also a title of dignity given to dukes, archbishops, and in Germany to barons and other inferior princes.
