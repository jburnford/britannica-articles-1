HALYWERCFOLK

article · 343 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HALYWERCFOLK, in old writers, were persons who enjoyed land, by the pious service of repairing some church, or defending a sepulchre.

This word also signified such persons in the diocese of Durham, as held their lands to defend the corps of St Cuthbert, and who from thence claimed the privilege of not being forced to go out of the bishopric.
