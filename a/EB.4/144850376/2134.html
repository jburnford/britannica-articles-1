INSTITUTION

article · 341 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

INSTITUTION, in general, signifies the establishing or founding something. — In the canon and common law, it signifies the investing a clerk with the spiritualities of a rectory, &c. which is done by the bishop, who uses the following formula: "I institute you rector of such a church with the cure of souls, and receive your care and mine."
