HABIT, in philosophy, an aptitude or disposition either of mind or body, acquired by a frequent repetition

Habit titution of the same act. See Custom and Habit.
Habit is also used for a dress or garb, or the composition of garments, wherewith a person is covered; in which sense we say, the habit of an ecclesiastic, of a religious, &c. a military habit, &c.
