GRACCHUS

article · 577 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GRACCHUS (Tiberius), elected tribune of the Roman people, demanded in the senate, in their name, the execution of the Agrarian law; by which all persons possessing above 200 acres of land were to be deprived of the surplus, for the benefit of the poor citizens, amongst whom an equal distribution of them

Gracula was to be made; having carried his plan into execution by violent measures, he fell a victim to his zeal, being assassinated by his own party, 133 B. C. Caius, his brother, pursuing the same steps, was killed by the consul Opimius, 121 B. C. See (history of) ROMS.
