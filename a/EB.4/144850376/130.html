GAUGAMELA

GAUGAMELA, (anc. geog.), a village of Aturia, lying between the rivers Lycus and Tigris; famous for Alexander's victory over Darius. It is said to have been allowed to Darius Hyrtaspes for the maintenance of a camel; and hence the name. It was not far from a more considerable place called Arbela; whence the latter gave the name to the victory. See ARBELA.
