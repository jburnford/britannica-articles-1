HARPOCRATES

article · 287 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HARPOCRATES, in Pagan theology, was the son of Isis, and esteemed the god of silence. He was represented under the form of a young man half naked, crowned with an Egyptian mitre, holding in his left hand a cornucopia, and a finger of the other placed on his lips as recommending silence.
