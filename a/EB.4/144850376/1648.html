HYDROCEPHALUS, a preternatural distension of

Hydrocharis of the head to an uncommon size by a stagnation and extravasation of the lymph; which, when collected in the inside of the cranium, is then termed internal; as that collected on the outside is termed external. See (the Index subjoined to) MEDICINE.
