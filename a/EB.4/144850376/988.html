HATCHES

article · 288 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HATCHES, in mining, a term used in Cornwall, to express any of the openings of the earth either into mines or in search of them. The fruitless openings are called essay-hatches; the real mouths of the veins, tin hatches; and the places where they wind up the buckets of ore, wind hatches.
