FIRE-GRAPPLING

article · 387 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FIRE-GRAPPLING, an instrument nearly resembling the former, but differing in the construction of its flukes, which are furnished with strong barbs on their points. These machines are usually fixed on the yard-arms of a ship, in order to grapple any adversary whom she intends to board. They are, however, more particularly useful in fire-ships for the purposes described in that article.
