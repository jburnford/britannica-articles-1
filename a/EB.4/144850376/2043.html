INDUCTION

INDUCTION, in law, is putting a clerk or clergyman in possession of a benefice or living to which he is collated or presented. See the article PARSON.—Induction is performed by a mandate from the bishop to the arch-deacon, who usually issues out a precept to other clergymen to perform it for him. It is done by giving the clerk corporal possession of the church, as by holding the ring of the door, tolling a bell, or the like; and is a form required by law, with intent to give all the parishioners due notice, and sufficient certainty of their new minister, to whom their tithes are to be paid.
