IVES (St.), a sea-port town of Cornwall, in England, seated on a bay of the same name; which being unsafe, it is only frequented by fishermen, for the taking of pilchards. However, it is a corporation, and sends two members to parliament. W. Long. 6. 15. N. Lat. 50. 15.
