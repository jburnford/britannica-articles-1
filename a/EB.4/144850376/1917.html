ILLYRIUS

article · 454 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ILLYRIUS, (Matthias, Flaccus, or Francowitz), one of the most learned divines of the Augsburgh confession, born in Iliria, anciently called Illyrica, in 1520. He is said to have been a man of vast genius, extensive learning, of great zeal against Popery; but of such a restless and passionate temper, as overbalanced all his good qualities, and occasioned much disturbance in the Protestant church. He published a great number of books, and died in 1575.
