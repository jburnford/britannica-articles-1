ACT OF GRACE

Cross-references: LAW

ACT OF GRACE, the appellation given to the act of parliament 1696, c. 32. which allows prisoners for civil debts to be set at liberty, upon making oath that they have not wherewithal to support themselves in prison, unless they are alimended by the creditors on whose diligences they were imprisoned, within ten days after intimation made for that purpose. See LAW, Part III. No clxxxv. 13.
