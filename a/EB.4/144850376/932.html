HARFLUER, an ancient town of France, in Normandy; but is now a poor place, on account of its fortifications being demolished, and its harbour choked up. It was taken by the English, by assault, in the year 1415. It is seated on the river Lizarda, near the Seine, five miles from Havre de Grace, forty north-west of Rouen, and one hundred and six north-west of Paris. E. Lon. 0. 17. N. Lat. 49. 30.
