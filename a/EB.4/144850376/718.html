GUIDO (Reni), an illustrious Italian painter, born at Bologna in 1575. In his early age he was the disciple of Denis Calvert, a Flemish master of good reputation; but afterwards entered himself in the school of the Caracci. He first imitated Ludovico Caracci; but fixed at last in a peculiar style of his own, that secured him the applause of his own time and the admiration of posterity. He was much honoured, and lived in splendor; but an unhappy attachment to gaming ruined his circumstances; the reflection on which brought on a languishing disorder, that put an end to his life in 1642. There are several designs of this great master in print, etched by himself.
