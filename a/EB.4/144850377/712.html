LIGARIUS (Quintus), a Roman proconsul in

Africa, 49 B.C. Taking part with Pompey, he was forbid by Julius Cæsar to return to Rome: to obtain his pardon, Cicero made that admired oration in his defence which has immortalized the memory of the client with that of his celebrated advocate.
