MATRONALIA, a festival of the ancient Roman matrons, from whom it had its name. It was celebrated on the kalends of March in honour of the god Mars; and was to the Roman ladies what the Saturnalia was to their husbands, for at this time they served their women-slaves at tables and received presents from their husbands.
