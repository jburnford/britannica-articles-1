Long MEASURES, or MEASURES of Application. The English standard long measure for commerce, or that whereby the quantities of things are ordinarily estimated in the way of trade, is the yard, containing three English feet. Its divisions are the foot, span, palm, inch, and barley-corn; its multiples the pace, fathom, pole, furlong, and miles. The proportions these severally bear to each other, are expressed in the following table.
