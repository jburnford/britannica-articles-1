MANCIPLE

article · 377 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MANCIPLE (mancips), a clerk of the kitchen, or caterer; and an officer in the inner temple was anciently so called, who is now the steward there; of whom Chaucer, the ancient English poet, some time a student of that house thus writes:

A manciple there was within the temple,
Of which all caterers might take example.

This officer still remains in colleges, in the universities.
