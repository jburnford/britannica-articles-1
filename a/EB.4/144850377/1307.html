MANSIO

article · 244 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MANSIO, a term often mentioned in itineraries, denoting inns on the public roads to lodge in, at the distance of eighteen miles from each other; (Lactantius). Also, in the lower ages, it came to denote "an encampment for one night," (Lamprius).
