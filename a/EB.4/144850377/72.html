KERN

article · 339 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

KERN, or KERNE, a term in the ancient Irish militia, signifying a foot-soldier.—Camden tells us, the armies of Ireland consisted of cavalry, called galloglasfer; and infantry, lightly armed, called kernes.—The kernes bore swords, and darts; to the last were fitted cords, by which they could recover them, after they had been launched out.
