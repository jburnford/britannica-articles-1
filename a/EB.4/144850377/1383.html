MARIA (St.), a handsome and considerable town of Spain, in Andalusia, with a small castle. It was taken by the English and Dutch in 1702, for the arch-duke of Austria. It is seated on the Guadaleta, at the mouth of which is a tower and a close battery. W. Long. 5. 33. N. Lat. 36. 35.
