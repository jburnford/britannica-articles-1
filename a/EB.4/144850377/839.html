LIVONIA

LIVONIA, a large province of the Russian empire, with the title of a duchy. It is bounded on the north by the gulph of Finland, on the west by that of Riga, on the south by Courland, and on the east, partly by Plescow, and partly by Novogorod. It is about 250 miles from north to south, and 150 from east to west. The land is so fertile in corn, that it is called the granary of the North; and would produce a great deal more, if it was not so full of lakes. The fish that abound here are salmon, carps, pikes, flat fish, and many others. In the forests there are wolves, bears, elks, rein-deer, stags, and hares. The domestic animals are very numerous; but the sheep bear very bad wool. Here are a great number of forests, which consist of birch-trees, pines, and oaks; and all the houses of the inhabitants are built with wood. The merchandizes which they send abroad are flax, hemp, honey, wax, leather, skins, and potashes.
