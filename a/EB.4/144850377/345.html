LAODICÆA

article · 267 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LAODICÆA on the Sea, (anc. geog.), according to Strabo, was a town of Seleucis in Syria, extremely well built, with a commodious harbour. The country about it yielded great quantities of wine. The city took its name from Laodice, mother of Seleucus the founder of it.
