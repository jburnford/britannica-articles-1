LEUCA

article · 276 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LEUCA, in antiquity, a geographical measure of length in use among the later Gauls; which according to Jornandes, who calls it Leuga, contained fifteen hundred paces, or one mile and a half. Hence the name of league, now reckoned at three miles. In the lower age called leuva.
