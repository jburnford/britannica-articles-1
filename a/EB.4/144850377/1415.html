MARLINE

article · 366 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MARLIN, in sea-affairs, are tarred white skains, or long wreaths or lines of untwisted hemp, dipped in pitch or tar, with which cables and other ropes are wrapped round, to prevent their fretting or rubbing in the blocks or pulleys through which they pass. The same serves in artillery upon ropes used for rigging guns, usually put up in small parcels called skains.
