MARTYNIA

article · 551 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MARTYNA, in botany, a genus of the angiospermia order, belonging to the didynamia class of plants. There are two species; both of them tender, herbaceous, flowery plants of South America; one of them an annual, the other a perennial, rising with erect stalks, from a foot, to two feet high, garnished with oblong simple leaves, and terminated by short spikes of large monopetalous, bell-shaped flowers, of blue and purple colours. They flower in July and August, and are very ornamental, but require always to be kept in the hottest part of the stove.
