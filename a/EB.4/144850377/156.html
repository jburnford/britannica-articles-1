KITCHEN, the room in a house where the provisions are cooked.

Army Kitchen, is a space of about 16 or 18 feet diameter, with a ditch surrounding it three feet wide; the opposite bank of which serves as a seat for the men who dress the victuals. The kitchens of the flank companies are contiguous to the outline of the camp; and the intermediate space is generally distributed equally

for the remaining kitchens; and as each tent forms a mess, each kitchen must have as many fire-places as there are tents in the company.
