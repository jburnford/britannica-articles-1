LAURENTIUM

LAURENTIUM, (anc. geog.), a town of Latium, supposed to be the royal residence of those most ancient kings Latinus, Picus, and Faunus, (Virgil). Hither the emperor Commodus retired, during a pestilence. Its name was from an adjoining grove of bay-trees, midway between Ostia and Antium. Supposed to have stood in the place now called San Lorenzo; which seems to be confirmed from the Via Laurentina leading to Rome.
