MARSHALLING a COAT

sub_entry · 234 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: HERALD

MARSHALLING a COAT, in heraldry, is the disposal of several coats of arms belonging to distinct families in one and the same escutcheon or shield, together with their ornaments, parts, and appurtenances. See HERALD, chap. vi. p. 3610.
