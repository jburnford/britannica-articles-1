LAY-BROTHERS

article · 373 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LAY-BROTHERS, among the Romanists, those pious but illiterate persons, who devote themselves in some convent to the service of the religious. They wear a different habit from that of the religious; but never enter into the choir, nor are present at the chapters; nor do they make any other vow except of constancy and obedience. In the nunneries there are also lay-sisters.
