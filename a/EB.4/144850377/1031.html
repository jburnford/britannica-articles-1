LUSTRUM

article · 460 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LUSTRUM, in Roman antiquity, a general muster and review of all the citizens and their goods, which was performed by the censors every fifth year, who afterwards made a solemn lustration. See the article LUSTRATION.

This custom was first instituted by Servius Tullius, about 180 years after the foundation of Rome. In course of time the lustrum were not celebrated so often, for we find the fifth lustrum celebrated at Rome only in the 574th year of that city.
