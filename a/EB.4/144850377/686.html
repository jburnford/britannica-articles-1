LICENTIATE

LICENTIATE, one who has obtained the degree of a licence.—The greatest number of the officers of justice in Spain are distinguished by no other title than that of licentiate. In order to pass licentiate in common law, civil law, and physic, they must have studied seven years, and in divinity 10. Among us a licentiate usually means a physician who has a licence to practise, granted by the college of physicians.
