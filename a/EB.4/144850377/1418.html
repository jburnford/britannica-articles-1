MARLY, a palace belonging to the king of France, between Versailles and St Germain; seated in a valley, near a village and forest of the same name. It is noted for its fine gardens and water-works, there being a curious machine on the river Seine, which not only supplies them with water, but also those of Versailles. It is 10 miles N. W. of Paris. E. Long. 2. 11. N. Lat. 48. 52.
