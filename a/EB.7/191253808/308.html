PERFECT NUMBER

article · 586 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PERFECT NUMBER, is one that is equal to the sum of all its aliquot parts when added together. Eucl. lib. 7, def. 22. As the number 6, which is 1 + 2 + 3, the sum of all its aliquot parts; also 28, for 28 = 1 + 2 + 4 + 7 + 14, the sum of all its aliquot parts. It is proved by Euclid, in the last prop. of book the 9th, that if the common geometrical series of numbers 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, &c. be continued to such a number of terms, as that the sum of the said series of terms shall be a prime number, then the product of this sum by the last term of the series will be a perfect number.
