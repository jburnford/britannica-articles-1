RING

article · 608 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

RING, in astronomy and navigation, an instrument used for taking the sun's altitude, &c. It is usually of brass, about nine inches diameter, suspended by a little swivel, at the distance of 45° from the point of which is a perforation, which is the centre of a quadrant of 90° divided in the inner concave surface. To use it, let it be held up by the swivel, and turned round to the sun, till his rays, falling through the hole, make a spot among the degrees, which marks the altitude required. This instrument is preferred before the astrolabe, because the divisions are here larger than on that instrument.
