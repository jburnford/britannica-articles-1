PRISM

article · 315 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PRISM, in geometry, is a body or a solid, whose two ends are any plane figures which are parallel, equal, and similar; and its sides, connecting those ends, are parallelograms. The definition of this figure in the Encyclopaedia we must, in candour, acknowledge to be unaccountably indistinct, if not unintelligible.
