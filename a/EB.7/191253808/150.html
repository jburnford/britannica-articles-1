MOERIS

article · 1,036 chars · page image at NLS ↗

MOERIS, a lake in Egypt, occasionally mentioned in that article (Encycl.), and generally supposed the production of human art. Of this, however, Mr Brown says it bears no mark. "The shape, as far as was distinguishable, seems not inaccurately laid down in D'Anville's map, unless it be, that the end nearest the Nile should run more in a north-west and south-east direction. The length may probably be between 30 and 40 miles; the breadth, at the widest part he could gain, was 5000 toises, as taken with a sextant; that is, near-

by six miles. The utmost possible extent of circuit must of course be 30 leagues. On the north-east and south is a rocky ridge, in every appearance primeval. In short, nothing can present an appearance more unlike the works of men. Several fishermen, in miserable boats, are constantly employed on the lake. The water is brackish, like most bodies of water under the same circumstances. It is, in the language of the country, Birket-el-kerun, probably from its extremities bearing some resemblance to horns.
