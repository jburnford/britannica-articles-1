VARENUS (Bernard), a learned Dutch geographer and physician of the 17th century, who was author of the best mathematical treatise on geography, entitled, Geographia Universalis, in qua affectiones generalis Terrarum

Variable ris explicantur. This excellent work has been translated into all languages, and was honoured by an edition, with improvements, by Sir Isaac Newton, for the use of his academical students at Cambridge.
