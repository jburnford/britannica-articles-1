OSCULATION

article · 309 chars · page image at NLS ↗

OSCULATION also means the point of concurrence of two branches of a curve which touch each other. For example, if the equation of a curve be y = \sqrt{x} + \sqrt{x}, it is easy to see that the curve has two branches touching one another at the point where x = 0, because the roots have each the signs + and -.
