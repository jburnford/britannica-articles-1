CUBIC HYPERBOLA

article · 398 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CUBIC HYPERBOLA, is a figure expressed by the equation x^2 = a, having two asymptotes, and consisting of two hyperbolas, lying in the adjoining angles of the asymptotes, and not in the opposite angles, like the Apollonian hyperbola; being otherwise called by New-

ton, in his Enumeratio Linearum Tertii Ordinis, an hyperbolismus of a parabola; and is the 65th species of those lines according to him.
